Weapons・Shields

Increasing Max Synthesis Slots

Max synthesis slots increases by 1 if you use a Blank Scroll as a secondary item in synthesis.
The max number of slots is 16 slots, and Mijinha and Sacred Shield are the only items that innately have 16 slots.

Transferring Upgrade Value Only

You can transfer upgrade value without transferring seals by using equipment with filled slots as a base in synthesis.
So don't discard items like Disposable Shield+2, but instead wait until your main shield's synthesis slots are filled.

Synthesizing Without Transferring Curses

Curses are treated as seals in Shiren 2 (呪).
Therefore you can synthesize without transferring a curse if you use equipment with filled slots as a base.
On a related note, Straw Voodoll family monsters won't be able to curse any equipment that has filled slots.

Remove Curses Using Straw Voodoll Monsters

Equipment will be purified when you take control of a Straw Voodoll family monster using a Possession Staff.

  • Straw Voodoll
    • Equipped shield.
  • Nailed Voodoll
    • Equipped weapon, shield, and bracelets.
  • Spiked Voodoll
    • All equipment in your inventory.

Long-Lasting Dried Bonito

If you synthesize a Dried Bonito onto a Disposable Sword, the [Bite] command will be added to the Disposable Sword.
Selecting [Bite] restores 30% fullness at the cost of lowering weapon strength by 1,
and the weapon can no longer be bitten once its strength reaches 0.

So a Dried Bonito can be bitten 4 times (120%), and a Disposable Sword can be bitten 35 times (1050%).
You can also transfer a Disposable Sword-35 with the か seal onto a weapon with low attack like Pickaxe or Club,
and then synthesize that onto a new Disposable Sword to get another 30+ bites.

Lastly, Kaburasutegi+99 with the か seal can be bitten 198 times, making it the longest lasting food source in the game.

Gamble Synthesis

If you find yourself running out of inventory space due to unidentified grasses but don't want to eat them in case
one of them is an ingredient for Healing Sword / 弟 seal for your shield, you could give gamble synthesis a try.

The idea is to attempt a Mixer synthesis using a spare weapon or shield such as Nagamaki or Tribal Shield
to see if key seals like 薬, 弟, or 命 get added to the equipment, preventing the first use of the grass going to waste.
Be sure to assign a name to the grass before throwing it, so that you can note what it was if the synthesis succeeds.

However, keep in mind that extra seals like ち or 幸 could get synthesized, resulting in a waste of a Strength Grass,
or possibly running out of synthesis slots when attempting to make a Healing Sword.

Whether a perosn uses this technique or not mostly comes down to play style.

Checking if a Mixer is a Copycat Mask

Mixergon overlaps with Trickster and Imposter Mask monsters between 50~80F inside the "Road to the End" dungeon,
so it's important to be able to determine if a Mixergon is real before throwing your equipment to avoid losing it.
(Mixer and Mixermon never overlaps with Copycat Mask family monsters).

The easiest method would be to shoot an arrow at the Mixergon. If it eats the arrow, it's real, and if it deals damage, it's fake.
The downside of this method is that it decreases the number of items the Mixergon can eat for synthesis to 3 items.

If you swing a Knowledge Staff and the description reads "Looks like Mixergon, but something isn't quite right.", it's fake.
It's one of the few practical uses for a Knowledge Staff, so if you happen to have one on hand, go ahead and use it for this.

Copycat Mask monsters have lower attack than Mixergon, so another way to tell would be to intentially get hit by an attack.
Mixergon has 48 attack before eating items, while Trickster Mask has 15 attack, and Imposter Mask has 20 attack.

Also, Copycat Mask monsters will always be generated awake (excluding Monster Houses),
so sleeping Mixergons are always real outside of Monster Houses.

Synthesizing Silver Arrows onto Equipment

Synthesizing a Silver Arrow onto a weapon or shield grants the 銀 seal,
which makes it so that the weapon or shield pierces through walls and objects when thrown.
The 銀 seal on a weapon also lets you hit monsters inside walls with regular attacks. (ex: Pumphantasm).

Mixer synthesis must be used since a Silver Arrow isn't a weapon or shield category item,
but due to its properties of piercing through objects, Mixers can't eat Silver Arrows.
However, possessed Mixers can eat items they step on, so you can synthesize this seal by using a Possession Staff.
Simply place the equipment and Silver Arrows on the ground, and step on them in the usual order.
Then either let another monster defeat the possessed Mixer, or return to your body and defeat the Mixer yourself.

Note that once a weapon or shield has the 銀 seal, all future Mixer synthesis will have to be done using this method.
The risk of losing the equipment due to an accidental throw is far greater than the advantage of hiting monsters inside walls,
so this synthesizing this seal onto a main weapon isn't recommended.

Knowledge Related to Ironhead's Head

Ironhead's Head cannot normally hit monsters as shown below, unless the 三 seal is synthesized.

            □□□□M　　S＝Shiren
            □□□□□　　M＝Monster
            □□□□□　　■＝Wall
            ■S■■■

Ironhead's Head will pass through monsters that evade the attack and hit the monster behind them.
If monster 1 evades the attack, monster 2 will get hit. And if both 1 and 2 evade the attack, monster 3 will get hit.

            ■■■■
            S123
            ■■■■

Ironhead's Head cannot pierce through multiple targets like a Spear can, even if the 銀 seal is synthesized.
If the ケ seal (Kengo's Katana) is synthesized, the attack can only fling a weapon if the monster is adjacent to Shiren.
If the 三 seal (Razor Wind) is synthesized, the attack will change to 3-way pattern from where Shiren is currently standing.
(As opposed to a 3-way pattern beginning 3 tiles ahead).

Thrown Equipment Damage

Thrown weapons deal damage based on the weapon's attack stat, Shiren's level, and Shiren's strength.
On the other hand, shields will always deal damage equal to the shield's defense stat. (1 damage minimum).
It's good to keep this in mind when hunting Cave Mamels early on inside the "Road to the End" dungeon.

Bracelets

Using Scout Bracelet to Determine Map / Monsters

Being attentive to the map when a Scout Bracelet is equipped helps lower the risk of ending up in a bad situation.

6~9 items in a group, 1~2 red dots that don't move.
Almost always a dungeon shop. The number of items for sale varies by the size of the shop.
It could also be an island with items.
Lots of items, no red dots nearby.
Island with items, or a treasure room.
Lots of monsters in a group, no movement.
Almost always a Monster House. Be extra careful if you see it's a Special Monster House when you walk near it.
Monster moving at half speed.
Likely a Snacky.
Monster moving at double speed.
Any monster in the current floor's monster table that has the ability to move at double speed.
If it moves toward an item and the blue dot vanishes, it's likely a Water Strider.
Monster moving in a random / unpredictable pattern.
Pumphantasm family monster.
Red dot turned into a blue dot on the next turn.
N'dubba was generated.
Monster that moved to an item and then stopped.
Bored Kappa or Gamarra family monster.
Monster that runs away from Shiren.
Mr. Drone family, Gamarra or Zalokleft family after stealing from Shiren, or Spearfish / Ocran family monster with low HP.
Red dot that moves from one item to the next.
Trader NPC.
Moving toward Shiren at triple speed, passing through walls.
Typhoon.
