Basic Techniques

This page introduces some of the common gameplay techniques to help you on your adventures.
See the Gameplay Basics page for explanations regarding gameplay systems and mechanics.

Have you ever been in a situation where you wondered, "What should I do here?"
Perhaps you ended up in such a situation due to your own inexperience or carelessness,
but even situations that appear to be hopeless at first glance often have solutions.
Your adventures will be more enjoyable if you overcome such challenges, rather than simply giving up.

Basics

Diagonal Movement

Diagonal movement is one of the simplest but most important techniques in the game.
It might take some practice, but it will help you navigate around monsters, and save on turns in general.

Lure Enemies

If you encounter an enemy in a room or hallway with a 1 tile gap:
＿＿＿＿＿＿＿＿＿
[Shiren] [　] [Enemy]
￣￣￣￣￣￣￣￣￣
Instead of moving toward the enemy, swing your weapon so that the enemy moves toward you:
＿＿＿＿＿＿＿＿＿
[Shiren] [Enemy]←
￣￣￣￣￣￣￣￣￣
That way you'll avoid giving the first hit to the enemy.

Fight in Hallways

If there are multiple monsters in the room, move into a hallway and fight them one at a time.
Getting surrounded means you'll end up taking damage from multiple attacks each turn.
Shiren can normally only attack one monster at a time, so nothing good will come from
trying to take on multiple enemies at the same time.

Step in Place to Recover HP

You can step in place and fast-forward turns by holding A+B.
This will let you quickly recover HP without moving, and should be done
before proceeding to other rooms if your HP is low.
However, you'll want to keep an eye on your fullness if you use this technique very often.

Use Items in a Pinch

If you collapse in a dungeon, you'll lose all of your items.
So if you find yourself in a pinch, don't hesitate to use items.

  • Herb or Otogiriso to restore HP.
  • Swift Grass if you want a movement advantage.
  • Warp Grass if you need a quick escape.
  • Decoy Scroll if you want to avoid taking damage.
  • Arrows or Cannonballs if you want to defeat enemies from a distance.
And so on - make use of your items depending on your current needs.

Riceballs

Keep them in Storage Pots

Riceballs will not spoil over time, but can spoil from outside factors such as stepping on a Spoil Trap.
However, they can be protected from spoiling by simply placing them inside pots.

It might not seem like a big deal during the Shuten Trails since you can buy new riceballs at Shuten Halfway,
but lowering the number of turns/actions you can perform before starving is a pretty significant hit.
It's best to get in the habit of placing riceballs inside Storage Pots now,
so that you'll have an easier time once you reach the later dungeons.

When to Eat

Ideally, you'll want to eat riceballs when none of the restored fullness goes to waste.
For example, if you eat a Large Riceball (restores 100%) at 20/100% fullness, 20% fullness is wasted.
So instead, it's best to eat the Large Riceball the moment your fullness reaches 0%.

Food is extremely valuable, since it increases the number of turns / actions you can perform before starving.
Remember, wait until none of the fullness goes to waste before eating, especially during early game.
Eating grass items also restores 5% fullness, so it's best to eat them when you're not at max fullness.

Eating Order

There are 5 types of riceballs in Shiren 2, and some should be prioritized over others.

When you don't have a Storage Pot, or have few riceballs:
  • Giant Riceball
  • Large Riceball
  • Riceball
  • Specialty Riceball
  • Spoiled Riceball

The scariest thing about not having riceballs inside Storage Pots is stepping on a Spoil or Flood trap.
No matter how good the riceball is, it'll be reduced to a Spoiled Riceball if you step on those traps.
Therefore, you should prioritize eating the best riceballs first to lower the risk of them going to waste.

When you have a Storage Pot:
  • Giant Riceball
  • Specialty Riceball
  • Riceball
  • Large Riceball
  • Spoiled Riceball

Giant Riceball should still be prioritized because it increases max fullness by 7%.
It's common to end up with multiple riceballs over time when you have a Storage Pot,
so the idea is to prioritize eating riceballs with low fullness restoration to free up inventory space.

Level Up by Defeating Strong Enemies

Eating Happy Grass increases level by 1, but it can also be thrown at a monster to level them up.
You can utilize this to create a strong enemy, and then defeat them for a large amount of experience points,
with the end result of gaining multiple level ups as opposed to just one from eating the Happy Grass.

Happy Staff can also be used to level up an enemy monster.
Snacky can spawn on early floors in dungeons like Road to the End,
possibly resulting in a monster defeating it and leveling up.
Leveling up a Pit Mamel into a Cave Mamel early on is highly recommended.

Of course, higher level enemies are much stronger, so you have to be careful with how you defeat them.
There are many ways to defeat high level enemies, but here are some ideas:

  • Deal lots of damage in a single hit by throwing Gitan or using Flame Grass, Dragon Grass, etc.
  • Slow the enemy or eat Swift Grass, and then loop attack → step back → attack → step back.
  • Step on a Poison Trap so that the Poison Arrow hits the monster, lowering their strength to 0.

Synthesize Otogiriso for an Easier Time

Synthesizing Otogiriso onto a shield or weapon lets Shiren restore HP when he receives or deals damage.
One 弟 seal restores 4 HP, and the amount of HP restored can be increased by stacking seals.

For example, if you have 5 弟 seals on a shield, 15 HP will be restored every time you get hit by an attack.
So, stacking 5 弟 seals pretty much nullifies any attack that deals 15 or less damage!
The HP restoration also takes effect when you're hit by ranged attacks like Dragon fire.
Furthermore, whereas the HP restoration only takes effect once per turn with weapons (1 attack per turn),
there's no limit for shields - you'll restore HP after each and every hit you take on that turn.

As you can see, 弟 on a shield is easily one of the best seals in the game to help with consistency.
Prioritize synthesizing it onto your shield once you reach Shuten Trail Hard.

Lastly, if you obtain a Phantasm Bracelet in one of the post-game dungeons, it can be a good idea
to synthesize 弟 seals onto your weapon so that you can stay inside walls longer too.

How to Handle Curses

Equipment found on the ground can sometimes be cursed. (呪 seal).
The curse doesn't affect the equipment's strength or abilities, but you won't be able to unequip the item.

In Shiren 2, only weapons, shields, and bracelets can be cursed.
And to reiterate, the only effect of a curse is that it prevents the item from being unequipped.
Other item categories cannot be cursed, and upgrade values and abilities are not affected.

So while it might sound mild compared to other games, cursed equipment can still be troublesome.
It prevents swapping equipment, synthesizing, and unequipping bracelets to keep them from shattering.

Curse Related Techniques

Remove the curse
Some ideas include...
  • Read a Purify Scroll to remove curses from all equipped items.
    • It only removes curses from equipped items, so equip cursed items before reading the scroll.
  • Synthesize in a way that avoids the transfer of the 呪 seal to the base item.
    • Curses are synthesis seals in Shiren 2. (呪 seal).
    • As long as the base item has filled seals, cursed equipment can be synthesized without the curse transferring.
    • However, since cursed equipment comes with -1 upgrade value, the base item will become slightly weaker.
  • Take control of a Straw Voodoll family monster using a Possession Staff.
    　What gets purified depends on the level of the monster:
    　Straw Voodoll → equipped shields.
    　Nailed Voodoll → all equipped items.
    　Spiked Voodoll → all equipment in your inventory, exluding items in pots.
    • In other words, if your items get cursed by a Straw Voodoll family monster,
      you can immediately undo the curse by taking control of them using a Possession Staff.
  • Let Gyandora pluck the cursed equipment. (It can also pluck other equipment in your inventory).
    • Gyandora can pluck your equipped items, including those that are cursed.
    • The random seal erasing effect could erase the 呪 seal if you're lucky.
    • However, note that the number of seal slots will also decrease by 1.
    • Monsoon Doll won't erase the 呪 seal, so they cannot be used to remove curses.
  • Have the Blacksmith erase the 呪 seal at Shuten Halfway. (Weapon or shield only).
    • This only works for cursed items that are not currently equipped.

Note that Heaven Scroll, Earth Scroll, and Plating Scroll will not remove curses in Shiren 2.

Unequip the cursed item
It's possible to unequip cursed items without removing the curse.
  • Intentionally step on a Strip Trap.
    • If you discover this trap, you can check all of your equipment for curses.
    • Ensure your surroundings are safe before stepping on it.
  • Let a Kengo family monster disarm you.
    • Make sure your back is facing a wall.
    • Kengo = shields, Iai = swords and shields, and Shihan = swords, shields, bracelets.
    • Iai and Shihan have high attack, so you'll want to pour water on them from a Water Pot.
    • You can also disarm Shiren yourself by taking control of a Kengo family monster.
  • Break or shatter the equipment.
    • Intentionally take damage from enemy attacks to shatter bracelets.
      • Weaken a weapon element monster using a Water Pot, or equip a shield with 弟 seals.
    • Break a Pickaxe by digging walls.
    • Though keep in mind, the equipment will be lost when it breaks.
    • Shields cannot be broken.
  • Let an Iron Zalokleft steal from you.
    • Iron Zalokleft is rather rare, so this might not be practical.
  • Return to Napus Village.
    • All items will be unequipped when you wake up, including cursed equipment.

As you can see, there are multiple ways to unequip cursed items without removing the curse.

What about Asuka's equipment?
The only way to remove curses from Asuka's equipment is for Shiren to read a Purify Scroll while standing next to her.
You could try to utilize a Strip Trap or Kengo family monster to unequip her, but it can be a little inconsistent.
If all else fails, you could intentionally have her collapse so that she drops her equipment.

Making Bracelets Last

Bracelets can shatter in Shiren 2, and there is no way to make them unbreakable.

All bracelets eventually crack and shatter from enemy attacks,
but this section aims to examine ways to prevent the inevitable for as long as possible.
See the Gameplay Basics page for details on durability values / crack and shatter conditions.

When to Equip / Unequip

Unequip when fighting enemies
This is one of the key rules. You might get away with not unequipping bracelets a few times,
but keeping them equipped during fights will guarantee that they crack sooner than later.
Other actions can cause bracelets to crack, but monster regular attacks are the most common cause.

For valuable bracelets like Scout Bracelet, you should unequip them even at the risk of receiving damage.
It's also good to get in the habit of keeping Pierce Bracelet unequipped while not in use,
so that you don't accidentally lose equipment when throwing items at Mixer or Synthesis Pots at walls.

That said, there are exceptions to this rule.
Bracelets that help evade attacks and effects (ex: Salty, Parry, Paper Thin) or boost offensive power (ex: Strength)
should be treated as consumable items and worn when needed during combat.

※ How to unequip 2 bracelets at the same time
If you have 2 valuable bracelets equipped and are about to receive an attack on the next turn,
you can insert both bracelets into a Storage Pot to unequip both at the same time.
This only works if you have a Storage Pot with 2 or more free slots, but can be good to remember.
Unequip when walking through rooms
This is to prevent bracelets from receiving damage due to traps like Trip Stone, Landmine, Rockfall, etc.
Explosions from landmines, bombs, and cannonballs have a greater chance to crack bracelets.
They shouldn't break from these damage sources, but a crack signals the countdown to shattering,
so if you're the type to skip checking for traps, keep this in mind.
Unequip when advancing to the next floor
There's no way to tell what's waiting for you at the beginning of the next floor,
so it's best to unequip bracelets beforehand in case you start in the middle of a Monster House, etc.
This is especially the case in deeper floors where Typhoon can shatter bracelets in the blink of an eye.

Dangerous Monsters

Ironhead Family
Its head is a ranged attack, but unlike arrows, it's treated as a physical attack so bracelets will take damage.
Taurus Family
Brutal hits seem to have a higher chance to crack bracelets.
They're difficult to one-shot thanks to their high HP, so bracelets are likely to take damage from counter attacks.
Pop Tank, Cranky Tank
They fire cannonballs from a distance even if you're not directly lined up with them,
often causing bracelets to crack before you have a chance to unequip them if you're caught off guard.
Cranky Tank is troublesome in particular because it can fire cannonballs and move at double speed.
Fast Monsters (ex: Gale Family), Nashaaga and Lashaaga
Getting hit by 2~3 attacks per turn instead of 1 means bracelets can shatter that much faster.
Wolf Droid Family
Gorilla Bot and Bear Borg can toss bombs from greater distances.
However, unlike Porky family monsters, they only toss bombs if Shiren is within view.
N'dubba
They get a surprise attack in when they reveal themselves, making it easy for bracelets to take damage.
N'dubba 60yr ~ 77yr moves at double speed, so it's best to unequip bracelets before picking up items.
If that's too much work, at the very least unequip bracelets when you check merchandise inside shops.
Nailed Voodoll, Spiked Voodoll
If your equipped bracelets get cursed, you won't be able to unequip them when fighting monsters.
Curses are not treated as seals for bracelets, (bracelets don't have seals to begin with),
so unlike weapons and shields, there's no way to protect bracelets through synthesis.

It's possible to unequip bracelets before Nailed Voodoll has a chance to curse equipped bracelets,
but Spiked Voodoll will curse all equipment in your inventory, even if they're not equipped.
If you're up against a Spiked Voodoll, you can place the bracelet on the ground or in a Storage Pot.
You can also protect bracelets from curses by synthesizing the 祓 seal (Holy Shield, Purify Scroll) onto your shield.

If you still have trouble...

If you still frequently end up with cracked and shattered bracelets despite your best efforts,
consider stocking up on either additional copies of the bracelet, or Plating Scrolls to mend cracked bracelets.

For example, if you have a stock of Scout Bracelets or Phantasm Bracelets inside a Storage Pot,
you can swap one out when it cracks and repair it at Shuten Halfway / read a Plating Scroll to mend it later.
Scout Bracelets can be found in Shuten Trail Hard, so stocking up on them shouldn't be too difficult.

You could also synthesize the 見 seal (Evasive Shield) or equip a Paper Thin Bracelet to increase evasion,
which will help bracelets last longer thanks to enemy attacks missing more often.

Reclaim Items that fell in Water

Have Himakichi retrieve items
Himakichi will throw any underwater items he sees over to Shiren,
so as long as the item is visible to Himakichi, you can obtain them just by bringing him along.
Evaporate Scroll
The simplest and quickest method - all you have to do is read the Evaporate Scroll.
Once the water evaporates, walk over to the item and pick it up.
※ Only works in Random Dungeon maps.
Freeze Scroll
Similar to Evaporate Scroll, all you have to do is read the Freeze Scroll.
Items that were underwater will be moved on top of the ice, so walk over to the item and pick it up.
※ Only works in Random Dungeon maps.
Wall Collapse Scroll
Like the above two scrolls, all you have to do is read the Wall Collapse Scroll.
The entire floor will be turned into one large room with regular ground tiles, so walk over to the item and pick it up.
※ Only works in Random Dungeon maps.
Specific Monster Pots + Possession Staff
You can collect underwater items if you take control of monsters that can pick up or steal items.
This includes monsters like Water Strider, Bored Kappa family, and Zalokleft family.
Water Strider drops its item when it returns to its pot, so you can collect items without a Possession Staff.
However, it might throw the item at a nearby enemy, so be sure to clear the room beforehand.
With Bored Kappa, simply move on top of the item and throw it toward dry ground.

Zalokleft will drop the item it stole when it returns to its pot,
but it's trickier to use than the other two because it warps away when it steals an item.
It can take a long time to return to where you were, and Zalokleft is easily defeated by enemy monsters.
Note that you can use a Possession Staff on enemy versions of these monsters too,
so you don't have to have the Monster Pots with you if these monsters appear in the monster table.
Sanctaruy Scroll
This is likely more trouble than it's worth, but it's one of the few uses for a Sanctaruy Scroll.
Place it on the ground so that items hop around, and then hope the item eventually lands on dry ground.
Once the item is on ground, you can pour water from a Water Pot onto the Sanctaruy Scroll to disable the effect.
This method is unreliable if the item is far from land, or if there are many underwater items.
Dosukoi Family Monster
This method is almost never used because there has to be both water and Dosukoi monsters on the current floor.
The idea is the same as Sanctaruy Scroll, where you hope Dosukoi's special attack shifts the item onto dry ground.
Dosukoi will only stamp the ground if Shiren is not in the same room,
and Hakkeyoi cannot shift items around with its special attack.
Use items like Unlucky Staff or Happy Staff to adjust their levels as needed.

※ Shiren cannot pick up underwater items using a Phantasm Bracelet.

Collecting Island Items in Dungeons

Sometimes small islands (ground surrounded by water) get generated inside rooms.
The island always consists of 1 Spring and items or Gitan on the remaining ground tiles.
Islands only appear in Random Dungeons, and serves as a bonus chance to collect items.

The water can occasionally be frozen, in which case you can walk across and collect items without issue.

Standard Methods

Scrolls
  • Read an Evaporate, Freeze, or Wall Collapse scroll.
  • Place a Sanctaruy Scroll on the ground and wait for the items to hop across the water.
  • Write Evaporate, Freeze, or Wall Collapse on a Blank Scroll... but there are better uses for Blank Scroll.
Phantasm Bracelet
Simply walk over the waterway without having to use any other items.

Other Methods

If a monster is in the middle
Swing or throw a Swap Staff at the monster, and step on the Spring to return.
Take advantage of enemies that can fly over water by moving in a way where they make it onto the island.
You can also use a Knockback Staff (deals 7 tiles of knockback) to force a monster onto the island,
though you may need to use a Pickaxe or Tunnel Staff to make a line long enough to do so.
Knockback Statue
Get hit by an enemy's regular attack from 5 tiles away so that you land on the island.
Spring
Extremely low chance to land on the island. Often more trouble than it's worth, and just wastes time.
However, you do feel pretty clever when it actually works out.
Log Trap
If there happens to be one in a location where getting hit by the log pushes you onto the island.
Flood Trap
Items will be scattered randomly throughout the current floor after the flood, including island items.
Note that all items that are not inside pots will get wet, including scrolls and riceballs.
Pull Staff
Swing a Pull Staff at the island items to pull them toward you.
Recommended to prioritize pulling items rather than Gitan.
※ Leave a 1 tile gap between the item and yourself so that the item doesn't land in water.
Knockback Staff
Swing a knockback staff at the island items to knock them off of the island and onto land.
※ Pots will shatter when knockback is dealt to them. Also, check for traps beforehand where the item will land.
Armordillo Tackle
Get sent over to the island by an Armordillo family monster's tackle, and return by stepping on the Spring.
Carry a Balance Staff or place items on the ground prior to getting hit by the tackle so that you don't
drop items in the waterway after you land on the island.
※ Don't leave items on the ground if a Trader is present, or if you're concerned about stepping on a Pitfall.

How to use the Storage

60 items can be deposited through the attendant, and 35 items can be placed on the floor. (95 items total).
This might seem like a lot, but once you clear Onigashima and reach post-game,
you'll likely run out of storage space quickly if you try to store everything.

Items inside pots will vanish when they are left in storage, and the pot's size will not be returned to normal.
This can make it a bit of a chore to manage items when entering dungeons that don't allow carry-in items.
So this section aims to offer tips and techniques to make the most of the available storage space.

Take items out of pots
As mentioned above, items inside pots will vanish when left in storage, so take out all items before depositing them.
Pour out all water from Water Pots before leaving them in storage as well.
Deposit important items
Talk to the man on the right inside the storage for details why this should be done.
Such items include strengthened equipment, rare bracelets and grasses, staves with lots of uses, etc.
Place pots on the floor
Being able to pick up commonly used items like Storage Pots saves time when heading to dungeons.
Synthesize staves together
Combining same name staves adds the number of uses together, and also saves on storage space.
Synthesize items you plan on synthesizing
If you have secondary items that you're planning on synthesizing later...
Ex: You don't have a Manji-Kabura, but have Crescent Arm, Flame Grass, Fish Spear, Mallet, and Golden Sword.
Instead of keeping all 5 items in storage, synthesize the other four seals onto Crescent Arm (火水木金).
Then you can synthesize that onto a Manji-Kabura in the correct seal order, while cutting down on storage space.
Deposit traps
It's possible to deposit traps obtained in Halfway Well or through the use of a Trapper Bracelet.
You could set a Landmine down and use it to destroy unwanted items, or a Strip trap to unequip cursed items.
Pitfalls are nice when searching for Perfect Wood in Shuten Trail Mid or Dragon Tile Bracelets in Onigashima.
However, if you set a trap down inside the storage, you'll need a Trapper Bracelet to pick it back up.
Be careful with Surprise Pots
Surprise Pots can contain monsters or pent-up anger, making them risky to throw inside the storage.
If you do decide to throw one, make sure you won't destroy important items in the event it explodes,
and be ready to exit the storage in the event monsters are inside. (They'll be gone when you re-enter).
※ Mixer synthesis inside the storage
If you register a Mixer with Monster Kingdom, you can synthesize inside the storage as much as you want.
You'll want to leave an open spot on the floor so that the synthesized item has somewhere to land.
It's not possible to attack ally monsters directly, so another means to defeat the Mixer is needed.
You could use a weapon with the 火 seal to chip away at it, but the fastest method is to make use of a Landmine.
Place the Landmine in a corner, and trigger it by shooting an arrow when Mixer is next to it.
The downside of this method is that you won't be able to leave items near the Landmine.

Example placement:

            ■■■■
            □ML■
            □M□■　　M = Mixer
            □□□■　　L = Landmine
            □□□■　　S = Shiren
            □SA■　　A = Arrow or Iron Arrow trap
            ■■■■

Shoot an arrow to the right so that the Arrow Trap activates when Mixer is standing next to the Landmine.

Another method is to surround a Trip Stone with Sanctuary Scrolls in a corner,
and then throw an item at Mixer after synthesizing while a Dragon Tile Bracelet is equipped.
Mixer will trip and drop the item, so return it to its pot and send it out again to synthesize
without having to go all the way back to Monster Kingdom each time.

Good Items to Store

Revival Grass
Fantastic item that not only revives you when you collapse, but also restores fullness and strength.
You don't need as many once you have a weapon and shield with +99 upgrade value,
but it's still a good idea to carry 3 Revival Grass when entering Sealed Room.
Recommended to keep 10 or so inside the storage at all times.
Wall Collapse Scroll
Stockpiled when searching for Golden Intervals.
Go ahead and use them up whenever you feel ready to visit the Golden Interval of your choice.
Escape Scroll
Nice for emergencies, or if you're finished with whatever goal you had in mind when entering a dungeon.
1~2 is probably enough, but if you want to stockpile more, you can loop 99F of Sealed Room.
Blank Scroll
You can never have too many Blank Scrolls. You might not need other scrolls if you have lots of these.
Restock Scroll
Stockpiled when searching for Dragon Tile Bracelets in Onigashima.
No reason to have them in storage once you've obtained the number of bracelets you needed.
(7 bracelets = 1 Daisangen Sword + 1 Daisangen Shield + 1 to equip when desired).
Sanctuary Scroll
Nice to have on the floor inside the storage in case monsters appear from a Surprise Pot.
Place 3 on the ground in a corner to make it easier to synthesize inside the storage using a Mixer.
Can be used alongside Perfect Iron Sand to put on a monster battle royale for fun.
Sanctuary Scrolls that are stuck to the ground will be reverted to being rolled up when you re-enter the storage.

Suction Scroll
Stockpiled when strengthening equipment using an Upgrade Pot,
or when hunting for rare items using Presto Pot inside Sealed Room.
Identify Scroll
Nice when you want to identify pots or staves inside new dungeons. 2 or so should be enough.
Purify Scroll
It's important to be able to remove curses when checking for equipment upgrade values. 3 should be enough.
Staves
Staves can be used multiple times, as opposed to only once like grasses and scrolls.
Keep the helpful ones, and synthesize same name staves together to reduce the amount of space they take up.
Storage Pot
Expanding your inventory is great, though it's not as necessary once you have strong equipment.
Keep 11 Storage Pots expanded to a size of 8, since you can carry-in a max of 11 items into dungeons.
Strength Bracelet
Stockpiled for Asuka. It's ideal to have her equip 2 Strength Bracelets at all times.
Of course, there's no need to keep them in storage if you prefer going solo.

Combat Techniques

This section covers tips against specific monsters, or monsters with similar characteristics as the ones listed.
For monsters that aren't listed here, check the individual dungeon or monster pages for more information.
These techniques are pretty standard, so it's best to practice them until they become second nature.

The order of monsters is based on the order of encountering the monsters while playing the story dungeons.

Shuten Trail Easy

Best Way to defeat Droplet Ninja

Monsters that place traps on the ground when they're defeated can be problematic in hallways.
Stepping on a Droplet Trap isn't too bad, but a Landmine or Summon Trap is another story.
So it's good to get in the habit of having them follow you into a room before defeating them.
Also, they won't be able to place a trap if there is already an item or trap on the ground.

So you can also have them follow you in a way where they're positioned on top of an item or trap.
Shop entrances and stairs can block trap placements as well, so they can be utilized if present.
If you're sandwiched in a hallway, prioritize defeating the monster that won't place a trap on the ground first.
That said, if you find it to be too risky to retreat to a room, you can just defeat the trap placing monster.

Zalokleft stole my Item!

This is pretty common. Ideally you'd defeat the Zalokleft before it steals, but sometimes it's unavoidable.
Zaloklefts warp away after stealing an item, but the monster will always be somewhere on the current floor.
The stolen item can be retrieved by defeating the Zalokleft, so if the item was important, be sure to hunt it down.

Zalokleft will run away from Shiren after stealing, so you'll need to figure out a way to get close enough to attack it.
Most maps have a room or hallway that leads to a dead end, so you could chase it into one of those areas.
If the map doesn't have a dead end, you can wait in a hallway until it appears next to you.
This works because monsters can only see 1 tile ahead when inside hallways,
meaning the Zalokleft won't detect Shiren until it's right next to him.

Items can also be used to catch up to it (ex: Slow Staff), or to defeat it from a distance (ex: arrows, Lightning Scroll).
These strategies work for Gamarra and Mr. Drone family monsters as well,
but they can be trickier to chase down since most of them move at double speed.

How to fight Horse Ronin and Bow Boy

These monsters have a ranged attack, so you'll receive damage if you try to lure them closer by stepping in place.
They can only use their ranged attack if Shiren is lined up with them (up, down, left, right, diagonal).
So the idea is to move in a way that breaks the line, forcing them to step closer to try and line up again.
You'll be able to avoid their ranged attacks, and can attack them directly once they're next to you.
This strategy works for most monsters that have a ranged special attack, so it's good to make it a habit.

Also, note that such monsters can end up hitting other monsters when they use their ranged attacks.
This can lead to monsters like Horse Ronin leveling up, which can be quite a problem early on.
It's important to move in a way that prevents other monsters from standing between the ranged monster and Shiren,
even at the cost of taking an extra hit from an enemy - unless you want to intentionally level them up.

Shuten Trail Mid

How to fight Boy Cart

Boy Cart not only shoots arrows from a distance, but it also likes to keep a set distance between itself and Shiren.
However, it does have a weakness - once it enters a hallway, its field of view will be limited to 1 tile.
This means it will repeatedly move forward, see Shiren, and take a step back without attacking.
So by luring it into a hallway, you can defeat it without using items or taking any damage.
Boy Cart's Iron Arrows have high attack, so this is a valuable technique to remember.

However, Boy Cart will shoot an arrow if there is a wall or monster behind it, making it unable to retreat.

Luring Gale

If there are 2 open tiles between you and Gale, perform a regular attack to have it move next to you.
And if there is only 1 open tile, take a step back to lure it next to you without taking damage.
This technique works for all monsters that move at double speed. (But not triple speed).

Water Strider is a bit of an exception, since it will try to line up with Shiren like Boy Cart if it's carrying an item.

How to fight Kengo

Kengo can disarm the shield you have equipped, which at first might not sound too terrible.
However, when it disarms the shield, the shield will be knocked away and fly behind you.
The shield will be lost if it hits another character, flies outside of the map, or lands on a Landmine, etc.
In order to prevent permanently losing your shield, it's best to unequip it beforehand when fighting Kengo.

But what if you don't have the chance to unequip it, or what if you're about to lose the fight without equipping it?
In that case, keep the shield equipped, and move toward a wall so that your back is against the wall.
That way even if your shield is disarmed, it won't fly anywhere, and you can simply pick it up.
Kengo family monsters are the leading cause of equipment loss, so it's important to remember this.

Shuten Trail Hard

How to fight Wolf Droid

This can be said for all monsters that deal explosions with their special attacks like Wolf Droid and Pop Tank,
but it's common for these monsters to level up as a result of defeating other monsters that get caught in the blasts.
Unexpected level ups can be scary, so if there is a weakened monster nearby, prioritize defeating it first.
That said, you'll likely encounter leveled up monsters even when you're being careful,

Onigashima

How to fight Twisty Hani

Twisty Hani has high HP and defense despite appearing on early floors, so it's difficult to defeat it in a single hit.
Its dance will lower your level by 1, which can be devastating if you take a long time to defeat it,
so you'll need to come up with ways of defeating it without getting hit by its special attack.

The safest method is to equip a Level Lock Bracelet, which completely blocks the level down effect of the dance.
Only equip it when you're fighting Twisty Hani so that the bracelet lasts a long time.
Though some would argue it's not worth taking up an inventory slot for an item that only has one small use case.

The standard method is to weaken the Twisty Hani with an arrow, and then finish it off with a regular attack.
Wooden Arrows lack power, so it's best to use Iron Arrows instead. (or 2 Wooden Arrows).
Some other options include the 火 seal on a weapon, cannonballs, stones, staves to inflict status conditions, etc.
These can also be used against other monsters, so it's more efficient than keeping a Level Lock Bracelet on hand.

Twisty Hani is always generated asleep, and only wakes up when Shiren walks next to it,
so you can simply ignore them if you don't have the means to defeat them safely.
However, defeating a Twisty Hani provides 300 experience points, and they also have a high item drop rate,
so it's good to hunt them whenever you have the means to do so.

Checking for Copycat Mask

Copycat Mask monsters are always generated disguised as another monster selected from the current floor's table.
There's no way to determine if a monster is real or fake from their appearance.
This isn't an issue if you simply defeat the monsters you come across, but can be a problem when
you want to synthesize using Mixers, throw an Extinction Scroll at a troublesome monster, etc.

Here are some characteristics you can watch for:

  • Copycat Mask monsters are always generated awake (excluding Monster Houses), so sleeping monsters are all real.
  • An unusual message is displayed when a Knowledge Staff is swung at a disguised Copycat Mask monster.
  • The disguise will be canceled if the Copycat Monster is sealed. (Don't use this method to check for real Mixers).
  • The disguise will be canceled if the Copycat Monster hits you and you have a shield with the 識 seal (Identify Scroll).
  • Likely fake if its attack strength doesn't feel right, or if it never uses special attacks.
