Halfway Well

Dungeon you can enter after registering 30 or more monsters to Monster Kingdom.

Shiren won't trigger traps by stepping on them in this dungeon,
but he can instead pick them up and set them on the ground to trap monsters.
Once a trap is set, it cannot be picked up again.
Traps have a chance to break after being triggered by monsters.
Lastly, note that Shiren can set traps down without ending his turn.

Dungeon Overview

The unlock event occurs the next time you exit Mumyu after registering 30 or more monsters to Monster Kingdom.
The carpenter will offer to take you to Shuten Halfway or Shuten Summit after the first attempt.
Note that Halfway Well doesn't allow carry-in items, so you'll have to have an empty inventory.

Trap Dungeon Basics:

  • You can pick up traps and carry them around.
  • Setting a trap down doesn't end your turn.
  • You can only set traps down on the tile you're standing on.
  • Shiren's attack is drastically reduced when he has the Trapper status.
  • Defeating enemies without using traps only provides 20% of the normal amount of experience points.
  • You gain experience points when monsters step on traps, even if the trap doesn't defeat them.
  • Throwing a trap at a monster deals a small amount of damage to them.
  • Traps have a chance to break when monsters step on them, unless a Sturdy Trap Statue is nearby.
  • Clearing the dungeon rewards you with a Trapper Bracelet, which grants the Trapper status while equipped.
  • Pickaxe is the only weapon found on the ground in this dungeon.
  • Shields cannot be found, except for a Traproid Shield from a Golden Interval treasure chest.
  • Storage Pots can be found normally, and grasses can be purchased from dungeon shops.
  • Chain and Flood traps can be found normally. (Both are exclusive to Shiren 2)

Expanding on some of the important points mentioned above:

Shiren's attack is greatly reduced when he has the Trapper status.
His attack strength nearly stops growing after level 3, and stops entirely after level 20.
To illustrate, Shiren's attack at level 99 in Halfway Well is lower than level 6 inside other dungeons.
So you won't be able to reliably defeat monsters using regular attacks beyond the first few floors.
That said, it's still worth leveling up in this dungeon to increase max HP.

Defeating enemies without using traps only provides 20% of the normal amount of experience points.
However, defeating monsters using traps provides 95% experience, or 75% if defeated using a Spoil Trap.
A monster stepping on a trap also provides 75% experience, so if you find a Sturdy Trap Statue early on,
you can easily reach level 99 near the beginning of the dungeon by chaining traps together.

Other notes:

The amount of damage dealt for damage type traps like Rockfall Trap increases by 2% per level up.
Despite that, most traps except for Rockslide Trap still don't deal enough damage to quickly defeat enemies.

Shiren is immune to Statues effects inside this dungeon, as they affect enemy monsters instead.
For example, you can circle around an Ouch Statue to have it defeat a monster without Shiren taking damage.

Monster Houses can be generated, but Special Monster Houses do not appear in this dungeon.

You'll automatically clear the dungeon the first time you pick up a Trapper Bracelet found starting on 10F.
After the first clear, the game will prompt you whether you'd like to exit or not when you either
pick up a Trapper Bracelet, or insert or take out a Trapper Bracelet from a Storage Pot.
However, you won't be able to exit while inside a Golden Interval.

Golden Stairs have a 100% chance to generate inside a wall between 50~55F.
The Golden Interval of Halfway Well is the only place where you can obtain a Traproid Shield.
The shield prevents traps from activating when Shiren steps on them, and is one of the best seals in the game.
You'll likely want to make two trips, one to synthesize into your main shield, and a second one for the gallery.

Basic Strategy

How to Defeat Enemies

Shiren's attack is greatly reduced inside this dungeon, so you won't be
able to reliably defeat enemies using regular attacks after the first few floors.
You could slow them or use a poison arrow to lower their strength, but it would still
take a while since you'll only be able to deal 1 damage per hit against later enemies.

So you'll want to make use of traps, statues, scrolls, and Gitan to defeat enemies.
You can obtain a large amount of Gitan to use as a projectile by letting a Gamarra
steal from you, and then inserting the dropped Gitan into a Storage Pot.

Monsters with Floating or Flying elements that step on a tile with a trap won't trigger the trap.
These monsters include Tiny Phoenix, and Yamikichi, Gale, Pumphantasm, Weather Doll families.
Piko Hammer family monsters will destroy traps instead of stepping on them as well.
So you'll have to get a little more creative in how you deal with these enemies.

For example, you could throw Gitan or step on an arrow trap to hit a flying monster,
or step on a landmine or use an Explosion Scroll to defeat everything within a 1 tile radius.
You could also read a Trap Activate Scroll while a flying monster is above a trap.
For Piko Hammer family monsters, you could swing a Swap Staff while standing on a trap,
and have the target trigger the trap you were standing on after swapping places.

Pickaxe Technique

You can dig out one wall tile in a room to mislead the monster AI into thinking there's a hallway,
allowing you to either buy some time or trigger traps along the wall.

Obtaining Food

Riceballs are only found in shops, so you'll have to be a little creative when it comes to obtaining food.

Lure a Nigiri Morph onto a Poison Trap, and then have it transform some items into Large Riceballs.
It's best to increase max fullness to 200% using this method, along with keeping some riceballs on hand for later.

You might come across a Riceball Statue, which causes monsters to drop a Riceball when they're defeated.

As a last resort, you can use Spoil Traps to obtain Spoiled Riceballs, which should be eaten when there are no
enemies nearby, and ideally right after a level up to minimize the loss of experience if you get the level down side effect.

Leveling Shiren

Use Chain and One-Way traps to create a chain reaction that provides lots of experience in a short amount of time.
It's best to wait until you come across a room that has a Sturdy Trap Statue before setting all of your traps to level up.
Avoid setting traps at room entrances on floors where Piko Hammer family monsters appear.

Leveling Enemies

You'll gain more experience by leveling up the target monster prior to having them step on traps.
Cranky Tank (2300), Gigahead (3150), Horse Emperor (3100) are all good early game options.
Disable the leveled up monster by having them step on Strip and Poison traps.

Obtaining Items Efficiently

Use a combination of a Summon Trap, Trip Stone, and either a Rockslide Trap or Landmine to finish the monster off.
Just be careful not to place the Landmine in a location that ends up destroying the dropped items.
Requires a Sturdy Trap Statue.

Important Items

Weapon

Pickaxe
Can be used to expand a room with a Sturdy Trap Statue, or to evade enemies and steal from shops.

Grass

Revival Grass
Sometimes sold in shops. Be sure to buy any that you see.
Otogiriso
Sometimes sold in shops. Good to have on hand to restore HP if needed.

Scroll

Wall Collapse Scroll
Keep at least one on hand if you're planning on visiting a Golden Interval for a Traproid Shield.
Useful to read if you see Sturdy Trap, Trip, or Riceball statues.
Explosion Scroll
Can be used to defeat Piko Hammer family monsters and floating enemies.

Staff

Swap Staff
Use it to evade monsters, or to trap Piko Hammer family monsters by swapping places with it while standing on a trap.
Knockback Staff
Use it to evade monsters. Enemies may lose sight of Shiren if used in a hallway.
Balance Staff
Prevents dropping traps due to Armordillo tackles. Note that dropped traps can't be picked back up.
Tunnel Staff
Use it to shape rooms or create hallways, or as a ranged attack that deals 10 damage per swing.
Heal Staff
Dropped by Evil Kangaroo.
Swing it at Ghost type monsters such as Kamikaze to deal 100 damage to them.
Seal Staff
Dropped by King Tusker.
Can be used to prevent monsters from using their special attacks, such as Porky throwing stones.
Slow Staff
Dropped by Monarch Tusker.
Use it to safely run away from a monster.

Pot

Storage Pot
Can be used to store traps or other items.

Projectile

Cannonballs
Always deals 40 damage, which is pretty decent in this dungeon since Shiren's attack strength is so low.

Trap

Spoil Trap
Turns a monster into a Spoiled Riceball, instantly defeating it.
Landmine, Big Landmine
Explosions instantly defeat all enemies in a 1 tile radius.
Great to use against floating enemies and Piko Hammer family monsters.
Recommended to use Landmines instead of Big Landmines to reduce the amount of damage Shiren takes.
Poison Trap
Lowers a monster's strength to 0.
Shiren can intentionally step on it to have poison arrows fly at Piko Hammer family monsters or floating enemies.
Spring
Use it like Warp Grass to jump out of Monster Houses, or to escape to another room when you're surrounded.
Pitfall
Intentionally step on it to proceed to the next floor.
Recommended to save them for emergencies, or to get through floors with difficult monsters like Kamikaze (41~51F).
Chain Trap
Activates all traps in a 1 tile radius. Use it to gain experience points quickly.
Note that only one Chain Trap will count for experience points in a trap activation chain, so you need to use other traps.
One-Way Trap
Easy to activate in a trap activation chain for experience points.
Trip Stone
Use it with a Summon Trap to obtain items, if there's a Sturdy Trap Statue in the room.
Summon Trap
Move it out of the way so monsters don't step on it.
Can be used with a Trip Stone to obtain items, if there's a Sturdy Trap Statue in the room.
Strip Trap
Seals a monster, preventing them from using their special attack. Place it in hallways.
Sleep Trap
Use it if you need to run away from a monster.
Slow Trap
Use it if you need to run away from a monster.
Also slows down monsters behind it, so it can be beneficial to leave the slowed monster alone instead of defeating it.
Spin Trap
Can be used to confuse a monster.
Use it if you need to run away, or if you want to try to level up a monster for more experience points.

Statue

Sturdy Trap Statue
Mostly used to level Shiren up or to obtain items.
Riceball Statue
Use it to increase max fullness to 200%.
Trip Statue
Use it to collect items. Note that Shiren won't trip.
Gitan Statue
Use it to obtain Gitan, which can be used as a projectile against Piko Hammer family monsters or floating enemies.
10 Damage Statue
Shiren only takes 10 damage when using Landmines or Big Landmines to defeat monsters.
Can also be used to defeat regular monsters fighting 1-on-1.
Ouch Statue
Doesn't attack Shiren, so you can circle around it to have it defeat enemies.
Good to eat Spoiled Riceballs near it for added safety.

Floor Guide

This floor guide assumes you're aiming to reach 99F,
rather than simply obtaining a Trapper Bracelet on 10F to clear the story.

The following floors have a fixed map generation of either a single mid-sized room, or a single large room with fog:

4, 5, 13, 23, 30, 31, 39, 40, 55, 57, 71, 80, 81, 90.

01~03F

1F enemies can be defeated using regular attacks, so pick up all traps and items.
Try to obtain Storage Pots and other items if you find a Trip Stone.
You can gain more experience by punching an enemy and then making it trip.

Mid Chintala makes an appearance on 2F, but it shouldn't be too difficult to defeat if you make use of traps.
Spin Traps can be used to level up Pit Mamel or Mid Chintala into Cave Mamel or Big Chintala, and then
you can defeat them using a combination of traps to level Shiren up, sometimes up to level 30 or so.
Summon Traps can be used to increase efficiency.

04~07F

Monsters include Bow Boy (4~7F) and Piko Hammer (4~10F).
4~5F is always a single large room with restricted view.

Piko Hammer roams around the map and destroys every trap it sees, so it's a troublesome monster inside Halfway Well.
One way to defeat it quickly would be to swing a Swap Staff at it while standing on top of a trap you want it to land on.
It only has 13 HP, so you could also swing a Tunnel Staff at it to deal 10 damage, and then finish it off with a punch.

You can use the knockback from an Armordillo Bro's tackle to level it up into an Armordillo Pop,
and then use a trap activation chain to level Shiren up. Just be sure to place your traps
inside a Storage Pot, and leave the pot on the ground while you do this.

Shiren quickly struggles to defeat enemies on his own from about this floor range.
It's still possible to defeat enemies with around 5 attacks, but by 10F it will be nearly
impossible to defeat enemies using regular attacks in a decent amount of time.

08~09F

Monsters include Boy Cart (8~9F), Ironhead (9~11F), Nigiri Morph (8~10F), and Gale (9~10F).

Lower Nigiri Morph's strength to 0 using a Poison Trap, and have it turn unneeded items into Large Riceballs.
Leave important items on the ground while you do this, and aim to increase max fullness to 200%.

You can collect Iron Arrows from a Boy Cart if you have a Wall Collapse Scroll.
Arrows don't deal much damage inside this dungeon, but can be used to check for monsters inside hallways.

Level up a Boy Cart or Ironhead into a Cranky Tank or Gigahead for experience points.
Shiren can get by at around level 30 or so, but ideally you want to reach level 99 here using a Sturdy Trap Statue.
Shiren will have about 500 max HP at level 99, greatly reducing the risk of collapsing.

Gale appears from 9F, and serves as the final challenge for picking up a Trapper Bracelet on 10F and clearing the story.
Gale moves at double speed, and is immune to traps thanks to its floating element.
Have Shiren step on a Landmine or Poison Trap to dispose of them.

10~19F

Trapper Bracelets can be found on every floor starting on 10F, so pick one up if you're ready to go home.
You can also pick one up and select stay, and then place it on the ground → pick it up again to return to the village later.
Note that the bracelet will take up an inventory slot that could be used for a useful item if you decide to carry it for later.

Start prioritizing traps that can one-shot enemies like Spoil Traps and Landmines, because the
smaller damage dealing traps like Log, Arrow, and Rockfall traps won't be enough to defeat enemies soon.
Also collect traps to help with running away, such as Springs and Pitfalls.

Landmine Ninja (10~12F, 22~24F, 40~42F, 59~63F, 81~83F) appears periodically from this point,
so linger around the floor and defeat them to stock up on Landmines if possible.
However, note that it won't drop Landmines if you use traps to defeat it, so you'll want to use traps
like Poison Arrow and Rust to lower its attack and defense to 0, and then use regular attacks to defeat it.

Poko Hammer (15~35F) makes an appearance.
It has high HP and attack, so use the Swap Staff technique to defeat it with traps.
You could also use Explosion Scrolls, but you'll ideally want to save those until later for Kamikaze.

If Evil Kangaroo (17~21F) drops a Heal Staff, keep at least one on hand for Kamikaze.

20~39F

Dangerous monsters include Sky Dragon (20~30F) and Monarch Tusker (20~26F).
Watch out for Monarch Tusker's Slow Staff in particular, as it can slow you from a distance.
Archdragon doesn't naturally spawn in Halfway Well, so 30F is the last of the Dragon family monsters.

Again, if Evil Kangaroo (17~21F) drops a Heal Staff, keep at least one on hand for Kamikaze.
Kamikaze has exactly 100 HP, so a Heal Staff swing will instantly defeat it.

40~49F

Kamikaze (41~49F) and Pako Hammer (45~60F) make an appearance.

Kamikaze moves at triple speed, attacks three times per turn, and is immune to traps.
Use items like Heal Staves, Explosion Scrolls, and Landmines to defeat them.
Also keep in mind, running away with Springs or Pitfalls is an option too.

Pako Hammer can be dealt with the same way as earlier, using items like Swap Staff + traps.

This would be a good floor range to pick up a Trapper Bracelet, if you aren't already carrying one.

50~55F

Golden Stairs has a 100% chance to be generated inside a breakable wall tile between 50~55F.
However, it won't be generated if there are no breakable walls on the floor, so you'll want to
check to make sure that condition is met before reading a Wall Collapse Scroll.
To minimize risk, go around the map a bit and defeat some enemies before reading a Wall Collapse Scroll.

56~60F

The Moat Koi castle decoration can be found starting on 50F.
If you're not aiming for 99F, go ahead and exit after obtaining the Traproid Shield and Moat Koi castle decoration.

61~85F

Monsters like Porkton (55~72F) and Lashaaga (60~87F) make an appearance.
They both hit hard, but should still be easier to deal with compared to Kamikaze and Pako Hammer.
All monsters except for Tiny Phonix can be trapped on this floor range.

86~99F

Stun Scorpion and Emperor Tusker have the potential to defeat you in an instant even at level 99,
so be very cautious of them and take it one step at a time.

Typhoon doesn't appear in Halfway Well, so it's not as difficult to reach 99F from this point
compared to some of the other post-game dungeons, as long as you take things slow.

The dungeon loops endlessly after 99F, so unless you have reason otherwise,
go ahead and return to Napus Village using a Trapper Bracelet on 99F.

Golden Interval

Golden Stairs is hidden inside of a breakable wall 100% of the time between 50~55F.
You'll be returned to 56F after claiming the treasure and exiting from the Golden Interval area.
Golden Interval maps are shuffle type, so items like Tunnel Staff and Pitfalls will be useless.

Gitan is the only item that can be found on the ground inside this Golden Interval,
and there are no traps, so you'll need to rely on the items you gathered prior to entering the area.
That said, remember that you can insert Gitan into a Storage Pot and use it as a projectile.

Pako Hammer makes an appearance, so you'll want to use the Swap Staff technique
combined with traps like Poison Arrow, Spoil, or Landmine to deal with them.
