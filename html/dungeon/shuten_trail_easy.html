Shuten Trail Easy

The first real dungeon of Shiren's adventure after clearing Small Valley.

Shiren's primary goal at this point in the game is to build a castle.
In order to do so, he has to gather castle materials in the mountains,
make castle parts out of them at the summit, and then return to Napus Village.

This dungeon has a lower difficulty, just as the name implies.
Monsters don't hit very hard, and there aren't any boss fights,
so you can likely one-shot clear it if you have some experience.

That said, you can't synthesize equipment yet, and you won't
find any bracelets, arrows, or staves on the ground, so
enemies in the second half might give you a bit of trouble.

Clearing this trail will unlock Shuten Trail Mid, and then
clearing the second trail will unlock Shuten Trail Hard.

Dungeon Overview

Three key points:

  1. Bracelets, staves, and arrows cannot be found in Shuten Trail Easy,
    so you'll progress through the dungeon while making use of scrolls and grasses.

  2. Items on the ground are already identified, including weapons and shields.
    You can check upgrade values as you pick items up, and none of them will be cursed.
    Take your time to search for equipment. Even if what you find has lower stats,
    you might be able to make use of it later on once you're able to synthesize.

  3. Shuten Trail's shuffle dungeon maps are quite pretty, so take a moment
    to enjoy the atmosphere of each floor instead of simply rushing through them.
    You could even return after obtaining a Phantasm Bracelet
    and take a walk through the air to admire the view.

Floor Guide

01~02F Green Forest (Shuffle)

The only monsters that appear are Mamel and Chintala, similar to Small Valley.
They're weak, so you shouldn't have any trouble defeating them.
However, there's still a chance you'll collapse if you're surrounded, so walk into
a hallway and take them on one at a time if you see multiple monsters in the room.
Recommended to get Shiren up to level 3 before proceeding to 3F.

You won't have any of the items you picked up in Small Valley, so you'll
start with just one Large Riceball in your inventory the first time you enter.
Be sure to check every room on each floor, picking up any items as you go.
If you find equipment, go ahead and equip it right away.

There's an item called Savings Pot exclusively found on 1F of this dungeon.
It lets you keep your Gitan if you collapse, but it takes up 3 inventory slots,
so it's only recommended for those who aren't confident in their abilities.

03~04F Bamboo Forest (Shuffle)

Monsters include Weather Doll, Ashigarudon, Slug, Yamikichi, and Piko Hammer.

Slugs have a special attack that lowers strength, so avoid letting them act first.
If there's a 1 tile gap between you and a monster, stay still and perform an
attack to have the monster approach you instead of you moving toward them.

The other monsters only use regular attacks, so you shouldn't have too much
trouble defeating them if you leveled up in Green Forest as recommended.
Recommended to get Shiren up to level 4~5 before proceeding to 5F.

05~06F Deserted Shaft

Monsters include Piko Hammer, Droplet Ninja, and Armordillo.

Traps begin to appear from this area.

Droplet Ninja places a Droplet Trap (deals 5 damage) on the ground when defeated.
Lure them to the middle of rooms, or on top of items (traps cannot be placed if the
tile is occupied) to avoid having them place the trap somewhere you're likely to step on.

Piko Hammer is a handy monster that destroys traps that it can see,
and often removes Droplet Traps left by Droplet Ninja in this area.
However, it has high attack, so be careful when fighting them.
If your HP gets low, use items such as Herb or Otogiriso to heal.

Armordillo can perform a tackle to make Shiren trip and drop items.
Fight with your back against a wall to avoid dropping items when tripped.
If you do drop items, be sure to pick them up after defeating it.

Hunger Puppet will make an appearance on 6F.

Hunger Puppet can sing a song that lowers Shiren's fullness by 5%.
A 5% loss of hunger isn't a big deal, so it's not something to worry about.
If you're concerned, you can buy some Giant Riceballs in the next area.

Shuten Halfway

An interval area where you can strengthen equipment and buy items from a shop.

Blacksmith

  • Strengthens a weapon for 1500 Gitan.
  • Can only strengthen equipment once per adventure.
  • Ususally the increase of upgrade value is +1, but on rare occasions it will be +3.
  • Can also remove synthesis seals, or repair cracked bracelets.
Service Price Info
Hone Weapon 1500 Increases weapon upgrade value by +1 or +3.
Erase Seal 1000 Removes 1 synthesis seal of your choice.
Repair Bracelet 500 Repairs cracked bracelets.

Trader's Shop

  • Offers items for sale including Otogiriso, Giant Riceball, equipment, and scrolls.
  • Since it's your first time through, go ahead and buy some Storage Pots and Otogiriso.
  • The shop also offers a strong Beetle Shield, but you likely won't be able to buy it yet.
    Damage reduction from defense value doesn't make as much of a difference in Shiren 2
    compared to other games in the series, so it's not an essential purchase either way.

If you happen to collapse 10 times before clearing Shuten Trail Easy,
a Trader will visit Napus Village and offer the same items for sale.
The Napus Village Trader will disappear when you clear Shuten Trail Easy.

07F Inexhaustible Falls (Shuffle)

Monsters include Hunger Puppet, Zalokleft, and N'dubba.

Zalokleft will steal an item on the ground or from Shiren's inventory, and then warp away.
You can get the item back by defeating it, so search the current floor until you
find it if it steals valuable items like castle materials or Revival Grass.

N'dubba disguises itself as an item, and performs surprise attack when you try to pick it up.
So be sure to have plenty of HP before picking up items on floors where N'dubba appears.
Note that you can recover HP quickly by holding B + A buttons together.

08F Stalactite Cave

Monsters include the ones from 7F, and new arrivals Samuraidon and Horse Ronin.

Samuraidon is the level 2 monster of the Ashigarudon family.
It has a Counter Shield equipped, so a portion of the damage
you deal to it when attacking will be reflected back at Shiren.

Horse Ronin shoots Silver Arrows that pierce through characters from a distance.
Be careful where you stand when Horse Ronin is nearby, since its silver arrows can
defeat other monsters, resulting in Horse Ronin leveling up into a Horse Samurai.
Not even Dragon Grass is enough to defeat Horse Samurai in a single hit (79 HP),
so if that happens, run to the stairs while moving in a zig-zag pattern to avoid arrows.

09~10F Abyss Gorge (Shuffle)

New arrivals for 9F include Nigiri Morph and Tiger Tosser.

Nigiri Morph transforms Shiren's items into Large Riceballs.
It's particularly bad if a Storage Pot gets turned into a riceball since you
lose all contents, so use items to defeat it before it gets near you when possible.

Tiger Tosser has high attack, and throws Shiren either at other monsters or onto traps.

New arrivals for 10F include Shaaga, Landmine Ninja, and Sheep Priest.

Shaaga doesn't have a special ability, but has high attack.
Don't hesitate to use scrolls or grasses against it.

Landmine Ninja drops a Landmine when defeated, which halves Shiren's current HP.
Be careful of the Tiger Tosser + Landmine dropped by Landmine Ninja combination.

Sheep Priest is a dangerous monster that can halve Shiren's strength for 30 turns.
Taking longer to defeat other monsters means an increase in the amount of damage
you take per encounter, and monsters have more opportunities to use special attacks.

There are usually multiple castle materials on the ground in this area,
so search every room to collect as many as you can.
However, note that materials found in Shuten Trail Easy are the lowest grade,
so you'll want to switch to higher grade materials after building the castle up a bit
to prevent the Oni from easily destroying sections of the castle during later events.
