Onigashima

Dungeon you can enter after completing the castle.
Shiren and his allies go separate ways to avoid being
attacked as a group, with the goal of meeting up inside.

You could say this is where the game truly begins, with the appearance
of Monster Houses, more unidentified items including scrolls, and a far
deeper dungeon without the safety of an interval area like Shuten Halfway.

Adding to the difficulty is a lower carry-in limit of 7 items.
Think carefully about what items you want to have on hand.

There is a boss fight against Galaha on 16F, and
then a final showdown against Boss Oni on 25F.
Defeating Boss Oni wraps up the main story,
and your save file gains a new Boss Oni icon.

Dungeon Overview

Four key points:

  1. All floors inside Onigashima use the random dungeon style map generation, and have fog in hallways.
    Every post-game dungeon uses the same style of map generation as Onigashima.

  2. Monster houses make an appearance for the first time.
    They have a random chance to spawn between 3~24F, excluding boss floors.
    Monster Houses can be challenging depending on how you tackle them,
    but they're a great way to obtain a bunch of items in short order.

  3. Flood Trap, Monster Trap, and Pitfall make an appearance for the first time.
    You'll want to perform an empty attack before each step inside a Monster House to avoid accidentally
    stepping on one of them, resulting in either falling to the next floor, or transforming all items into monsters.

  4. You won't have any allies when you enter Onigashima for the first time, but you might
    run into some of them while exploring the dungeon, in which case you can join back up.
    If you collapse in Onigashima during the story, Mamo will retrieve the equipment you lost.
    You can pick the equipment up by talking to Mamo in the morning, though
    note that the equipment will have a 25% upgrade value / seal penalty.

Floor Guide

01~06F

Standard early game monsters you've seen throughout the three trails, along with Small Oni on 4~6F.

Small Oni has somewhat high attack for an early game monster,
but they shouldn't be much of a threat if you have the recommended equipment.

There are many waterways in Onigashima, so don't let your guard down and
get tripped in the direction of water by an Armordillo if you didn't bring a Balance Staff.

07~12F

This is where troublesome monsters like Gazer, Shotfish, Nigiri Morph, and Kengo make an appearance.

Shoot arrows at Gazer (7~8F) to defeat it before they get close and have a chance to hypnotize you.

Shotfish (7~9F) can turn all scrolls in your inventory into Wet Scrolls, excluding those inside pots.

Nigiri Morph (9~10F) can transform your valuable items into Large Riceballs.
Use arrows from a distance, or items if they got close to avoid letting them act if at all possible.

Fight Kengo (11~12F) with your back against a wall, or unequip your shield beforehand to avoid losing it.

Note that Mixer can spawn on 9~12F, along with Mixermon on 12F.
Mixermon is the level 2 monster of the Mixer family, and can swallow up to 3 items.
Go ahead and combine same name staves to free up inventory space, or
synthesize equipment if you have some on hand that you want to combine.

13F~15F

Ranged attackers such as Wolf Droid, Pop Tank, Water Strider, and Bored Kappa make an appearance.

Watch out for Water Strider and Bored Kappa in particular,
since they can potentially throw dangerous items like Gitan, Dragon Grass, and Poison Grass.
Don't let your guard down when you're inside rooms, and keep your HP topped up in case of a surprise attack.

Rocky Oni (14~15F) also makes an appearance, and move and attack at double speed.
Make a habit of unequipping your bracelets when fighting up close to prevent them from cracking,
especially when dealing with monsters that have faster movement.

16F is a boss fight against Galaha and his squad of Oni, so restore your HP
and equip a Pierce Bracelet if you happen to have one before proceeding past 15F.

16F Galaha

Boss fight against Galaha, 5 Small Oni, 4 Rocky Oni, 5 Lanky Oni, and 4 Stumpy Oni.
Note that you only need to defeat Galaha to end the battle.

Galaha has about the same attack and defense stats of an Armor Spider (13F),
so he isn't too difficult to take out. Getting close to him is the trickiest part of the fight.

There are many ways to tackle this fight, but here are some strategies:

  • Snipe Galaha with arrows while having a Pierce Bracelet equipped.

  • Read a Decoy Scroll to lure the squad into attacking shadows,
    and then eat Warp Grass to get close to Galaha and use regular attacks.

  • Read a Blaze Scroll to wipe out the squad.

  • Scatter Perfect Iron Sand to blind everyone in the room, and then run up to Galaha.

  • Throw 710 or more Gitan at Galaha to one-shot him.

If you decide to fight them head on, you're going to take many hits,
so you'll want to equip a shield with multiple 弟 to counter the HP loss.
Also be sure to unequip any bracelets, because they will otherwise quickly shatter.

Side note: lightning damage will actually heal Oni, so reading a Lightning Scroll is not effective.

If you return to Onigashima after finishing the main story, simply send out ally monsters to handle things.
They will automatically go back inside their pots after the fight. (Same with the Boss Oni battle)

※ ※ Warning ※ ※
There's a bug where the game will freeze if Shiren either goes inside of a pot, or
becomes invisible during the fight. (example: ally Devil Kangaroo swinging an Invisible Staff at Shiren).

17~21F

Watch out for Porky, Nigiri Boss, and Tiger Hurler in particular.

Special attacks of some of the more dangerous monsters:

Porky

Tosses stones at targets within 2 tiles, even if they're outside of view.
Nigiri Boss Transforms Shiren into a riceball, reducing his defense to 0.
Tiger Hurler Throws Shiren at monsters or onto traps, or even into Monster Houses.
Stormy Doll Lowers weapon or shield upgrade value by 1.

Shops between 17~20F or Traders between 17~24F have a chance to offer Dragon Tile Bracelets for sale.
Be sure to buy any that you see, since they can be used to synthesize powerful Daisangen equipment.

Come back when you have a Trapper Bracelet and use Pitfalls to hunt for shops and traders more efficiently.
You can also read Restock Scrolls to refresh merchandise if you happen to find a bracelet specialty shop.

22~24F

Powerful monsters including Dragon, Demon Scorpion, and Monarch Tusker make an appearance.
Keep in mind, hurrying to the stairs instead of trying to visit every room is a legitimate strategy in roguelikes.

Monarch Tusker in particular can swing a Slow Staff to either slow you down or paralyze
you from a distance, sometimes resulting in you collapsing before being able to act.
Recommended to rush to the stairs if you don't have Revival Grass on hand.

Special attacks of these monsters:

Dragon

Breathes fire that deals 20 damage if Shiren is lined up on a straight line.
Demon Scorpion Lunges its stinger forward, slowing Shiren down by 1 stage.
Monarch Tusker Swings a Slow Staff at Shiren, slowing him down by 1 stage.
Paralyzes Shiren for 3 turns if he was already slowed, making him unable to act.

Copycat Mask can also spawn between 22~23F.
It's pretty weak, but disguises itself as other monsters, sometimes resulting in wasted item usage like
eating Dragon Grass or throwing Gitan at what you thought was a Dragon, but turned out to be one of these.
You can identify them by swinging a Knowledge Staff or Seal Staff.

25F Boss Oni

The final boss of story mode is a 1-on-1 fight against Boss Oni.
He has high offensive and defensive stats, and can use a multitude of special attacks, including
recovering from status conditions, breathing fire, and making Shiren trip from anywhere in the room.

You might see your items go up in flames if you don't have a Balance Staff in your inventory.
Sanctuary Scrolls also burn up, so placing one on the ground to create a safe zone can be unreliable.

If Boss Oni has a status condition, he will instantly cure it the next time he is attacked by Shiren.
However, he can't restore lost strength due to getting hit by Poison Grass.

You can one-shot Boss Oni by throwing a Storage Pot at him with a total of 4500 or more Gitan inside.
It's best to equip a Bullseye Bracelet if you use this strategy so that you don't miss.
Bullseye Bracelet is easier to find in Shuten Trail Hard rather than Onigashima.

Otherwise, use items like Swift Grass and Decoy Scroll to get some hits in without taking damage.

Boss Oni will give you a Crescent Arm every time you return here and defeat him after finishing the story.
The weapon has high attack and slot counts, and offers one of the seals required creating a Kaburasutegi.

Some post-game strategies include using a Landmine and Trapper Bracelet to
one-shot Boss Oni, or sending out ally monsters take care of the fight.
You could even throw an Extinction Scroll at him for fun.
