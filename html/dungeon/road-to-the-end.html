Shiren 2 Wiki / Road to the End

Road To The End

The ultimate gameplay experience of Shiren 2.
Most items and monsters appear in this dungeon, and
bracelets, grasses, staves, pots, and scrolls are found unidentified.

Each run of this dungeon can play out in many different ways depending on
the items you find, the actions you take, the knowledge and strategies you use,
and of course, last but not least, how good your luck is.

The difficulty is on the high end, thanks to a carefully balanced monster table.
In particular, monsters like Megataur, Gigahead, Emperor Tusker, Typhoon,
and Stun Scorpion appear from 86~99F, often making for an intense final stretch.

If you successfully make it through to the end, you'll find yourself arriving at a strange
new area unlike any other location in the game, and your save file will gain a Shiren icon.

Side note: Escape Scrolls can be found on 30F, 50F, and 70F.

Important Items

Key items that greatly increase the clear rate:

  • Healing Sword (Synthesize Herb, Otogiriso, and Life Grass onto a weapon)
  • Scout Bracelet
  • Phantasm Bracelet (Shop, Trader, Presto Pot)
  • Blank Scroll
  • Extinction Scroll (Shop)
  • Wall Collapse Scroll
  • Monster Pot
  • Possession Staff
  • Strength Grass, Boost Grass, Grass Gainer, Grass Tosser
  • Revival Grass

Scout Bracelet is the single most effective item in the game to lower the difficulty of a dungeon.
Use it to avoid surprise attacks in hallways, and to identify shops or Monster Houses at a glance.
Unequip it before fighting monsters to help prevent it from cracking.

Phantasm Bracelet rivals Scout Bracelet in utility once you master how to use it.
It can be used to attack from inside walls, take shortcuts, or warp to a different room by unequipping it inside a wall.
It's rare to obtain both bracelets in a run, but Phantasm Bracelet and Scout Bracelet play well together as a set too.

Blank Scroll can be turned into a Blank:Extinction scroll to banish monsters like Archdragon and Typhoon,
or into a Blank:Sanctuary that can be reused by splashing water on it from a Water Pot,
or use it as synthesis fodder to increase an equipment's slot count, etc.
Side note: Extinction Scroll is a shop-exlusive item in Road to the End.

Wall Collapse Scroll is mainly used to search for Golden Stairs leading to Golden Intervals.
The items you find inside Golden Intervals can be of great help on your descent to 99F.
The first Golden Interval between 6~10F is usually skipped, due to food shortages.
The second one can be found between 24~29F, and the last one between 90~95F.
Keep 6 Wall Collapse Scrolls on hand for the third one if you're looking for a Mijinha.

Monster Pots can be used to capture an Armordillo Pop, which can be used to obtain a ridiculous amount of items.
If you find one early in the dungeon, level up an Armordillo Bro (5~6F) using an item like Happy Staff to capture a Pop.
If you don't have a way to level up an Armordillo Bro, you could go ahead and capture the Bro instead.
However, you'll need to be extra careful with how you handle it to keep it alive.
Watch out for things like Nigiri Morph's special attack and Trip Stones so that you don't lose the Monster Pot.

If you find a Monster Pot after 6F, you could wait until Armordillo Pop can be found naturally between 30~39F,
or capture a different monster like Shihan, Monsoon Doll, Hyper Gazer, Gazelle Pope, Water Shinryu,
Stun Scorpion, Emperor Tusker, or Tiny Phoenix. Shihan is likely the second choice, as it can hold its own
in fights thanks to its high stats, and can be used to obtain Scout Bracelets and other equipment.

Possession Staff can be used to level monsters (have the target monster defeat the monster you're controlling)
and obtain items (take control of Armordillo or Kengo family monsters), among other uses.
This is often your go-to item to collect items between 5~6F where Armordillos appear.
This staff doesn't cost much (6 uses = 650G), so be sure to buy it if you see it in a shop.

Strength Grass, Boost Grass, and either Grass Gainer or Grass Tosser
can be used in combination to quickly increase Shiren's strength.
Strength plays a key role in damage calculation in Shiren 2, so it's important to increase it when possible.
Dealing more damage means you'll restore more HP per attack from Healing Sword (回 seal) as well.

Shiren 2 doesn't have pots or scrolls that can restore HP, so it's important
to make use of Otogiriso and Revival Grass in ways that aren't wasteful.

Weapon

Main Weapon

Ryujin Sword is the best possible main weapon, but you likely won't find four Dragon Killers
inside a Golden Interval without using Armordillo or Shihan to obtain more items.
Dragon Killer, Dotanuki, and Spark Sword are all good options. You can make up the lack of attack
by increasing your strength, so it's best to go for a weapon with many synthesis slots instead of raw stats.

Mijinha is technically the strongest obtainable weapon,
but it's only available from 95F, so it's not practical to include it in the list.
Same with Daisangen Sword, since it's unrealistic to obtain 3 Dragon Seal Bracelets in a single run.

Shield

Main Shield

Defense doesn't increase from level ups or strength, so the only way to increase it is to equip stronger shields.
The defense stat in Shiren 2 doesn't play as large of a role as other games in the series at reducing the amount
of damage you take, but despite that, it's still good to aim for high defense to reduce the damage as much as possible.

Shields with high base defense are good for early game, since it's difficult to increase upgrade value at that point.
That said, Fuuma Shield, Ivory Shield, Leather Shield, and Walrus Shield are all good main shield options.
The shield you choose will likely come down to a mix of play style and whatever you happen to find.

Basilisk Shield, Daisangen Shield, and Helix Shield aren't included in the list due to being impractical to obtain.

Bracelet

Scout Bracelet
Bracelet that drastically lowers the difficulty of the dungeon.
Allows you to see the locations of all monsters, items, and statues on the map.
Avoid walking while it's equipped, since you don't want it to crack from stepping on a trap.
If it cracks, be sure to read a Plating Scroll to repair it before it breaks.
Phantasm Bracelet
Powerful bracelet that rivals Scout Bracelet.
Allows you to go inside walls, letting you defeat enemies without being hit and therefore saving items.
Seals like 回 and 弟 should be synthesized onto your weapon to restore HP while inside walls.
Watch out for Archdragon fire and other monsters that can attack inside walls like Pumphantasm and Typhoon.
Can be used to steal, and unequipping it while inside a wall will warp you to a random room on the current floor.
Pierce Bracelet
Bracelet that can be either good or bad, depending on how you use it.
Throw items like Poison Grass or Gitan to weaken or defeat a line of monsters, roll a cannonball
while it's equipped to trigger traps below monsters, clear out a Monster House with arrows, etc.
Keep it unequipped when you don't need its effects to avoid losing valuable items.
Salty Bracelet
Blocks the strength lowering effects of Slug licks, which cannot be blocked by a Scale Shield.
Level Lock Bracelet
Blocks level down effects of Twisty Hani dances and Gazelle Pope spells.
Twisty Hanis have a high item drop rate, so it's nice to have this bracelet when hunting them for items.
Dragon Tile Bracelet
Rare bracelet that is exclusively found in shops between 2~29F.
Can be used with a Trip Stone to collect items, and is also an ingredient for synthesizing Daisangen equipment.

Staff

Grass Gainer
Insert Strength Grass to increase max strength.
Other grasses like Angel Seed, Happy Grass, Life Grass, and Warp Grass can be used,
however Strength Grass should be prioritized, since strength is a large factor in Shiren 2's damage calculation.
To get one more use out of a "Strength Gain Staff[0]", throw it at a Tonosamadon to have it reflect the staff,
or use a Bend Bracelet and throw it at a wall so that it curves around and hits Shiren.
Don't forget to eat Boost Grass before swinging it to double the gains.
Grass Tosser
Trickier to use than Grass Gainer, but can be used in a similar manner by swinging it at monsters that reflect magic.
Such monsters include Taishodon, Escargon, Asst. Manager, and Manager.
Make use of Tiger Tossers or Bomb type monsters to level up a Shopkeeper.
Other grasses like Poison Grass, Twisty Grass, and Warp Grass can be inserted, but Strength Grass should be prioritized.
Miss Staff
Lowers a monster's regular attack accuracy to 0%, making all of their attacks miss.
Monster attacks are guaranteed to connect even if they are under the effect of this staff when Shiren is paralyzed,
so it's not as effective against monsters like Monarch Tusker, Stun Scorpion, Rock Beast, and Boulder Beast.
Doesn't affect the accuracy of projectiles or special attacks.
Fire Staff
Effective against Cave Mamels, leveled up enemies, and monsters in hallways.
Can be used to destroy troublesome statues or scrolls stuck to the ground within 5 tiles.
Use a Knockback Staff after swinging a Fire Staff when an enemy is close to deal 80~100 damage in a single turn.
Quarter Staff
Reduces a monster's HP to 1/4 of its current amount.
Great when fighting monsters with lots of HP, or when you lack offensive power.
Slow Staff
Allows you to defeat enemies without taking damage by looping attack → step back → attack.
Be careful against enemies with ranged attacks, or encountering an enemy in the opposite direction.
Possession Staff
Among the best staves in the game once you master how to use it.
Can be used to level up enemies, guard Shiren while he regenerates HP,
collect items by taking control of Armordillo or Kengo family monsters,
remove curses from items by taking control of a Straw Voodoll family monster,
gather wooden or iron arrows by taking control of a Bow Boy or Bow Cart,
obtain riceballs by taking control of a Nigiri Master, or use it to walk while ignoring traps, etc.
Seal Staff
Prevents a monster from being able to use their special attack.
Nice to have when facing Kengo family monsters, Monsoon Doll, Nigiri Master, Tiger Chucker, etc.

Scroll

Blank Scroll
One of the best items in the game. Can be used as any scroll you've read in the past, including Extinction Scroll.
Extinction Scroll
Makes a monster vanish from the dungeon for the duration of the current adventure.
Some nice options include Archdragon, Cranky Tank, Porkton, Shihan, and Typhoon.
Think carefully before you throw it, and watch out for Copycat Mask monsters disguised as the target monster.
Plating Scroll
Can be used to repair bracelets or add a red 金 seal to equipment.
Sanctuary Scroll
One of these is usually enough to handle an entire Monster House.
However, you still need to watch out for monsters that have ranged attacks.
Slumber Scroll
Great for emergencies, such as when you're surrounded by monsters from stepping on a Summon Trap.
Note that monsters will wake up with increased movement speed after 10 turns.
Also makes for an effective synthesis seal.
Decoy Scroll
Great for emergencies. No downsides like Slumber Scroll, and can be used to evade Archdragon fire.
Note that shadows will fail to appear if the current floor is at the monster/character limit,
and monsters like Ironhead and Cranky Tank can attack through the shadows.
Fear Scroll
Great for emergencies. Limited to a 1 tile radius around Shiren, but effective against enemies inside walls.
Identify Scroll
Particularly nice for this dungeon, since most item categories are found unidentified.
Prioritize grasses first in search of a Life Grass to make a Healing Sword, and then items like bracelets and pots.
Staves and equipment should only be identified using this scroll during the second half of the dungeon.
Restock Scroll
Read it inside shops to restock or change the items for sale.
Save it for shops between 71~90F if you're interested in obtaining an Upgrade Pot.
Wall Collapse Scroll
Used to steal from shops or to search for Golden Stairs.
Recommended to read it on 9~10F, 24~29F, and 90~95F when searching for Golden Stairs.
Can also be used to quickly find a path to the stairs when Archdragon fire is wearing you down.
Items like Talking Seed, Slumber Scroll, Swap Staff, and Seal Staff can be used afterward to secure a path to the stairs.
Escape Scroll
Found on the ground on 30F, 50F, and 70F. However, there's no point in carrying it around if you're aiming for 99F.
That said, it can be nice to have on hand in case you find rare items like a Dragon Tile Bracelet or Upgrade Pot.

Grass

Revival Grass
Fully restores HP, strength, and fullness if you collapse. No effect when it's inside a pot.
Sometimes dropped by Mr. Gyro. Shiren 2 lacks items that fully restore HP, so stock up on as many as possible.
You don't want to waste these, so don't let your guard down or get careless just because you have this in your inventory.
Herb
Healing Sword ingredient. Somewhat rare, so you'll want to identify it by checking prices inside a shop,
or by reading an Identify Scroll on a grass item, as opposed to eating unidentified grasses.
Otogiriso
Restores 100 HP. Healing Sword ingredient, and provides the fantastic 弟 seal when synthesized. Pretty common.
Life Grass
Increases max HP by 5 points, and is also a Healing Sword ingredient.
Somewhat rare, so don't eat unidentified grasses in case one of them happens to be this grass.
Strength Grass
Use it with Boost Grass and Grass Gainer to greatly increase Shiren's strength.
Easy to identify, since Mr. Drone always drops this grass.
Boost Grass
Used with Strength Grass and Grass Gainer to increase max strength.
Can also be used with Happy Grass, Angel Seed, and Life Grass, but Strength Grass should be prioritized.
Happy Grass
Throw it at a monster early game to gain more experience points. Better to eat it yourself later on.
Can be thrown at a Mixer monster to level it up and increase the number of items it can swallow,
or to create a Mr. Gyro in hopes of it dropping a Revival Grass.
Swift Grass
Nice to have for Monster Houses, or if you're in a room with many monsters and need to escape.
Warp Grass
Allows you to escape from a Monster House even if there's a No Magic Statue present in the room.
Try to keep one on hand at all times.
Antidote Grass
Restores lost strength due to poison arrows, Slug licks, and Scorpion stings.
Be sure to eat one of these before you use Strength Grass so that you increase max strength.
Sight Grass
Best to eat it at the beginning of a floor.
Use it on 9F to look for a Poison Trap, which can be used to lower Nigiri Morph's strength and obtain riceballs.
Can also be used to look for Pitfalls, or a Trip Stone if you have a Dragon Tile Bracelet and want to collect items.

Pot

Storage Pot
One of the most important items in the game, since it lets you expand your inventory.
Easily identified by looking inside a pot and checking for a sound effect by pressing the L button.
Identify Pot
Somewhat common pot that lets you identify multiple items. Prioritize grasses, bracelets, and staves.
Degrade Pot
It might appear useless at first glance, but it can be used to store riceballs and grasses,
along with unneeded bracelets or other items to sell when you come across a shop.
Grass Effect Pot
Can be used to weaken en entire Monster House by inserting grasses like Twisty Grass or Poison Grass,
or to deal 100 damage to all monsters in a Ghost House by inserting Otogiriso.
Also used as a container for non-grass items.
Presto Pot
Insert unneeded items or single arrows to transform them into different items.
The probability is low, but it's possible to obtain items like Scout Bracelet or Echo Shield from a Presto Pot.
The Presto Pot item table is narrowed down inside Golden Intervals.
Monster Pot
Armordillo Pop lets you obtain an item from every monster you encounter.
Mixergon is another good option that allows you to synthesize all you want.
You can make Mixergon trip by using a Trip Statue, Possession Staff on an Armordillo,
Trip Stone and either Dragon Tile Bracelet or rolling a Cannonball with a Pierce Bracelet equipped, etc.
Upgrade Pot
Incredibly rare item that can only be found in shops between 71~90F.
Stock up on Restock and Blank scrolls using an Armordillo Pop if you want to try to obtain one,
but it's common to go through 20 Restock Scrolls and still not see it, so you'll likely need to make multiple trips.

Identifying Items

Most items inside this dungeon are found unidentified, so it's important to learn how to
identify using techniques rather than solely relying on items like Identify Pot and Identify Scroll.
You can compare prices, check for specific effects, or narrow down possibilities based on what you've found.
The only items that are found in an identified state are projectiles, riceballs, Blank Scroll, and Escape Scroll.

Weapon + Shield

The name is already identified, but you won't know if it's cursed until you either equip it or fully identify it.
So it's best to wait to equip a weapon or shield until you find a Strip Trap, or until you reach a floor
where Kengo family monsters can be found.

You can also check for curses or upgrade values by comparing prices inside a shop.
If the selling price is higher than the listed price, it has a positive upgrade value,
but if it's lower, it's either cursed or has a negative upgrade value.

See the Price Chart page for weapon and shield prices.

Floor Guide

The beginning of this dungeon is generally the most difficult part due to enemies having
high attack stats, along with not being able to obtain riceballs using a Nigiri Morph until 9F.
It usually calms down from about mid game, but don't let your guard down when things are going well.

01~04F

Basics

Explore every room and pick up every item you find.
Generally aim to have Shiren's level equal the current floor number + 2 until mid game.
You could proceed to 2F at level 2 if you're concerned about hunger, but it might take up to 3 hits
to defeat Ashigarudon and Yamikichi on 2F if you do so.

Try to move in ways that reduces the number of steps you have to take to check each room.
Walking diagonally is a given, but also watch for terrain patterns to recognize dead ends or loops.
There's a smaller chance of stepping on Pitfalls or Flood Traps with fewer steps, along with conserving fullness.

Monster Houses can appear between 2~99F, but don't appear between 7~10F.
Shops can appear between 2~90F, and Traders can appear between 4~83F.
Special Monster Houses can appear between 12~78F, with every 4 floors starting from 12F
having a higher chance than other floors of one being generated. (12F, 16F, 20F, 24F...)

Monsters

Most monsters only use regular attacks, but Shiren doesn't have much HP yet, so you can collapse pretty easily.
Mamels and Pit Mamels are always generated awake, so it's common to fight many of them in a row on 1F.
Avoid dashing inside rooms so that monsters don't get the first hit.
That said, you can enter the dungeon again right away even if you collapse,
so you could chalk it up to bad luck and not worry too much about playing carefully yet.

Snacky (1~19F) occasionally spawns a Mid Chintala or Cave Mamel when it's defeated by a monster.
It has a higher chance of appearing on 1F than other floors due to the smaller monster table.
If a Chintala (1~2F) levels up into a Mid Chintala, it's usually best to just head for the stairs.

However, if a Pit Mamel (1~3F) levels up into a Cave Mamel, you should try to defeat it for 500 experience points.
Cave Mamels have very high attack and defense but only have 5 HP,
so you can easily defeat it by throwing Gitan, swinging a Knockback Staff, etc.
If you managed to defeat one, you'll jump to level 8~9 or so, making early game much easier.

Slugs (3~5F) can lower your strength, so you'll want to use projectiles or staves
to defeat them before they have a chance to use their special attacks if possible.
You won't be able to restore lost strength until Antidote Grass is identified.

Cave Mamel Hunting

Level up a Pit Mamel into a Cave Mamel between 1~3F for experience points.

  • If you find a Snacky, let a Pit Mamel defeat it.
  • Swing a Happy Staff at a Pit Mamel.
  • Use a Knockback Statue to have a Pit Mamel defeat another one with knockback damage.

Defeating Cave Mamel
Strategies in ascending order of low risk to high risk.
  • Use staves. Easy to identify beforehand, multiple uses, can be used from a distance, and 100% accuracy.
  • Throw 50 or more Gitan. Gitan can be carried around by inserting it into a Storage Pot.
  • Roll a Cannonball to deal 40 damage. Just be careful not to get hit by the explosion yourself.
    There's a chance it will miss, so it's safer to roll it at a wall instead of directly at Cave Mamel.
  • Use a regular attack in a room where a 10 Damage Statue is present.
  • Use regular attacks in a room where a Knockback Statue is present.
  • Shoot 5 arrows at it. Arrows can miss, so you'll want to be at least 7 tiles away to be safe.
    You could also use another monster to block the entrance of a room to defeat it safely.
  • Throw a shield with at least 5 defense.
  • Throw a Storage Pot with enough items inside to deal 5 damage.
  • Throw 5 items that can damage it.
  • Use traps. Poison Trap will lower its strength to 0. Landmines will defeat it, but you won't gain experience.
  • Use an Explosion or Bomber statue to defeat it. However, you won't gain experience.
  • Throw grass at it, or eat a grass item next to it in hopes of it being Swift, Dragon, or Flame grass, Talking Seed, etc.
  • Read a scroll. Place it on the ground to check for Sanctuary Scroll first,
    and then read it when Cave Mamel is next to you in case it's a Fear Scroll.

Items

The biggest issue at this point in the game is hunger.
If you find items like Large Riceball, Giant Riceball, or Leather Shield, it's best to check for traps
so that you don't accidentally step on a Flood or Spoil trap and let the food go to waste.
Keep food safe by storing it inside pots like Storage Pot, Degrade Pot, and Grass Effect Pot.

Wait to identify items until you find a shop or Identify Pot. You don't want to accidentally eat a Revival Grass.

Having cursed equipment is better than no equipment, so go ahead and equip the first items you find.
However, avoid equipping weak items like Club, or items with negative effects like Heavy Shield.
Keep a second weapon on hand to use as a base for making a Healing Sword.

Don't immediately pick up Gitan on 1F, since it can be used to hunt Cave Mamels for experience points.
Snacky spawns less on 2~3F and often gets defeated by other monsters, so it's fine to pick up Gitan there.

If you come across a Trip Statue, collect items from enemies until the wind blows. Just be careful of hunger.

05~08F

Waterways are commonly found between 5~8F, and monster special attacks become more varied.

If an Armordillo or Armordillo Bro (5~6F) makes you trip and drop items in water, the items are likely to be lost.
Monsters like Spearfish (5~8F), Lt. Spearfish (6~8F), Capt. Spearfish (8F) can move underwater.
The latter two can attack from inside water, and Capt. Spearfish has 50 HP, making it particularly tough.
It might be a good idea to recover HP in a room before going into hallways that are surrounded by water.

Mr. Drone (7~9F) makes an appearance. It always drops Strength Grass, so try to defeat it before it runs away.
It's ideal to save Strength Grass until you find Boost Grass and a Grass Gainer to increase gains,
but you could eat some if your inventory is full. Just be sure to save 1 Strength Grass for a Grass Gainer later on.

Limit the amount of time you spend in hallways on 8F to reduce the risk of Gazer (6~8F) or Scorpion (8F)
surprising you with their special attack, which either forces you to use an item, or lowers your strength.
Scorpion and Capt. Spearfish only appear on 8F, so you could rush to the stairs if you don't want to deal with them.

There's a 25% chance for Golden Stairs to be generated inside a wall between 6~10F, so this floor range
is a good time to read unidentified scrolls, in case one of them happens to be a Wall Collapse Scroll.
However, you'll return to 11F after exiting the first Golden Interval, so if you go too early, you'll miss out on
being able to use Nigiri Morphs to make riceballs, along with 2 floors worth of Mixer spawns.
Food cannot be found inside the Golden Interval, so there is some risk of collapsing from starvation too.

Obtaining Items w/Armordillo Pop

If you have a Possession Staff or Monster Pot, you can level up an Armordillo Bro into an Armordillo Pop
between 5~6F to collect items by making enemies trip using their special attack.
It's very rare to find a Monster Pot this early, but if you do find one, be sure to capture an Armordillo Pop here,
because the ability to generate additional items on every floor greatly increases the chance of clearing the dungeon.
Possession Staves are much more common than Monster Pots, and will still let you collect items for 2 floors.

09~12F

The first major grinding area of this dungeon.
Nigiri Morph (9~10F), Mixer (9~15F), and Mixermon (12~20F) make an appearance,
along with Boy Cart (7~9F), Mr. Helico (10F), Taishodon (10~12F), and Kengo (11~12F).
How you utilize these monsters will play a large role in how the rest of the adventure progresses.

Food Related (Nigiri Morph + Mixer)

The reason to look forward to Nirigi Morph is for its ability to produce Large Riceballs.
Have it transform items like single arrows, Spoiled Riceballs, or other unneeded items.

At the same time, you can syntheize items by throwing them at a Mixer.
Some like to secure a food source by synthesizing the 飯 seal onto a weapon by throwing a Large Riceball
they obtain from a Nigiri Morph, which provides a 4% chance for defeated monsters to drop a Riceball,
which can be stacked to increase the drop rate. (4%, 5%, 6%, ... up to 19% at 16 seals).

However, since monsters don't always use their special attacks, you'll likely need to be able to tank some hits.
The ideal method would be to eat Sight Grass and use a Poison Trap to drop their strength to 0.
Miss Staff can also be used, and if it's a Nigiri Morph, you can throw Poison Grass to lower its attack and slow it down.
If you don't have any of these items, you can try to transform one item at a time,
stepping in place to recover HP while you wait for the next monster.

Sight Grass is not only useful for finding Poison Traps, but also to reveal Pitfalls that send you to the next floor.
However, don't eat unidentified grasses to search for it unless Herb and Life Grass have already been identified.
If you don't have Sight Grass, you can swing your weapon inside rooms to check for traps one tile at a time.
Or if that's too much trouble, you can dash across rows of tiles to search for a Poison Trap.
Just keep in mind, you'll lose 1 strength if you step on one without a Scale Shield equipped.

If a Trader isn't present on the current floor, and you've either eaten Sight Grass or have checked for traps,
you can place important items on the ground to avoid having them turned into Large Riceballs while you explore.
Don't place them near Landmines or Pitfalls, though, since a Tiger Tosser may cause an accident.

It's ideal to make riceballs and synthesize on 9F, because 10F has a set terrain pattern of either 8-rooms or 12-rooms,
making for very small room sizes and lots of hallways, along with new monsters that have stronger attacks.

Monsters

It's best not to dash inside rooms on these floors so that monsters don't get to act before you.
Straw Voodoll (9~10F) can curse your shield, making synthesis difficult.
You can block curses with a Holy Shield, or by having the 祓 seal synthesized onto your shield from a Purify Scroll.
Worst case scenario, you can have Kengo unequip a cursed shield between 11~12F.

Monsters begin to hit harder from 10F with the appearance of monsters like Ironhead, Golem, and Kengo (11~12F).
Either unequip your shield beforehand, or fight Kengo with your back against a wall to avoid losing your shield.

Try to clear out Monster Houses when possible to obtain more items.
However, Special Monster Houses can appear starting on 12F, so avoid them if you see one.
Encountering a Porkton or Typhoon this early in the dungeon would ensure the end of your adventure.

Other Tips

Arrow Traps and Iron Arrow Traps only appear between 5~10F. Use them to collect arrows while you can.

Try reading a Wall Collapse Scroll on 9F after you explore the map and defeat some monsters.
You might find a Golden Stairs, and you can also collect Iron Arrows from a Boy Cart (7~9F).
Eat Sight Grass to remove the risk of stepping on a Pitfall on the way to the Golden Stairs.
Prepare Large Riceballs using a Nigiri Morph before entering the Golden Interval to avoid collapsing from hunger.

Mr. Helico makes an appearance on 10F and occasionally drops Happy Grass when defeated.
Don't eat the Happy Grass right away if you obtain one, but instead, throw it at another Mr. Helico
to level it up into a Mr. Gyro, which has a chance to drop Revival Grass when defeated.
Don't forget to assign a name to the unidentified Happy Grass or Revival Grass if you manage to obtain them.
If you have a Possession Staff, Happy Grass can also be used to level up Kengo, allowing you to obtain more equipment.

Taishodon appears between 10~12F. If you have a Grass Tosser, go ahead and insert Strength Grass
or Happy Grass and swing it at a Taishodon to have it reflect the magic bolt back at Shiren.
Don't forget to eat a Boost Grass beforehand, if you happen to have one on hand.

Kengo (11~12F) can be troublesome as an emeny, but they're an excellent monster that can be used to collect
shields if you have a Possession Staff or Monster Pot. However, you'll likely want to level Kengo up into an Iai first
so that you can also collect weapons, along with having higher stats so they won't be defeated as easily.
You could also go for Shihan instead of Iai in hopes of obtaining a Scout Bracelet.

13~15F

Monsters include Wolf Droid (13~15F), Pop Tank (13~15F), Water Strider (13~15F), and Bored Kappa (14~19F).
Bombs and items can fly at you from outside of view when you're in a room, so never let your guard down.
Don't let a monster defeat another one with explosion damage, resulting in Gorilla Bot, Bear Borg, or Cranky Tank.

Hurry through the floor to pick up items before Bored Kappas and Water Striders have a chance to pick them all up.
Seal Staff can prevent Water Striders from throwing their items, and Swap Staff is effective against Bored Kappas.
If you don't have any staves, items like Decoy and Slumber scrolls can be used to save items.

Cranky Tank can be sealed by splashing water on it from a Water Pot, and has very low physical attack strength,
so intentionally level Pop Tanks into Cranky Tanks for 2300 experience each if you have a Water Pot or Seal Staff.
It's also possible to defeat Cranky Tank by throwing 950 or more Gitan at it, if you don't have a Water Pot.

Gorilla Bot and Bear Borg have high attack strength, so sealing them won't do much in terms of weakening them.
The best way to deal with them would be to swing an Unlucky Staff, or throw either a Twisty Grass or Unlucky Seed.

Twisty Hani (14~19F) provides 300 experience when defeated, and has a 35% item drop rate.
However, it has 50 HP and can lower Shiren's level by 1 each time it dances, so be careful when hunting them.
It's best to have a Level Lock Bracelet equipped, but items like Seal Staff and Drain Buster can also be used.
Schubell (15~24F) can be utilitzed to summon Twisty Hanis for experience and items, which will be explained later.

Both Mixer and Mixermon appear between 12~15F, providing many synthesis opportunities.
Monsters with high item drops rates also appear, including Shaaga (16%), Goat Pastor (16%), Bored Kappa (25%),
and Bone Dragon (32%), so it's good to linger until the wind blows using the riceballs you obtained earlier
in hopes of obtaining useful items that can be synthesized.

However, Monster Houses between 13~15F are dangerous, since they can result in Pop Tanks leveling up
into Cranky Tanks, Twisty Hanis waking up, items being thrown, etc.

16~21F

Bored Kappas and Twisty Hanis continue to appear until 19F, and new monsters include Whirlwind (16~18F),
Gyadon (17~18F), Chainhead (17~19F), Rock Beast (18~21F), Tiger Hurler and Patient Bag (19~21F).

Whirlwind moves at double speed, so if your shield's upgrade value is low or lacks 弟 seals, you'll have a hard time.
It can potentially level up into a Kamikaze or Typhoon if it defeats Snacky as well, creating a major problem.
Kamikaze moves at triple speed, so you'll want to avoid hallways unless you have a Scout Bracelet.
Typhoons move at triple speed and can pass through walls, so again, hallways should be avoided.
It's best to wait for them in the middle of a room, and use items to deal with them.

Gyadon can pluck a staff from your inventory and lower its number of uses by 1.
You can protect your staves by keeping them inside pots, but avoid inserting them into a Degrade Pot.
If a staff is already at 0 uses such as a Balance Staff, there's no need to keep them inside a pot.
They only have 40 HP, so you should be able to defeat them in one hit if you've increased your strength.

Avoid moving in ways which allow Chainheads to hit other monsters, since Gigahead has a
ridiculously high attack stat and can be difficult to deal with if a Chainhead levels up into one.
Rock Beast can paralyze Shiren for 3 turns, sometimes resulting in a collapse if there are other monsters nearby.

Tiger Hurler can throw Shiren onto hidden traps, and can also potentially throw Shiren into a Monster House.
Use arrows or seal it from a distance if possible to avoid giving it a chance to use its special attack.

Patient Bag (120 HP) stops moving when its HP drops below 31, and explodes once its HP drops below 11.
The explosion has the power of a Big Landmine, so use arrows or punches to make sure it doesn't explode.
That said, a Patient Bag's explosion can be used to one-shot monsters.

22~29F

Monsters include Monarch Tusker (22~26F), Dragon (22~28F), Demon Scorpion (22~29F), Mixermon (26~40F).
This floor range is among the most difficult sections of this dungeon, with some players going as far as saying
the rest of the dungeon is smooth sailing until 99F in comparison.

Monsters (22~24F)

Demon Scorpion only slow Shiren when it's next to him, so 1-on-1 fights are relatively safe if you defeat it in 2 hits.
However, Monarch Tusker can slow you from a distance if you're lined up with it, and can swing its staff again
to paralyze you, often leading to a collapse if there are other monsters nearby.

The ideal solution would be to equip a shield with the 山 (Echo Shield) seal to reflect its magic,
but it's quite rare to have obtained and synthesized one at this point in the dungeon.
Other seals like 弟 (Otogiriso), バ (Counter Shield), 眠 (Slumber Scroll) offer some defense when paralyzed.
Even a simple 1-on-1 fight with a Monarch Tusker can be dangerous, so don't hesitate to use items.
Good items to use include Seal Staff, Decoy Scroll, Slumber Scroll, Swift Grass, and Warp Grass.
The ド seal (Drain Buster) is also effective against Demon Scorpions, since it's a Drain type monster.

Keep an eye on your remaining HP when fighting Dragons (and Sky Dragons from 25F).
It may take 4~5 hits to defeat one if you haven't increased your strength / haven't upgraded your weapon.
Use items like Otogiriso when your HP gets low, and equip a Dragon Killer if you obtained one in a Golden Interval.
They have a high item drop rate, but it's often not worth the risk if you don't have the strength to defeat them quickly.

Monsters (25~29F)

Monsters include Super Gazer, Stone Golem, Skull Dragon, Sky Dragon, Tonosamadon, and Explosion Ninja.
Monster stats ramp up here, and it can take at least 3 hits to defeat any of them without increased strength.
Super Gazer's hypnosis adds to the already troublesome Monarch Tusker and Sky Dragon.
It's best to take each fight carefully, as you're likely to be worn down if you simply try to muscle your way through.

Tonosamadon reflects projectiles and thrown items, so never shoot arrows or throw stones at it.
A reflected projectile can deal upwards of 100+ damage based on Shiren's level and strengh.
Explosion Ninja places a Big Landmine on the ground when defeated, which can be used to one-shot monsters.
However, you'll take damage equal to 3/4 of your current HP, so it's good to eat an Otogiriso after stepping on one.

All said and done, this floor range isn't too bad if you've sufficiently upgraded your weapon and shield.
Mixermons make a second appearance between 26~40F, so synthesize and strengthen your equipment again.
If you feel like your equipment is underpowered, go ahead and skip exploring 25F where Mixermon doesn't appear.

Regarding the Golden Interval

There's a 30% chance for Golden Stairs to be generated inside a wall between 24~29F,
so read Wall Collapse Scrolls if you have them after you've explored the floor and defeated some monsters.
There's a pretty decent chance of entering a Golden Interval if you managed to save up 2~3 scrolls,
and it's best to read them between 26~29F so that you can make use of Mixermons beforehand.
You'll also want to read them while on top of the normal stairs in case a Sky Dragon is present.

Lashaaga (normally found on 70F+) appears from 3F inside this Golden Interval, so use items to deal with them.
See the Golden Interval section for more details.

30~39F

Monsters

Monsters include Armordillo Pop (30~39F), Orcan (30~43F), Archdragon (30~59F), Adm. Spearfish (30~59F),
and Rancor Slug (32~40F) among others.

Archdragon can breathe fire that deals 35 damage from anywhere on the current floor, even if you're inside a wall.
A shield with 竜 (Dragon Shield), 火 (Flame Grass / Dragon Grass), and multiple 弟 seals greatly reduces fire damage.
That said, the easiest way to deal with it would be to thrown an Extinction Scroll at it.
Its has a low spawn rate and is often generated while sleeping, so you might not encounter very many if you're lucky.

Rancor Slug not only lowers strength, but can also divide when it takes damage, causing another one to be generated.
The second half of the dungeon will be quite difficult if your strength gets lowered here, so be sure to equip
a Salty Bracelet if you have one, and use items like Seal Staff if you haven't found the bracelet yet.

Waterways are more common from this point, along with amphibian element monsters like Orcan and Adm. Spearfish.
Armordillo Pop also makes an appearance, so don't let your guard down if you don't have a Balance Staff.
You really don't want to drop items in water or have pots shatter as a result of being tripped.
If you're worried about item loss, simply cut the exploration short and proceed to the next floor.

Orcans generally behave in the same manner as the Spearfish family, and can restore HP when its underwater.
However, it can perform a jumping attack that deals x2 damage when it's underwater,
so avoid walking through hallways surrounded by water when your HP is low.
Use arrows to defeat it when it tries to run away to heal.

Preparing for the Second Half

Monster attack strength is generally calmer here compared to 22~29F, and Mixermon continues to appear,
so take some time to collect items, synthesize equipment, and gain experience points.
You can likely get by fine with the minimum of 回 (Healing Sword) and 三 (Razor Wind) seals,
so it's a good idea to linger on floors until the wind blows if you have the means to keep hunger at bay.

That said, the monster table between 31~39F swaps a previous monster out for a new one on each floor,
so monsters will gradually get stronger the further you progress on this floor range. (Monsters section for details).
Nashaaga (35~43F) may not be as strong as Lashaaga which were inside the second Golden Interval,
but they're still among the top threats on this floor range with their high stats and ability to attack twice.
If you let your guard down thinking Archdragon is the only real threat, you might find yourself losing strength
from slug licks, or losing 1-on-1 fights against the stronger enemies in the second half of this floor range.

Monster Pots are more common between 30~59F. (about x8 chance compared to other floors).
If you find one, be sure to capture an Armordillo Pop so that you can collect additional items.
Also remember that you can use a Possession Staff on an Armordillo Pop to collect items too.
Just be careful of Gyairas (30~49F), which can pluck pots and reduce the size by 1,
effectively ruining a Monster Pot if the ally monster was outside of it.

Shopkeepers level up to Asst. Managers starting on 30F.
Asst. Managers reflect magic, so keep that in mind if you plan on using staves to steal from shops.
You can use this to your advantage by swinging a Grass Tosser at an Asst. Manager to increase your strength.

40~49F

Monsters include Intolerable Slug (39~47F), Iai (43~55F), and Nigiri Master (45~57F).

Intolerable Slug lowers your max strength by 3 per lick, and can also divide, creating additional copies of itself.
Be sure to equip a Salty Bracelet or Scout Bracelet so that you don't get licked by surprise in hallways.
Both Rancor Slug and Intolerable Slug appear between 39~40F, so hurry to the stairs if you want to be safe.
Keep Warp Grass or Decoy Scroll on hand in case you step on a Summon Trap and get surrounded by slugs.

Escargon (40~80F) can warp in front of other monsters and reflect magic bolts from staves back at Shiren.
You can swing a Grass Tosser at one to give yourself a boost using Strength Grass or Happy Grass.

Monsters with decent stats begin to appear all at once from 40F, including Nailed Voodoll, Orcada, and Sea Dragon.
You might find the sudden change of the monster table more difficult than the gradual change of 31~39F.

As always, it's best to fight Iai with your back facing a wall since you likely won't be able to defeat it in one hit.
Losing your equipment here could very well be the end of the run, so always prioritize moving in a way
that guarantees safety of flung equipment, even if you have to take a hit in order to do so.
There's a chance a defeated Iai will drop a Kengo's Katana, but it doesn't have much use.

Nigiri Master can transform Shiren into a riceball, lowering his defense to 0.
It can also transform items into riceballs if Shiren is already a riceball, so seal it or use projectiles from a distance
before it gets a chance to use its special attack. A riceball can be thrown to one-shot it, if needed.

Devil Kangaroo (30~59F) has a chance to drop an Invisible Staff when it's defeated,
which can be used to prevent a monster from using its special attack while the monster is invisible.
It's possible to lower the level of a Nigiri Master to make more riceballs, but the risk is greater than the payoff.

50~59F

The high risk, high reward area of this dungeon. How the second half goes typically comes down to whether or not
you managed to get through this area without accidents, and if you were able to successfully obtain items like
Strength Grass and Iron Arrows using Schubell and Menbell, along with hunting Mr. Gyro for Revival Grass.

Monsters

This floor range has a variety of extremely troublesome monsters, including Iai (43~55F), Nigiri Master (45~57F),
Monsoon Doll (49~59F), Gyandora (50~80F), Tiger Chucker (51~95F), and Spiked Voodoll (50~59F).
Tiny Phoenix (50~75F) can heal other monsters, making it harder to defeat them quickly as well.

Monsoon Doll can erase a seal from your equipped weapon or shield with its special attack.
Losing key seals like 回 or 山 will quickly turn the tide of your adventure for the worse, so you'll want to use
arrows to defeat it from a distance, or use an item like Seal Staff to prevent it from using its special attack.
On that note, only swing a staff at it when it's right next to you, to prevent Escargon from reflecting the magic bolt.
It always erases a red 金 seal first, so you could intentionally have it erase that seal to free up a slot, if desired.

Gyandora can pluck equipment or a pot from your inventory, lowering synthesis slots or pot size by 1.
This enemy alone is why you need the ト (Walrus Shield) seal to block monsters from stealing your items.
If you don't have the ト seal, avoid dashing, and shoot arrows in hallways to try and detect upcoming monsters.
Note that the contents of an occupied seal slot or full pot will vanish when it gets plucked.

Tiger Chucker can block off escape routes, throw ally monsters onto landmines, or Shiren into a Monster House, etc.
It's best to use arrows to defeat it from a distance, or swing a Seal Staff if you meet one in a hallway.
Tiger Chucker will be present for most of the dungeon from this point, all the way until 95F.

Obtaining More Items

Despite the scary lineup of monsters, this floor range also offers some of the best opportunities to obtain items.

Mixergon (50~80F) makes an appearance, allowing you to synthesize items again.
However, a Mixergon that has swallowed 4 items is very powerful, so use Slow, Miss, or Quarter staves to weaken it.
Also, a Copycat Mask family monster could be disguised as a Mixergon on any floor, so don't throw your equipment first.
You can determine if it's a real Mixergon by shooting an arrow (swallows the arrow), or by swinging a Knowledge Staff.
It's possible to remove curses before or after synthesis without a Purify Scroll by posessing a Spiked Voodoll.

Menbell (53~70F) makes an appearance, and is the key to collecting additional items.
Use Menbell to obtain Iron Arrows, and lower its level to obtain Strength Grass from Mr. Drone. (see next section)
You might get lucky and obtain Revival Grass from a passing Mr. Gyro while hunting summoned monsters, too.

Mr. Gyro (50~59F) makes an appearance, and has a 75% chance to drop Revival Grass when defeated.
Chase it down if you see one, as stocking up on Revival Grass greatly increases the chance of clearing this dungeon.
Mr. Gyro and Menbell overlap between 53~60F, so it's a great opportunity to hunt both Mr. Drones and Mr. Gyros.

Cranky Tank (55~77F) makes an appearance, and deals a fixed 20 damage per cannonball explosion.
Bracelets have a higher chance of cracking from explosion damage, so be sure to unequip important ones,
especially if you don't have any Plating Scrolls on hand to repair cracked bracelets.
That said, bracelets never shatter from explosion damage, so you don't need to unequip cracked bracelets.
You can level down a Cranky Tank into a Boy Cart to collect Iron Arrows, if desired.

60~69F

Projectile zone where Silver Arrows, Stones, and Bombs can fly at you from every direction.

Monsters include Horse Shogun (57~73F), Horse Emperor (60~77F), Porkchop (56~71F), Porkton (60~72F),
Gorilla Bot (60~74F), Bear Borg (60~76F), Menbell (53~70F), and Bellthoven (60~75F).

Archdragon finally exits the monster table from 60F, but Porkchop and Porkton appear,
so the threat of getting hit by a ranged attack from outside of view persists.
The amount of damage you take from stones can rival Archdragon fire, depending on your shield's strength.
It's common to get caught in hallways where you can't heal easily using the 回 seal. If that happens,
move toward the source of the throw using a Tunnel Staff or Pickaxe as needed to try and defeat the monster quickly.
Expect to use some Revival Grasses from stone tosses, especially if you don't walk in an optimal manner.
Lastly, stone tosses won't damage bracelets, so you can keep them equipped while you move toward the source.

Watch out for Hyper Gazer (63~90F) if you don't have the 山 seal (Echo Shield).
In particular, its hypnosis could result in forcing Shiren to eat valuable Revival Grasses.

The risk of item loss is lower here, since monsters like Iai, Nigiri Master, and Monsoon Dolls no long appear.
However, Gyandora and Tiger Chucker continue to make an appearance, and Hyper Gazer is a new addition,
so if you only focus on Porkchop and Porkton, you might find yourself being hypnotized or tossed across the map,
or your equipment getting ruined by Gyandora and King Shotfish (60~99F).

70~85F

Monsters that excel at close-range combat make an appearance this time,
including Garm Dragon, Orcaon, Hakkeyoi, Gottsuan, along with the ones mentioned below.

Lashaaga (70~87F) attacks three times per turn, dealing lots of damage and quickly shattering bracelets.
It's possible to weaken its attacks by splashing water on it using a Water Pot, since it has the Weapon element.
Lashaaga and Typhoon have the same attack stat, and both attack 3 times per turn, so fighting Lashaaga
gives you an idea how you'll fare against Typhoon later on.

Shihan (74~90F) makes an appearance. Losing your weapon or shield here likely means the end of your run,
so prioritize moving in ways that prevents your equipment from getting flung, using staves as needed.
It's good to keep Evaporate, Freeze, and Wall Collapse scrolls on hand in case your equipment falls in water.
Consider throwing an Extiction Scroll at Shihan if you have one on hand.
If you don't have any of these items, it's recommended to rush to the stairs to minimize risk.

Boulder Beast (75~89F) can tackle Shiren, knocking him away and paralyzing him for 3 turns.
Diagonal knockback won't break walls, leaving you paralyzed in the open. It's even worse if a Shihan is nearby.
You'll want to avoid fighting it inside shops, in case you're sent outside of the shop while holding merchandise.

The classic power type monster of the series, Minotaur (78~85F), also makes an appearance.
It has a 25% chance to deal a x1.4 damage critical hit, but can be weakened by splashing water using a Water Pot.
Mixergon appears alongside Minotaur between 78~80F, so you might be able to synthesize a Minotaur's Axe
if you're lucky enough to get the drop (10% chance), greatly increasing your damage output.
However, it might not be worth the trouble of using items such as Synthesis Pot, Blank Scroll, and Purify Scroll
to synthesize it, if you have enough strength already or have a good number of staves on hand.

Mixergons won't be available past 80F, so wrap up any synthesis you need before proceeding past that floor.

N'dubba 60yr (80~94F) moves at double speed, and hits as hard as Gigahead or Megataur when both hits connect.
It might be best to assume all items on the floor are N'dubbas at this point, to avoid getting hit by surprise attacks.
Safely check for N'dubbas by having an ally monster stand on top of an item, and then switching places with it.

The merchandise table for shops changes from 71F. Be extremely cautious of unidentified grasses from this point.
A 400G grass could be a Disaster Seed, which lowers your level back down to 1, likely ending your adventure.
Upgrade Pot can now be seen in shops, so go ahead and read Restock Scrolls if you're trying to obtain one.
However, you might want to see if you come across a Pot Specialty Shop before doing so.
Also keep in mind that shops can only be found until 90F inside this dungeon.

86~99F

Monsters include Gigahead (84~99F), and Typhoon, Megataur, Emperor Tusker, Stun Scorpion (86~99F).

The final stretch. Highly recommended to rush to the stairs on each floor, unless you're aiming for a Golden Interval.
Don't hesitate to use items now, since this floor range is why you were saving them to begin with.
Many of the monsters here use regular attacks, so seals like バ and 眠 will have a chance to shine.

Pass turns inside a room to check for Typhoons before entering a hallway when you begin a new floor.
If you have a Phantasm Bracelet, go ahead throw an Extinction Scroll at a Typhoon.
Splashing water from a Water Pot can weaken both Megataur and Typhoon's attacks.
Typhoon can be used to quickly restore HP with enough Water Pot uses, provided you have 弟 seals on your shield.
If you do decide to drag a weakened Typhoon around as a healer, you'll want to watch out for Shihan until 90F.

Megataur has incredibly high HP, attack, and defense, and can take 5~7 hits to defeat if you have low strength.
Use items like Slow Staff or Miss Staff to deal with them, rather than items like Seal Staff or Unlucky Staff.

Emperor Tusker can reduce your current HP to 1/4 of its current value with one swing of its staff.
Don't hesitate to use a Seal Staff if you don't have the 山 (Echo Shield) seal synthesized.
If it drops a Quarter Staff (10% chance), it's best to use it against Megataurs and Stun Scorpions.

Stun Scorpion can paralyze you for 3 turns with its special attack. You might lose even in a 1-on-1 fight
if you become paralyzed, so don't let your guard down just because other monsters aren't nearby.
Note that monsters will have 100% accuracy when they attack Shiren when he is paralyzed,
even when they are under the effect of a Miss Staff.

Walk diagonally inside rooms when you see a red dot but haven't identified what monster is present,
since it could be a Gigahead or Emperor Tusker that can attack when you're lined up with it.
You made it this far, so do everything you can to try and stay alive.

86~90F

The monster table still includes Hyper Gazer and Shihan, so consider waiting until 91F
before throwing an Extinction Scroll so that you don't indirectly increase their appearance rate.
If you don't have the 山 (Echo Shield) seal, you'll want to be extra careful around Hyper Gazers.

King Tusker (86~89F) and Monarch Tusker (88~90F) make a brief appearance.
They're weak on their own, but can cause problems if Shiren is sealed or slowed near stronger monsters.

Shops no longer appear after 90F, so keep that in mind if you're aiming for an Upgrade Pot,
Disaster Seed, or an 8-digit score using bags of 1,000,000 Gitan.

90F+

Cave Mamel (90~99F) shouldn't be too threatening, since it has lower defense in Shiren 2 compared to other games.

Shihan and Hyper Gazer no longer appear from 91F, so it's a good time to throw an Extinction Scroll at a monster.
The monster you choose should be based on your shield's strength and seals.

As you progress further into this floor range, other monsters including Lashaaga, Boulder Beast, Orcaon,
and Gottsuan exit the monster table, effectively increasing the appearance rate of other end game monsters
like Megataur, Gigahead, Stun Scorpion, Typhoon, and Emperor Tusker. Even regular Monster Houses
are terrifying at that point, so try to find ways around them if you come across any.

There's a 30% chance for Golden Stairs to be generated inside a wall between 90~95F, so don't forget to
read Wall Collapse Scrolls if you're aiming to obtain a Mijinha or Basilisk Shield to complete the gallery.
You could transfer your main weapon or shield to either option for fun, if you have a Synthesis Pot on hand.

The End

Once you descend the stairs on 99F, you'll arrive at a new location unlike any other in the game.
A written description of the area wouldn't be able to fully capture the sight, so it's best to experience it yourself.
You'll also obtain a clear icon, special reward, and NPCs inside the udon restaurant will have new dialogue.

Golden Interval

Notes that apply to all three Golden Intervals
  • Stronger monsters appear, so be careful not to collapse.
  • Pitfalls and Flood Traps don't get generated.
  • Food and Grass items cannot be found on the floor, so be careful of hunger.
    • Defeat monsters using a weapon with the 飯 (Riceball) seal to obtain food inside Golden Intervals.
  • Water Pots cannot be refilled.
    • Techniques involving splashing water on Blank Scrolls or monsters have a limited number of uses.
    • Think carefully before splashing water, especially against Lashaaga and Megataur.
  • Insert Gitan into Storage Pots, and use Gitan as a projectile to deal with troublesome monsters.
  • Most items found inside Golden Intervals can be sold for a large amount of Gitan.
  • The item table may be small, but powerful items can be found.
    • Dragon Killer and Dragon Shield are exclusive to Golden Intervals.
  • Arrow Traps can be generated, meaning they can be used to replenish arrows.

First (6~10F, Return 11F)

It's rare to find Wall Collapse Scrolls this early in the dungeon, so you're unlikely to visit this Golden Interval.
That said, if you do get lucky, be sure to explore the entire map on each floor to collect all items.

The monster table changes from 3F and includes numerous monsters with ranged attacks.
Snacky can also appear, often causing a Horse Ronin or Pop Tank to level up into a Horse Samurai or Cranky Tank.
Store Gitan inside of a Storage Pot, and use the Gitan as a projectile to deal with leveled up monsters.
You could try to intentionally level one of those monsters up as well, to increase the amount of experience you gain.

Dragon Killer and Dragon Shield can only be found in Golden Intervals, and are both powerful pieces of equipment.
Dragon Killer surpasses Dotanuki with its 15 base attack and 7 slots, and should be used as a main weapon.
Ryujin Sword can be synthesized using 4 Dragon Killers as well, and is the strongest main weapon in this dungeon.
If you have a Possession Staff, you can create an Iai on 3~4F and use its special attack to obtain more Dragon Killers.

However, note that you'll skip past important floors if you enter this Golden Interval too early:

  • 8~9F - Have Nigiri Morph make Large Riceballs for you.
  • 7~9F - Hunt Mr. Drones for Strength Grass drops.
  • 9~10F - Synthesize items using Mixers. (That said, Mixer appears until 15F so it's not too big of a loss).

So the downsides of entering this Golden Interval early cannot be ignored.
In particular, there's a high chance of collapsing from starvation if you enter it before making Large Riceballs on 8F.
You'll always be returned to 11F when you exit the Golden Interval, no matter what floor you entered it on.
So it's best to enter the Golden Interval on either 9F or 10F to limit the downsides as much as possible.

Lastly, Heaven Scrolls are common, so you can get by with a Dried Bonito if you have one on hand.
Simply read a Heaven Scroll while it's equipped to increase its upgrade value and number of bites by 1.
It's a little wasteful, but it's a technique that can be used without having to synthesize a Disposable Sword,
and you can make up for the missed upgrade values on your main weapon by raising your Strength later on.

Second (24~29F, Return 30F)

Golden Interval that can have a drastic effect on how the rest of the dungeon progresses.
In particular, this is the last chance to obtain equipment effective against Dragon type monsters.
Archdragon will likely give you trouble if you fail to obtain a Dragon Shield and don't have an Extinction Scroll.

Dragon, Sky Dragon, and Skull Dragon appear on 1~2F, and Lashaaga is added to the monster table on 3~4F.
Lashaaga normally appears on 70F+ inside this dungeon, and is far stronger than anything you've seen so far.
Keep multiple bags of 1000+ Gitan inside a Storage Pot to use as a projectile to one-shot it,
or use an item such as Miss Staff to weaken it first before using regular attacks.
You might have a chance of defeating it without items if you have a Dragon Killer,
along with a shield with the 見 (Evasive Shield) and 眠 (Slumber Scroll) seals.
If you don't have a way to deal with Lashaaga, it's best to hurry through 3~4F to limit the number of encounters.

The item table is the same as the first Golden Interval. Surprise Pots can contain items from multiple categories.
Lashaaga doesn't spawn on 1~2F, so you can safely linger for experience points and additional item drops.
An ally Armordillo Pop or Iai can be used to gather Dragon Killers for a Ryujin Sword as well.

Dragon and Sky Dragon have high item drop rates, so you're very likely to obtain additional items if you linger.
Monster traps can be used to generate more monsters, and a Sanctuary Scroll can be placed on the ground
on 3~4F to safely hunt monsters until the wind blows.
Arrow traps can be found in this Golden Interval, so it's a good idea to gather arrows if you happen to find one.

High Score

Score = (Floor - 1) * 5000 + Gitan + Selling price of items in inventory + Clear Bonus (5000 for Road to the End)
  • Steal from shops and make as many 1,000,000 Gitan bags as you can.
  • Gather as many vouchers as you can from Traders to help with stealing or reselling at a profit.
  • Synthesize expensive equipment, such as Wonder Pick, Nirvana Shield, Daisangen, Helix Shield, etc.
    • Dragon Killers can be obtained using an ally Armordillo or Iai monster inside a Golden Interval.
    • Pickaxes and Heavy Shields can be obtained using an ally Armordillo or Kengo family monster.
    • Dragon Tile Bracelets require many Restock Scrolls gathered using an Armordillo family monster.
    • Helix Shield is possible on paper, but Fuuma Shield is very rare, and it also needs to be
      upgraded to +99, so it's not realistic to aim for it. That said, it might be a fun challenge.
    • Kaburasutegi and Fiery Fuuma require a blacksmith, so they cannot be synthesized in this dungeon.

Speed Running

  • Healing Sword, Scout Bracelet, and Phantasm Bracelet are required.
    • Unequip Phantasm Bracelet over water or inside a wall to warp to a different room.
  • Shiren 2 doesn't have items that reveal where the stairs is, so you have to rely on intuition and luck.
    • Wall Collapse Scrolls can be used, but a negative statue effect could lead to time loss or a collapse.
    • It's best to defeat monsters from inside walls and search for the stairs like normal.
  • There's no time to upgrade equipment, so focus on seals instead of finding a good main weapon or shield.
    • Sword: 回 (Healing Sword), 三 (Razor Wind), 弟 (Otogiriso)
    • Shield: 山 (Echo Shield), 皮 (Leather Shield), 見 (Evasive Shield)
  • It's best to have 3 Extinction Scrolls. Use them in the order of Archdragon → Cranky Tank → Typhoon.
    • These three monsters have long attack animations, leading to time loss.
    • Extinction Scrolls can only be found in shops, so you typically use Blank:Extinction scrolls.
    • Typhoon can quickly shatter Phantasm Bracelets, so have at least 1 scroll on hand proceeding to 86F.
    • If you don't have enough scrolls, skip using one on Archdragon, since it's often generated asleep.
  • Use standard power up techniques between 20~30F so that you can rush through the second half smoothly.
    • Phantasm Bracelet can cover the lack of defense, so focus on weapon seals and increasing strength.
