Road To The End

The ultimate gameplay experience of Shiren 2.
Most items and monsters appear in this dungeon, and
bracelets, grasses, staves, pots, and scrolls are found unidentified.

Each run of this dungeon can play out in many different ways depending on
the items you find, the actions you take, the knowledge and strategies you use,
and of course, last but not least, how good your luck is.

The difficulty is on the high end, thanks to a carefully balanced monster table.
In particular, monsters like Megataur, Gigahead, Emperor Tusker, Typhoon,
and Stun Scorpion appear from 86~99F, often making for an intense final stretch.

If you successfully make it through to the end, you'll find yourself arriving at a strange
new area unlike any other location in the game, and your save file will gain a Shiren icon.

Side note: Escape Scrolls can be found on 30F, 50F, and 70F.

Important Items

Key items that greatly increase the clear rate:

  • Healing Sword (Synthesize Herb, Otogiriso, and Life Grass onto a weapon)
  • Scout Bracelet
  • Phantasm Bracelet (Shop, Trader, Presto Pot)
  • Blank Scroll
  • Extinction Scroll (Shop)
  • Wall Collapse Scroll
  • Monster Pot
  • Posession Staff
  • Strength Grass, Boost Grass, Grass Gain Staff, Grass Toss Staff
  • Revival Grass

Scout Bracelet is the single most effective item in the game to lower the difficulty of a dungeon.
Use it to avoid surprise attacks in hallways, and to identify shops or Monster Houses at a glance.
Unequip it before fighting monsters to help prevent it from cracking.

Phantasm Bracelet rivals Scout Bracelet in utility once you master how to use it.
It can be used to attack from inside walls, take shortcuts, or warp to a different room by unequipping it inside a wall.
It's rare to obtain both bracelets in a run, but Phantasm Bracelet and Scout Bracelet play well together as a set too.

Blank Scroll can be turned into a Blank:Extinction scroll to banish monsters like Archdragon and Typhoon,
or into a Blank:Sanctuary that can be reused by splashing water on it from a Water Pot,
or use it as synthesis fodder to increase an equipment's slot count, etc.
Side note: Extinction Scroll is a shop-exlusive item in Road to the End.

Wall Collapse Scroll is mainly used to search for Golden Stairs leading to Golden Intervals.
The items you find inside Golden Intervals can be of great help on your descent to 99F.
The first Golden Interval between 6~10F is usually skipped, due to food shortages.
The second one can be found between 24~29F, and the last one between 90~95F.
Keep 6 Wall Collapse Scrolls on hand for the third one if you're looking for a Mijinha.

Monster Pots can be used to capture an Armordillo Pop, which can be used to obtain a ridiculous amount of items.
If you find one early in the dungeon, level up an Armordillo Bro (5~6F) using an item like Happy Staff to capture a Pop.
If you don't have a way to level up an Armordillo Bro, you could go ahead and capture the Bro instead.
However, you'll need to be extra careful with how you handle it to keep it alive.
Watch out for things like Nigiri Morph's special attack and Trip Stones so that you don't lose the Monster Pot.

If you find a Monster Pot after 6F, you could wait until Armordillo Pop can be found naturally between 30~39F,
or capture a different monster like Shihan, Monsoon Doll, Hyper Gazer, Gazelle Pope, Water Shinryu,
Stun Scorpion, Emperor Tusker, or Tiny Phoenix. Shihan is likely the second choice, as it can hold its own
in fights thanks to its high stats, and can be used to obtain Scout Bracelets and other equipment.

Posession Staff can be used to level monsters (have the target monster defeat the monster you're controlling)
and obtain items (take control of Armordillo or Kengo family monsters), among other uses.
This is often your go-to item to collect items between 5~6F where Armordillos appear.
This staff doesn't cost much (6 uses = 650G), so be sure to buy it if you see it in a shop.

Strength Grass, Boost Grass, and either Grass Gain Staff or Grass Toss Staff
can be used in combination to quickly increase Shiren's strength.
Strength plays a key role in damage calculation in Shiren 2, so it's important to increase it when possible.
Dealing more damage means you'll restore more HP per attack from Healing Sword (回 seal) as well.

Shiren 2 doesn't have pots or scrolls that can restore HP, so it's important
to make use of Otogiriso and Revival Grass in ways that aren't wasteful.

Weapon

Main Weapon

Ryujin Sword is the best possible main weapon, but you likely won't find four Dragon Killers
inside a Golden Interval without using Armordillo or Shihan to obtain more items.
Dragon Killer, Dotanuki, and Spark Sword are all good options. You can make up the lack of attack
by increasing your strength, so it's best to go for a weapon with many synthesis slots instead of raw stats.

Mijinha is technically the strongest obtainable weapon,
but it's only available from 95F, so it's not practical to include it in the list.
Same with Daisangen Sword, since it's unrealistic to obtain 3 Dragon Seal Bracelets in a single run.

Shield

Main Shield

Defense doesn't increase from level ups or strength, so the only way to increase it is to equip stronger shields.
The defense stat in Shiren 2 doesn't play as large of a role as other games in the series at reducing the amount
of damage you take, but despite that, it's still good to aim for high defense to reduce the damage as much as possible.

Shields with high base defense are good for early game, since it's difficult to increase upgrade value at that point.
That said, Fuuma Shield, Ivory Shield, Leather Shield, and Walrus Shield are all good main shield options.
The shield you choose will likely come down to a mix of play style and whatever you happen to find.

Basilisk Shield, Daisangen Shield, and Helix Shield aren't included in the list due to being impractical to obtain.

Bracelet

Scout Bracelet
Bracelet that drastically lowers the difficulty of the dungeon.
Allows you to see the locations of all monsters, items, and statues on the map.
Avoid walking while it's equipped, since you don't want it to crack from stepping on a trap.
If it cracks, be sure to read a Plating Scroll to repair it before it breaks.
Phantasm Bracelet
Powerful bracelet that rivals Scout Bracelet.
Allows you to go inside walls, letting you defeat enemies without being hit and therefore saving items.
Seals like 回 and 弟 should be synthesized onto your weapon to restore HP while inside walls.
Watch out for Archdragon fire and other monsters that can attack inside walls like Pumphantasm and Typhoon.
Can be used to steal, and unequipping it while inside a wall will warp you to a random room on the current floor.
Pierce Bracelet
Bracelet that can be either good or bad, depending on how you use it.
Throw items like Poison Grass or Gitan to weaken or defeat a line of monsters, roll a cannonball
while it's equipped to trigger traps below monsters, clear out a Monster House with arrows, etc.
Keep it unequipped when you don't need its effects to avoid losing valuable items.
Salty Bracelet
Blocks the strength lowering effects of Slug licks, which cannot be blocked by a Scale Shield.
Level Lock Bracelet
Blocks level down effects of Twisty Hani dances and Gazelle Pope spells.
Twisty Hanis have a high item drop rate, so it's nice to have this bracelet when hunting them for items.
Dragon Tile Bracelet
Rare bracelet that is exclusively found in shops between 2~29F.
Can be used with a Trip Stone to collect items, and is also an ingredient for synthesizing Daisangen equipment.

Staff

Grass Gain Staff
Insert Strength Grass to increase max strength.
Other grasses like Angel Seed, Happy Grass, Life Grass, and Warp Grass can be used,
however Strength Grass should be prioritized, since strength is a large factor in Shiren 2's damage calculation.
To get one more use out of a "Strength Gain Staff[0]", throw it at a Tonosamadon to have it reflect the staff,
or use a Bend Bracelet and throw it at a wall so that it curves around and hits Shiren.
Don't forget to eat Boost Grass before swinging it to double the gains.
Grass Toss Staff
Trickier to use than Grass Gain Staff, but can be used in a similar manner by swinging it at monsters that reflect magic.
Such monsters include Taishodon, Escargon, Asst. Manager, and Manager.
Make use of Tiger Tossers or Bomb type monsters to level up a Shopkeeper.
Other grasses like Poison Grass, Twisty Grass, and Warp Grass can be inserted, but Strength Grass should be prioritized.
Miss Staff
Lowers a monster's regular attack accuracy to 0%, making all of their attacks miss.
Monster attacks are guaranteed to connect even if they are under the effect of this staff when Shiren is paralyzed,
so it's not as effective against monsters like Monarch Tusker, Stun Scorpion, Rock Beast, and Boulder Beast.
Doesn't affect the accuracy of projectiles or special attacks.
Fire Staff
Effective against Cave Mamels, leveled up enemies, and monsters in hallways.
Can be used to destroy troublesome statues or scrolls stuck to the ground within 5 tiles.
Use a Knockback Staff after swinging a Fire Staff when an enemy is close to deal 80~100 damage in a single turn.
Quarter Staff
Reduces a monster's HP to 1/4 of its current amount.
Great when fighting monsters with lots of HP, or when you lack offensive power.
Slow Staff
Allows you to defeat enemies without taking damage by looping attack → step back → attack.
Be careful against enemies with ranged attacks, or encountering an enemy in the opposite direction.
Posession Staff
Among the best staves in the game once you master how to use it.
Can be used to level up enemies, guard Shiren while he regenerates HP,
collect items by taking control of Armordillo or Kengo family monsters,
remove curses from items by taking control of a Straw Voodoll family monster,
gather wooden or iron arrows by taking control of a Bow Boy or Bow Cart,
obtain riceballs by taking control of a Nigiri Master, or use it to walk while ignoring traps, etc.
Seal Staff
Prevents a monster from being able to use their special attack.
Nice to have when facing Kengo family monsters, Monsoon Doll, Nigiri Master, Tiger Chucker, etc.

Scroll

Blank Scroll
One of the best items in the game. Can be used as any scroll you've read in the past, including Extinction Scroll.
Extinction Scroll
Makes a monster vanish from the dungeon for the duration of the current adventure.
Some nice options include Archdragon, Cranky Tank, Porkton, Shihan, and Typhoon.
Think carefully before you throw it, and watch out for Copycat Mask monsters disguised as the target monster.
Plating Scroll
Can be used to repair bracelets or add a red 金 seal to equipment.
Sanctuary Scroll
One of these is usually enough to handle an entire Monster House.
However, you still need to watch out for monsters that have ranged attacks.
Slumber Scroll
Great for emergencies, such as when you're surrounded by monsters from stepping on a Summon Trap.
Note that monsters will wake up with increased movement speed after 10 turns.
Also makes for an effective synthesis seal.
Decoy Scroll
Great for emergencies. No downsides like Slumber Scroll, and can be used to evade Archdragon fire.
Note that shadows will fail to appear if the current floor is at the monster/character limit,
and monsters like Ironhead and Cranky Tank can attack through the shadows.
Fear Scroll
Great for emergencies. Limited to a 1 tile radius around Shiren, but effective against enemies inside walls.
Identify Scroll
Particularly nice for this dungeon, since most item categories are found unidentified.
Prioritize grasses first in search of a Life Grass to make a Healing Sword, and then items like bracelets and pots.
Staves and equipment should only be identified using this scroll during the second half of the dungeon.
Restock Scroll
Read it inside shops to restock or change the items for sale.
Save it for shops between 71~90F if you're interested in obtaining an Upgrade Pot.
Wall Collapse Scroll
Used to steal from shops or to search for Golden Stairs.
Recommended to read it on 9~10F, 24~29F, and 90~95F when searching for Golden Stairs.
Can also be used to quickly find a path to the stairs when Archdragon fire is wearing you down.
Items like Talking Seed, Slumber Scroll, Swap Staff, and Seal Staff can be used afterward to secure a path to the stairs.
Escape Scroll
Found on the ground on 30F, 50F, and 70F. However, there's no point in carrying it around if you're aiming for 99F.
That said, it can be nice to have on hand in case you find rare items like a Dragon Tile Bracelet or Upgrade Pot.

Grass

Revival Grass
Fully restores HP, strength, and fullness if you collapse. No effect when it's inside a pot.
Sometimes dropped by Mr. Gyro. Shiren 2 lacks items that fully restore HP, so stock up on as many as possible.
You don't want to waste these, so don't let your guard down or get careless just because you have this in your inventory.
Herb
Healing Sword ingredient. Somewhat rare, so you'll want to identify it by checking prices inside a shop,
or by reading an Identify Scroll on a grass item, as opposed to eating unidentified grasses.
Otogiriso
Restores 100 HP. Healing Sword ingredient, and provides the fantastic 弟 seal when synthesized. Pretty common.
Life Grass
Increases max HP by 5 points, and is also a Healing Sword ingredient.
Somewhat rare, so don't eat unidentified grasses in case one of them happens to be this grass.
Strength Grass
Use it with Boost Grass and Grass Gain Staff to greatly increase Shiren's strength.
Easy to identify, since Mr. Drone always drops this grass.
Boost Grass
Used with Strength Grass and Grass Gain Staff to increase max strength.
Can also be used with Happy Grass, Angel Seed, and Life Grass, but Strength Grass should be prioritized.
Happy Grass
Throw it at a monster early game to gain more experience points. Better to eat it yourself later on.
Can be thrown at a Mixer monster to level it up and increase the number of items it can swallow,
or to create a Mr. Gyro in hopes of it dropping a Revival Grass.
Swift Grass
Nice to have for Monster Houses, or if you're in a room with many monsters and need to escape.
Warp Grass
Allows you to escape from a Monster House even if there's a No Magic Statue present in the room.
Try to keep one on hand at all times.
Antidote Grass
Restores lost strength due to poison arrows, Slug licks, and Scorpion stings.
Be sure to eat one of these before you use Strength Grass so that you increase max strength.
Sight Grass
Best to eat it at the beginning of a floor.
Use it on 9F to look for a Poison Arrow Trap, which can be used to lower Nigiri Morph's strength and obtain riceballs.
Can also be used to look for Pitfalls, or a Trip Stone if you have a Dragon Tile Bracelet and want to collect items.

Pot

Storage Pot
One of the most important items in the game, since it lets you expand your inventory.
Easily identified by looking inside a pot and checking for a sound effect by pressing the L button.
Identify Pot
Somewhat common pot that lets you identify multiple items. Prioritize grasses, bracelets, and staves.
Degrade Pot
It might appear useless at first glance, but it can be used to store riceballs and grasses,
along with unneeded bracelets or other items to sell when you come across a shop.
Grass Effect Pot
Can be used to weaken en entire Monster House by inserting grasses like Twisty Grass or Poison Grass,
or to deal 100 damage to all monsters in a Ghost House by inserting Otogiriso.
Also used as a container for non-grass items.
Presto Pot
Insert unneeded items or single arrows to transform them into different items.
The probability is low, but it's possible to obtain items like Scout Bracelet or Echo Shield from a Presto Pot.
The Presto Pot item table is narrowed down inside Golden Intervals.
Monster Pot
Armordillo Pop lets you obtain an item from every monster you encounter.
Mixergon is another good option that allows you to synthesize all you want.
You can make Mixergon trip by using a Trip Statue, Posession Staff on an Armordillo,
Trip Stone and either Dragon Tile Bracelet or rolling a Cannonball with a Pierce Bracelet equipped, etc.
Upgrade Pot
Incredibly rare item that can only be found in shops between 71~90F.
Stock up on Restock and Blank scrolls using an Armordillo Pop if you want to try to obtain one,
but it's common to go through 20 Restock Scrolls and still not see it, so you'll likely need to make multiple trips.

Identifying Items

Most items inside this dungeon are found unidentified, so it's important to learn how to
identify using techniques rather than solely relying on items like Identify Pot and Identify Scroll.
You can compare prices, check for specific effects, or narrow down possibilities based on what you've found.
The only items that are found in an identified state are projectiles, riceballs, Blank Scroll, and Escape Scroll.

Weapon + Shield

The name is already identified, but you won't know if it's cursed until you either equip it or fully identify it.
So it's best to wait to equip a weapon or shield until you find a Strip Trap, or until you reach a floor
where Kengo family monsters can be found.

You can also check for curses or upgrade values by comparing prices inside a shop.
If the selling price is higher than the listed price, it has a positive upgrade value,
but if it's lower, it's either cursed or has a negative upgrade value.

See the Price Chart page for weapon and shield prices.

Staff

Staves can be used to escape tricky situations, so identify them whenever you're able to do so.
It's best to swing them at a level 2 monster at least 2 tiles away so that you can
differentiate between Unlucky Staff, Quarter Staff, and Pull Staff.
It's also good to swing staves near the stairs so that you can run away if it happened to be a Happy Staff.

A staff with the [Insert] menu option is either a Grass Toss Staff or Grass Gain Staff.
It's difficult to tell them apart, but Grass Gain Staff costs slightly more than Grass Toss Staff,
so you can try to identify which one it is by checking the price inside of a shop.
It's best to fully identify it before wasting one of its uses.

See the Price Chart page for staff prices.

Scroll

Shiren 2 doesn't have the negative effect scrolls like making you unable to pick up items
or increasing enemy movement speed which can be seen in some of the other games in the series.
However, you might find yourself surrounded by monsters if it happened to be a Wall Collapse Scroll,
so it's best to read scrolls after you've explored the current floor and have defeated some monsters.

Wait until 9F to read unidentified scrolls in case you get lucky with a Wall Collapse Scroll and find a Golden Stairs.
If you find a shop, go ahead and read scrolls that aren't in the price range of a Wall Collapse Scroll.

Grass

Typically identified by checking prices inside a shop.

Mr. Drone family monsters will always drop the following grasses:
Mr. Drone = Strength Grass, Mr. Helico = Happy Grass, Mr. Gyro = Revival Grass.

Revival Grass will activate even if it's in an unidentified state, as long as it's not inside a pot.

There are many nice grasses in the 500G price range such as Dragon Grass, Sight Grass, and Otogiriso,
so it's better to keep them on hand for emergencies or synthesis early game instead of eating them right away.

Twisty Grass, Shrink Seed, and Poison Grass are the only negative effect grasses that can be found on the ground.
Super Unlucky Seed is a shop exclusive, and Unlucky Seed is only found in shops or from Presto Pots.
So there isn't much risk in eating grasses to identify them. However, you'll want to avoid doing so
to prevent accidentally eating a Herb or Life Grass before synthesizing a Healing Sword.

If you arrive at a floor with Mixer monsters without having been able to identify your grass items,
you can still synthesize them in hopes of one of them being a Healing Sword ingredient.
Throw an unneeded weapon at a Mixer, and then throw the grass item you want to identify.
If the synthesis worked, you can check the seal and make note of it,
and if it didn't, you can go ahead and eat the grass to identify it.

Floor Guide

The beginning of this dungeon is generally the most difficult part due to enemies having
high attack stats, along with not being able to obtain riceballs using a Nigiri Morph until 9F.
It usually calms down from about mid game, but don't let your guard down when things are going well.

01~04F

Basics

Explore every room and pick up every item you find.
Generally aim to have Shiren's level equal the current floor number + 2 until mid game.
You could proceed to 2F at level 2 if you're concerned about hunger, but it might take up to 3 hits
to defeat Ashigarudon and Yamikichi on 2F if you do so.

Try to move in ways that reduces the number of steps you have to take to check each room.
Walking diagonally is a given, but also watch for terrain patterns to recognize dead ends or loops.
There's a smaller chance of stepping on Pitfalls or Flood Traps with fewer steps, along with conserving fullness.

Monster Houses can appear between 2~99F, but don't appear between 7~10F.
Shops can appear between 2~90F, and Traders can appear between 4~83F.
Special Monster Houses can appear between 12~78F, with every 4 floors starting from 12F
having a higher chance than other floors of one being generated. (12F, 16F, 20F, 24F...)

Monsters

Most monsters only use regular attacks, but Shiren doesn't have much HP yet, so you can collapse pretty easily.
Mamels and Pit Mamels are always generated awake, so it's common to fight many of them in a row on 1F.
Avoid dashing inside rooms so that monsters don't get the first hit.
That said, you can enter the dungeon again right away even if you collapse,
so you could chalk it up to bad luck and not worry too much about playing carefully yet.

Snacky (1~19F) occasionally spawns a Mid Chintala or Cave Mamel when it's defeated by a monster.
It has a higher chance of appearing on 1F than other floors due to the smaller monster table.
If a Chintala (1~2F) levels up into a Mid Chintala, it's usually best to just head for the stairs.

However, if a Pit Mamel (1~3F) levels up into a Cave Mamel, you should try to defeat it for 500 experience points.
Cave Mamels have very high attack and defense but only have 5 HP,
so you can easily defeat it by throwing Gitan, swinging a Knockback Staff, etc.
If you managed to defeat one, you'll jump to level 8~9 or so, making early game much easier.

Slugs (3~5F) can lower your strength, so you'll want to use projectiles or staves
to defeat them before they have a chance to use their special attacks if possible.
You won't be able to restore lost strength until Antidote Grass is identified.

Cave Mamel Hunting

Level up a Pit Mamel into a Cave Mamel between 1~3F for experience points.

  • If you find a Snacky, let a Pit Mamel defeat it.
  • Swing a Happy Staff at a Pit Mamel.
  • Use a Knockback Statue to have a Pit Mamel defeat another one with knockback damage.

    S:Shiren  P:Pit Mamel
    □□□
    PSP  Shiren will fly at a Pit Mamel every time he's hit, 
    □□□  causing one of them to defeat the other and level up.
Defeating Cave Mamel
Strategies in ascending order of low risk to high risk.
  • Use staves. Easy to identify beforehand, multiple uses, can be used from a distance, and 100% accuracy.
  • Throw 50 or more Gitan. Gitan can be carried around by inserting it into a Storage Pot.
  • Roll a Cannonball to deal 40 damage. Just be careful not to get hit by the explosion yourself.
    There's a chance it will miss, so it's safer to roll it at a wall instead of directly at Cave Mamel.
  • Use a regular attack in a room where a 10 Damage Statue is present.
  • Use regular attacks in a room where a Knockback Statue is present.
  • Shoot 5 arrows at it. Arrows can miss, so you'll want to be at least 7 tiles away to be safe.
    You could also use another monster to block the entrance of a room to defeat it safely.
  • Throw a shield with at least 5 defense.
  • Throw a Storage Pot with enough items inside to deal 5 damage.
  • Throw 5 items that can damage it.
  • Use traps. Poison Arrow Trap will lower its strength to 0. Landmines will defeat it, but you won't gain experience.
  • Use an Explosion or Bomber statue to defeat it. However, you won't gain experience.
  • Throw grass at it, or eat a grass item next to it in hopes of it being Swift, Dragon, or Flame grass, Talking Seed, etc.
  • Read a scroll. Place it on the ground to check for Sanctuary Scroll first,
    and then read it when Cave Mamel is next to you in case it's a Fear Scroll.

Items

The biggest issue at this point in the game is hunger.
If you find items like Large Riceball, Giant Riceball, or Leather Shield, it's best to check for traps
so that you don't accidentally step on a Flood or Spoil trap and let the food go to waste.
Keep food safe by storing it inside pots like Storage Pot, Degrade Pot, and Grass Effect Pot.

Wait to identify items until you find a shop or Identify Pot. You don't want to accidentally eat a Revival Grass.

Having cursed equipment is better than no equipment, so go ahead and equip the first items you find.
However, avoid equipping weak items like Club, or items with negative effects like Heavy Shield.
Keep a second weapon on hand to use as a base for making a Healing Sword.

Don't immediately pick up Gitan on 1F, since it can be used to hunt Cave Mamels for experience points.
Snacky spawns less on 2~3F and often gets defeated by other monsters, so it's fine to pick up Gitan there.

If you come across a Trip Statue, collect items from enemies until the wind blows. Just be careful of hunger.
