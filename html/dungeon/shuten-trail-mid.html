Shuten Trail Mid

Dungeon you can enter after clearing Shuten Trail Easy.

There are more troublesome monsters compared to the first trail.
In particular, you'll want to be careful when progressing
through Dororo Creek, Wandering Cave, and Phantom Pass.
Don't hesitate to use items to make it past those areas.

However, there's a boss fight on 13F, so it would be good
to keep Decoy, Slumber, and Lightning scrolls on hand,
along with Dragon and Swift grasses.

※ Perfect Wood can only be found on this trail past Shuten Halfway.

Clearing this dungeon will open up a side quest
that eventually leads to unlocking Shuten Trail Hard.

Dungeon Overview

Three key points:

  1. Staves can be found on the ground, unlike the first trail.
    They can be used multiple times instead of being single-use
    like scrolls and grasses, so don't hesitate to put them to use.

  2. Weapons and shields on the ground will be unidentified.
    Understand that you risk equipping a cursed item that you
    won't be able to remove when you equip unidentified items.

    Heaven and Earth scrolls can be found in this dungeon, but unlike
    some of the other games, they cannot remove curses in Shiren 2.

  3. It's possible to clear this dungeon without carry-in items.
    However, if it's your first time through, it can be helpful
    to bring the equipment and items you found on the first trail.

Floor Guide

01~02F Quiet Cave

Monsters include Mamel, Pit Mamel, and Chintala, along with Ashigarudon on 2F.

Pit Mamels deal 3~4 damage without a shield, so be sure to move into a
hallway to take them on one at a time if there are other monsters around.

Ashigarudons have 10 HP which is on the higher end, but they only use regular attacks.
Stay calm and you shouldn't have too much trouble defeating them.

On a side note, the music for this area is slightly different than the first trail's Green Forest.

03~04F Ravine Stream (Shuffle)

New monsters include Yamikichi and Nigiri Trainee, along with Droplet Ninja on 4F.

Both Nigiri Trainee and Yamikichi only use regular attacks,
so they shouldn't be too threatening if you leveled Shiren up a bit.
Note that Yamikichi floats on a cloud, so it can cut corners while pursuing you.

Droplet Ninja will place a Droplet Trap on the ground when defeated, so lure it
on top of an item or into a room before defeating it to avoid stepping on the trap.

This area has a similar appearance to Small Valley and the first trail's Inexaustible Falls,
but Ravine Stream typically has larger rooms than those two areas.

05F Lapis Grotto

New monsters include Bow Boy and Mid Chintala.

Bow Boys shoot Wooden Arrows at you from a distance, so move up and down
or left and right to lure them closer while avoiding their attacks.
They occasionally drop some Wooden Arrows when defeated.

Mid Chintalas have a moderate amount of attack and HP at this point in the dungeon,
so you may want to use arrows to weaken them before using regular attacks.

It would be a good idea to spend time here to level Shiren up before proceeding.

06~07F Dororo Creek (Shuffle)

The first real danger zone of this dungeon.

Poison Arrow and Spoil traps are common in this area, so you'll want to keep
riceballs inside Storage Pots to prevent them from turning into Spoiled Riceballs.

Be careful of Gazer, as it can hypnotize Shiren and make him perform a random action.
If you're unlucky, you may find yourself eating Revival Grass or reading a Retreat Scroll.
Use ranged attacks to weaken/defeat it before it gets a chance to use its special attack.
An Echo Shield can reflect its hypnosis, but it's not obtainable yet at this point in the game.

Lt. Spearfish can be an annoying monster in this area because it has a tendency to dash
toward water to recover HP when its HP gets low, and can also attack you from inside water.
Shoot arrows to finish them off as they run away to avoid prolonging the fight.

A monster called Mr. Drone appears, and will always drop Strength Grass when defeated.
Strength matters a lot in Shiren 2 so it's an excellent monster to hunt, but it can
be difficult to do so since it runs away from Shiren and can hover over water.
Mr. Helico also appears on 7F, and sometimes drops Happy Grass when defeated.

Shuten Halfway

See Shuten Trail Easy for Blacksmith services and Trader's Shop items.

Hone your weapon at the Blacksmith, and buy recovery items and food from the Trader's Shop.
If you have a lot of money, go ahead and fill your inventory with Otogiriso.

Note that it's possible to enter a different trail from the one you started with at Shuten Halfway.
If you're finding Shuten Trail Mid to be too difficult, or if you don't feel prepared for the
upcoming boss fight, go ahead and enter Shuten Trail Easy and come back to Mid later.
However, keep in mind that you need to clear the second half of Mid to unlock Shuten Trail Hard.

On a related note, all unidentified items will be identified when you enter Shuten Trail Easy.

08F Wandering Cave

Boy Cart moves in a way to keep a distance of 2 tiles between Shiren and itself.
It has high attack, so fighting inside of a room can be dangerous.
Lure it into a hallway to defeat it without taking damage.
However, it will attack if there's a wall or monster behind it.

Gazer continues to make an appearance, so do your best to prevent it from getting near you.

Dungeon shops occasionally spawn on 8F.
They sell a variety of items including "Good" grade castle materials,
but they cost a lot so you likely won't be able to buy any at the moment.
Stealing is an option, but would be quite difficult at this point in the game.

From this point forward, you'll be able to pick up "Good" grade castle materials,
along with Perfect Wood, which is exclusively found in the second half of Shuten Trail Mid.

09~10F Great Canyon (Shuffle)

Another area that resembles Inexhaustible Falls. The exit is always on the upper side of the map.

Kengo is a monster that can fling your equipped shield away, making it fly behind you.
Either fight with your back against a wall, or unequip your shield beforehand to avoid losing it.
Shoot arrows to weaken/defeat it from a distance, if possible.

Also be careful of Sheep Priest, which can cast a spell that halves your strength for 30 turns.
You're likely to collapse if you get surrounded by Gales or other monsters while your strength is halved.

11~12F Phantom Pass (Shuffle)

Monsters include Kengo, Summon Ninja, Goat Pastor, Pumphantasm, and Taishodon.

Again, fight Kengo with either your back against a wall, or with your shield unequipped beforehand.

Summon Ninjas place a Summon Trap on the ground when defeated.
The walkways are wide in this area, so it should be easy enough to avoid the traps.

Goat Pastors can cast a spell that halves Shiren's max HP for 25 turns.
Try to fight them when no other monsters are nearby.

Taishodons can reflect magic, so you'll likely want to fight them using regular attacks.

Traders have a somewhat decent chance of appearing in Phantom Pass.
Be careful not to hit the Trader when using long-ranged attacks like arrows or weapons with the 火 seal.

Good items to keep on hand for the boss fight: Decoy and Slumber scrolls, Dragon and Swift Grasses, etc.

13F Headwater Cave

Boss fight against 3 Water Striders.

Name HP ATK DEF EXP Element Notes
Water Strider 54 18 20 100 Fast (2x)

Walks on water.
Throws items in a staight line within 5 tiles.

Water Striders move at double speed, and can pick items up and throw them at Shiren.
They can potentially one-shot you if they throw items like Dragon/Flame grass or Gitan.
Not only do they move at double speed, but they can attack twice per turn as well.

Example strategies:

  1. Read a Decoy Scroll, and then eat Dragon Grass when they run in to attack the shadows.

  2. Read 2~3 Lightning Scrolls in a row.

  3. Read a Slumber Scroll if the Water Striders are next to you.

  4. Read a Blaze Scroll to block thrown items and deal damage to them at the same time.

  5. Eat Swift Grass so you match their speed, and then fight them normally.

There are hidden traps, so it may be a good idea to eat Sight Grass if you happen to have one on hand.
