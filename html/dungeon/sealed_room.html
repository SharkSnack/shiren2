Sealed Room

Dungeon you can enter after clearing Onigashima and visiting Shuten Summit.
Strong monsters appear from 1F, but you can bring allies and up to 11 items into the dungeon.
Be careful not to let your guard down even with an assortment of powerful items on hand.

The main objective is to rescue Shuten children who got lost inside the dungeon.
You'll be rewarded with an Ad Balloon castle decoration and a
Shopkeeper icon for your save file after rescuing all 5 children.

The children can be found on 20F, 30F, 40F, 50F, and 60F.
However, the dungeon actually continues infinitely even after 99F, as it begins a new loop.
An Escape Scroll is guaranteed to be on the ground on 99F, including 99F in subsequent loops.

You'll need to make multiple trips to the 70~80F Golden Interval if you want to complete the
equipment gallery, since both Dodge Shield and Reflect Shiled are treasure chest exclusives.

Note that you won't be able to enter Sealed Room until your
next visit to the Summit if you process castle parts.

Dungeon Overview

Shiren's stats will be reset when he enters the Sealed Room. (Level 1, strength 8/8, etc.)

This dungeon generally has less items per floor than others, and often has Gitan or negative
effect items like Unlucky Seed that can be thrown by Water Striders or Kappa family monsters.
Throw grasses at enemies or insert them into Identify Pots to identify them, rather than eating them.

Shops and Traders don't appear in this dungeon, but on rare occasions
you'll come across a Special Monster House called a Hawker House.
Hawkers are level 2 versions of Traders, and sometime have Angel Seeds for sale,
so it's recommended to bring some Gitan with you in case you run into one.

Note that Special Monster Houses no long appear after 88F.

Rescuing Children

  • The first child, Jiro, can be found on 20F.
  • The second child, Taro, can be found on 30F.
  • The third child can be found on 40F.
  • The fourth child can be found on 50F.
  • The fifth child, Dan, can be found on 60~99F.

Talking to a child will trigger an escape from the dungeon.
Rescuing the fifth child requires one inventory slot to be free so that you can receive a reward.
The score bonus from rescuing a child is pretty significant, so you might
want to leave one child in a non-rescued state if aiming for a high score.

The order of children doesn't change even if you rescue a later floor child first.
For example, if you rescue the first child on 60F, it will count as the 20F child,
and you'll have to go to 60F again later to rescue the fifth child.

If a child is defeated, or you proceed to the next floor without talking to them, the child will
appear again on the next floor where children can appear. (20F, 30F, 40F, 50F, 60~99F)

The reward for rescuing all 5 children is an Ad Balloon castle decoration, and a Shopkeeper icon for your save file.
You can only obtain one Ad Balloon per save file, so don't throw it away if you want to 100% the game.

Obtaining Rare Items w/Presto Pot

The Presto Pot item table for Sealed Room includes many rare weapons and shields, and the table doesn't
appear to change based on the floor number, so bringing many Presto Pots inside and using them on 1F
can be a good way to obtain rare items to help with preparations before going for a deeper run.

Stock up on Presto Pots by using an ally Armordillo Pop in Onigashima or Shuten Trail Mid.
Then prepare a stack of 99 arrows to use as insert items.
Suction Scrolls will let you reuse Presto Pots, so bring those if you have some too.
Then simply enter Sealed Room and insert 1 arrow at a time into the Presto Pots.
Be careful not to insert your main weapon by accident by going through the menus too quickly.

The monsters on 1F can be challenging, so you might want to bring a strong shield,
a scroll for emergencies, and a strong ally monster to take care of enemies.
You can bring Mamo along to carry more items into the dungeon if desired.

You can also cheat by copying your save file to a controller pak before using the Presto Pots,
and then copy the save file from the controller pak if you didn't get what you want.
However, note that your save file will permanently show a . instead of : after the file number in the main menu,
making it clear that you copied the data from a controller pak. It doesn't affect anything gameplay wise,
but it's something to keep in mind if you're aiming for a true 100% completion of the game.

Preparations Before Entering

A strong weapon and shield is pretty much mandatory for this dungeon.
You'll want a minimum of +50 upgrade value, and it's best to go all the way up to +99 beforehand.
If you want to be extra safe, collect Blank Scrolls in Onigashima and use them in synthesis to increase the number
of slots on equipment, allowing you to use more seals to cover a greater number of weaknesses or situations.

The quickest way to increase upgrade value would likely be to use an ally Iai monster
to collect equipment, and then synthesize the items using an ally Mixer monster.
Be sure to make Mixer trip to drop the item using an Armordillo before returning it to its pot.

Weapon
Kaburasutegi: 99 ATK, 12 slots. Makes the dungeon a cakewalk with its absurd base attack.
Ryujin Sword: 25 ATK, 7 slots + inherent 龍 seal. Made by combining 4 Dragon Killers.
Dragon Killer: 15 ATK, 7 slots + inherent 竜 seal. Onigashima Golden Interval.
Crescent Arm: 14 ATK, 6 slots + inherent 月 seal. Defeat Boss Oni again in Onigashima after finishing the story.
Ivory Sword: 13 ATK, 4 slots + rustproof. Onigashima Golden Interval.

Manji-Kabura can be obtained from a Presto Pot inside Sealed Room, so you
could work on making a Kaburasutegi before attempting a deep run of Sealed Room.
The others can be obtained from Golden Intervals inside Onigashima, excluding Crescent Arm
which is simply a guaranteed reward for defeating Boss Oni again after finishing the main story.

Weapon Seals
三 and 回 are required.
火 lets you defeat enemies from a distance, but the animation is long, so you might prefer it on a secondary weapon.
幸 or 天 helps Shiren level up faster. Best to stack multiple seals on a secondary weapon to use early game.
仏, ド, and 竜 will increase your damage output (x1.5) against certain enemies.
眠 sometimes puts enemies to sleep (8%), allowing you to defeat them without getting hit.
飯 or に will help prevent collapsing from starvation by making enemies drop riceballs when defeated.
サ lets you dig through walls without the weapon breaking, allowing you to create shortcuts.
金 (red) prevents rust, and protects against 1 use of Monsoon Doll's seal removing special attack.
会 sometimes deals a critical hit (35%) that deals 1.5 times the damage of a regular hit.
必 increases your regular attack accuracy to 100%.

Shield
Walrus Shield: 6 DEF, 6 slots + inherent ト seal. Protects against Gyandora. Obtained from Onigashima Traders.
Leather Shield: 4 DEF, 5 slots + inherent 皮 seal and rustproof. Low defense isn't an issue with high upgrade value.
Ivory Shield: 12 DEF, 5 slots + rustproof. Onigashima Golden Interval.

Shield Seals
山 reflects magic like Gazer hypnosis, Sheep Priest spells, and King Tusker staves.
見 increases evasion (35%), making monster regular attacks miss more often.
皮 halves hunger rate.
爆 reduces damage (30%) from explosions such as cannonballs, landmines, and Patient Bags.
ト blocks stealing attacks by Zalokleft, Gamarra, and Gyadon family monsters.
弟 restores HP when Shiren receives damage. Stack multiple seals to increase its effectiveness.
竜 reduces fire damage by 30%. Combine with 火 to reduce fire damage by 50%.
火 reduces fire damage by 20%. Combine with 竜 to reduce fire damage by 50%.
金 (red) prevents rust, and protects against 1 use of Monsoon Doll's seal removing special attack.
眠 somtimes puts enemies to sleep (8%) for 5 turns when they hit you with a regular attack.
身 allows you to dodge all projectiles, excluding objects falling from above.
返 allows you to reflect all projectiles, excluding objects falling from above.
カ makes it so that traps no longer activate when stepped on.

Other Items
Revival Grass, Sanctuary Scroll, staves, Scout and Phantasm bracelets, Plating Scroll to repair bracelets, etc.
Empty Monster Pots if you still need to capture N'dubbas, Taurus family monsters, etc.
Escape or Blank scroll so that you can return to Napus Village when desired.
Disposable Sword with a か seal to use as a long-lasting food source.
Wall Collapse Scrolls if searching for Golden Intervals.
Storage Pots expanded to the max size of 8.

Strength and Boost grasses if you want to defeat monsters in a single hit.
Note that Shihan, Gigahead, and Taurus family monsters cannot be defeated
in one hit even with a Kaburasutegi+99 without increasing your strength.

Trapper Bracelet can be used to collect Pitfalls to use in emergencies, or to descend faster.
However, keep in mind that Trapper Bracelets crack more easily than other bracelets,
and Shiren's attack is significantly reduced while it's equipped.

Allies / Monster Pots
Bringing Mamo along will effectively let you carry in 21 items instead of 11.
Asuka equipped with powerful items can prove to be a reliable ally, and she can catch your equipment
if she is behind you when a Kengo family monster disarms you with its special attack.
That said, Asuka can also be disarmed by Kengo monsters, so don't be surprised if she loses her equipment.
You might want to stick to non-upgraded basics like Ryujin Sword, Heavy Shield, and 2 Strength Bracelets
for Asuka so you don't get too down if that happens.

Typhoon, Nigiri Manager, Hyper Gazer, Monsoon Doll, Tiny Phoenix, and Queen Spider are good to bring with you.
However, be careful around Bomber Statues, Patient Bags, and Tiger Tosser family monsters
if there are landmines nearby, because any of those explosions can one-shot ally monsters.
Water Shinryu can also be used either as a freeze breath cannon, or as a wall.

You'll likely want to bring Armordillo Pop and Shihan (or Iai) with when searching for Golden Intervals
so that you can obtain additional rare items and get the most out of your trip once you find one.

Floor Guide

01~19F

There's a chance you'll be surrouded by monsters at the start, so you'll want to keep your
equipment and Revival Grass inside the same pot so that you can take them out in a single turn.
You could also equip your sword and shield in Napus Village beforehand to start the dungeon with them equipped.

Shiren's stats like level, strength, and max fullness will be reset
when you enter the dungeon, so the first thing you'll want to do is level up.
For example, you could feed Riku and an ally Snacky to a Scorpion to level it up into a
Stun Scorpion worth 4900 experience, and then defeat it by throwing a total of 1250 Gitan.

Patient Bag makes an appearance from 1~10F.
It has 120 HP and high attack for a 1F monster, and can explode when its remaining HP drops below 11.
Bracelets have a greater chance to crack from explosions, so be sure to unequip them before fighting these monsters.
They offer 200 experience when defeated, so try to finish them off without having them explode if possible.

Kengo appears from 2~4F, and can fling your equipped shield away, so don't let your guard down.
Keep a Scout Bracelet equipped so you don't get hit by a surprise attack.
You could also unequip your shield when facing them if you leveled up on 1F as recommended.
Asuka will catch your shield if she is behind you as well.

Water Strider appears from 5~7F.
Gitan is often found on the ground in Sealed Room, so Water Striders can potentially one-shot you by throwing Gitan.
You can use items like arrows and staves to defeat or otherwise prevent them from throwing Gitan from a distance.

Golden Stairs have a 30% chance to spawn inside the walls on 6~10F, so
go ahead and read Wall Collapse Scrolls if searching for the first Golden Interval.
It takes more time, but you could also level a Pop Tank up and then take control of the
Cranky Tank using a Posession Staff to break walls with cannonballs.

Iai appears from 13~17F. It's a tough monster to defeat quickly at
such an early point in the dungeon, so think of it as a level and equipment check.

Bored Kappa appears from 14~17F, so watch out for the combo of Iai
flinging your sword or shield, followed by Bored Kappa walking to it up and throwing it at you.
Use items like Slumber Scroll or Swap Staff if a Bored Kappa moves on top of your equipment.

You'll also want to be careful of Gazelle Pope (13~15F) if you don't have an
Echo Shield (山 seal), and Gyandora (16~30F) if you don't have a Walrus Shield (ト seal).
Note that you can always defeat them in a single hit if you have a Kaburasutegi+99 with the 必 seal.

20~30F

The first child can be found on 20F, so talk to them to clear the dungeon and return to Shuten Summit.
Don't worry if you accidentally defeat them or proceed without talking to them, because they will
appear again on the next floor where children can appear. [TODO: DOUBLE CHECK FLOORS]

Archdragon makes an appearance on 20~35F.
They shouldn't be a problem if you have both 竜 and 火 seals on your shield, along with multiple 弟 seals.
That said, if you don't have those seals on your shield, you'll want to rush to the stairs as soon as possible, and
then make up for the missed level ups by using projectiles or other items once you get through the Archdragon floors.

Monsoon Doll appears on 21~30F.
Their special attack is less damaging compared to Kengo family monsters since you don't risk losing
the equipment, but losing valuable synthesis seals is still something you want to avoid at all costs.
Equip a Scout Bracelet while walking, and use staves or ally Monsters to prevent their special attack from being used.

Bellthoven (25~36F) can summon up to three level 3 monsters on the same turn.
Lure them into a hallway when fighting them to limit their summoning to 1 monster, or else
you might find yourself quickly surrounded by 3 Stun Scorpions or other strong monsters.

31~44F

Shihan makes an appearance on 31~40F.
Again, keep a Scout Bracelet equipped while walking to avoid surprise attacks.
It has high HP and defense, so you likely won't be able to defeat it in one hit, even with a powerful weapon.
Tiny Phoenix (20~99F) might come to the rescue and heal Shihan as well, negating any damage you dealt.
Equip a Phantasm Bracelet to attack them from inside walls, or get the first strike in by attacking around corners.
Use Slumber Scroll or Warp Grass if you're surrounded by multiple Shihans, such as from stepping on a Summon Trap.

Kappa Pest also appears on 31~49F, so watch out for the Shihan flinging your equipment
followed by Kappa Pest throwing the equipment at you combo attack.

Spiked Voodoll (31~39F) can curse bracelets, so avoid letting them use their special attack.

45~99F

End game monsters like Emperor Tusker and Typhoon appear starting from 45F.
However, you actually shouldn't have too much trouble if your equipment is sufficiently strong,
and you have the recommended seals like ト and 山 to block or reflect their special attacks.
The monster lineup doesn't change much, so if you made it this far, you can probably make it to 99F without issues.

The Golden Stairs leading to the second Golden Interval has a 30% chance to spawn between 70~80F.
Be sure to visit it if you're looking to finish the equipment gallery, as there are many
weapons and shields exclusively found in this second Golden Interval.

Minotaur (78~85F) and Megataur (86F~) make an appearance toward the end.
Don't forget to capture them if you still need to register them to Monster Kingdom.
Along those lines, the only way to capture a Taurus is lower the level of a Minotaur or Megataur,
which can be done using items like Twisty Grass, Unlucky Seed, Unlucky Staff, etc.

Lastly, don't forget to search for rare N'dubba ages as well if you wish to complete Monster Kingdom.
Remember, the floor number = N'dubba's age, so if you want a 99 year old N'dubba, search for one on 99F, and so on.

Golden Interval

First (6~10F, Return 11F)

Heaven and Earth are the only scrolls that can be found in this Golden Interval,
so an Armordillo Pop can be used to quickly strengthen equipment.

Homing Blade is found only in this Golden Interval (excluding Secret Phrases),
and is an excellent weapon to synthesize onto your main weapon,
as the 必 seal ensures that your regular attacks will never miss.

Gamarra Shield is a collector's item that is exclusive to this Golden Interval.
Be sure to pick one up for the gallery if you haven't done so.

Second (70~80F, Return 81F)

The second Golden Interval has many exclusive pieces of equipment that differ from the first one.

Sacred Sword and Sacred Shield have 14 and 16 slots, making them viable as both main or secondary equipment.
For example, you could synthesize many 幸 or 天 seals onto a Sacred Sword to use it as early game level up weapon,
or stack 14 火 seals to deal 60 damage per swing of your weapon at full HP, etc.
They can also be used as seal containers to free up storage space.

Dodge Shield or Reflect Shield can be synthesized onto your main shield to make most projectiles a non-issue.
The 身 seal recommended over 返 because it allows you to dodge Silver Arrows from Horse Ronin family monsters.

Note that Shihan makes a return, so don't let your guard down while you hunt for items.
King Tusker and Monarch Tusker also make an appearance, which can be
troublesome if you don't have the 山 seal from an Echo Shield.

You can only obtain one treasure chest item at a time, and the treasure is randomly selected,
so you'll have to make at least one more trip to this Golden Interval to collect both Dodge Shield and Reflect Shield.
