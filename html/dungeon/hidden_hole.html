Shrine's Hidden Hole

Dungeon you can enter after clearing Onigashima.

The idea is to capture monsters using an item called Monster Pot,
and progress through the dungeon by utilizing ally monster abilities.

You can bring up to 10 Monster Pots (empty or monster inside) into the dungeon,
and you'll also be given 3 empty Monster Pots and an Escape Scroll when you enter.

Once you have some useful monsters like Mixer, Iai, and Armordillo Pop,
this dungeon becomes the easiest one among the post-game dungeons,
since you'll be able to synthesize a Leather Shield+99 (弟弟弟弟薬) with ease.

※ ※ ※ Warning ※ ※ ※

This dungeon loops endlessly after going down the stairs on 99F,
but Escape Scrolls are only available during the initial descent.
So if you want to go home at some point, you'll need to pick up the
Escape Scroll on 10F, 20F, or 99F before the dungeon loops.

If a Kappa throws the scroll at you, or you fall into a pitfall on 99F
before you obtain the scroll, you'll have to intentionally collapse or
select Suspend → Return to Village menu options to exit the dungeon.

Dungeon Overview

Equipment cannot be found naturally on the ground, so you'll have to use
Kengo family monsters to obtain Clubs or Leather Shields if you want equipment.
If you're lucky, you might obtain monster drops like Fish Spear, Ironhead's Head, and Minotaur's Axe.

Mixer monsters do not spawn in this dungeon, and Synthesis Pots cannot be found.
You'll have to capture a Mixer from a different dungeon and bring it with you if you want to synthesize.
Side note: Shrine's Hidden Hole (B) doesn't allow carry-in items, so the Leather Shield+99 strategy cannot be used.

This is the only dungeon where Queen Spider can be found.
If you wish to complete Monster Kingdom, you'll have to descend to 60F of this dungeon to capture one.
That said, she is the strongest ally monster in the game when you max her growth, so it's worth the effort.

Special Monster Houses can spawn on all floors,
so you might be placed in an unwinnable situation from the very first floor if you're unlucky.
However, that also means you have a chance to capture high level monsters from the first floor too.
Don't give up if you happen to have one bad experience with a Special Monster House.

Basic Strategy

The most consistent strategy is to synthesize a Leather Shield+99 (弟弟弟弟薬) using Kengo and Mixer.
Armordillo Pop will make obtaining Otogiriso easier, and you'll also want to bring Nigiri Boss or Manager
along to make sure you have enough food, as it's a very time-consuming process.

Of course, you don't have to use this strategy, but since this is a wiki aimed at helping players of
any skill level clear these dungeons, the floor guide will assume the Leather Shield strategy is being used.

One other thing to keep in mind, you can level Shiren up by defeating monsters even if you don't have a weapon.
Weaken enemy monsters with abilities like Slug licks or Emperor Tusker's Quarter Staff, and have Shiren finish them off.

Monster Pot + Ally Monster Mechanics

Monster Pots are the key items of this dungeon, so their mechanics will be detailed in this section, along with ally monsters.

Monster Pots are guaranteed to hit the target when thrown at a monster.
Therefore, they can be used not only to capture monsters, but also as a way to
completely nullify a monster if you're cornered and need a way to escape the situation.

Ally monsters will grow stronger when they defeat enemy monsters, increasing their HP and attack by a small amount.
Note that Shiren doesn't gain any experience when ally monsters defeat monsters.
Ally monsters don't gain experience either, but will instead always grow by 1 stage for every monster they defeat.
So you could even max their stats on 1F by defeating Mamels and Chintalas until you reach the growth limit.
You'll want to hit the growth limit beforehand on monsters with high potential like Monsoon Dolls when attempting a deep run.

Monsters will always use their special attack on the same turn they are sent outside of their pot. (With some exceptions)
Therefore, you can reliably use abilities like Armordillo's tackle or Aqua Dragon's freezing breath on approaching monsters.

Select the [Call] menu option to have monsters move toward Shiren and return to their pots.
Status conditions will be cured, and monsters will regenerate HP while they're inside their pot.
If Shiren doesn't have an ally monster's pot on hand, they will just continue to wander around in search of enemy monsters.

For the most part, you'll want to keep ally monsters inside their pots to keep their HP topped up when you
don't immediately need their abilities or help, since they don't regenerate HP when they're outside of their pots.
You also risk losing them if Shiren happens to step on a Pitfall or Landmine while they're outside of their pots.
It may be good to keep multiple pots of the important monsters as backup in case you lose one.

It's easy to forget, but it's possible to throw an ally monster's pot at them to have them return to their pot from a distance.
It's good to keep that in mind for situations where they're about to be defeated, or have been confused by a Gazer or reflected magic.
Just be sure to check for traps on the way to pick up the ally monster's pot.

Ally monsters registered with Monster Kingdom will return to Monster Kingdom
in the event they're defeated, or if Shiren collapses in a dungeon and loses his items.
Their growth will be halved when that happens, but it shouldn't take long to raise them again.
However, monsters that are not registered will be permanently lost, with one exception:
If a monster was outside of their pot when Shiren collapsed, there's a chance that they'll still be
around when you return to that location, so you can throw a new monster pot to capture them.
Talk to Oracle Gran to find out if there are any former ally monsters wandering around inside dungeons.

Other Items

Shiren's offensive capabilities are limited, so it's easy to collapse if you start the floor in the middle of a Monster House.
There's also a chance you won't be able to send out ally monsters or summon shadows with a Decoy Scroll
if the number of characters is at the current floor's limit due to a Monster House being present.
So it's good to keep items like Swift Grass, Warp Grass, Swap Staff, and Sanctuary Scroll on hand at all times.

Floor Guide

01~10F

Feed a Snacky to a Pit Mamel to make a Cave Mamel, and then have Shiren defeat it to get him up to level 8~9.
Use ally monsters like Monarch Tuskery to slow it down / weaken it first, and then have Shiren get the last hit in.
It would also be good to increase your max fullness to 200% by having Nigiri Manager defeat monsters.

After doing the above, use Armordillo Pop to gather items like Posession Staff, Otogiriso, and Revival Grass.
Once you have a some of those items, use Iai to gather Clubs and Leather Shields with positive upgrade values.
After that, go ahead and start synthesizing the Clubs and Leather Shields using Mixergon to strengthen them.
However, don't forget to fill the Leather Shield's synthesis slots with 弟 (Otogiriso) before combining other Leather Shields.
Then you'll want to collect more Otogiriso to increase Shiren's max HP. Aim for 400 or higher if you want to be safe.

Antidote Grass cannot be found in this dungeon, so avoid getting your strength lowered by Slugs (3~5F) and Scorpions (8F).
That said, you can restore it by intentionally collapsing and using a Revival Grass, or by leveling down a
Bellthoven (51~60F) into a Schubell to have it summon Mr. Drones for Strength Grass drops.
You can also get by without Shiren doing the fighting thanks to ally monsters, so it's not the end of the world if it gets lowered.

Be careful not to lose your monster pots due to getting tripped by Armordillo and Armordillo Bro (5~6F).
It's best to carry a Balance Staff in your inventory at all times to help prevent such accidents.
You can level Shiren up by standing between two Armordillo Bros to have one defeat the other with knockback damage,
and then have Shiren defeat the Armordillo Pop to gain 700 experience points per monster.

Watch out for Zaloklefts (5~8F), as they might steal one of your Monster Pots and go underwater.
Use Monsoon Doll to seal them, or Hyper Gazer to confuse them before they get a chance to steal.
That said, you shouldn't have much trouble, since they're always sleeping inside rooms when they spawn.

Gazer (6~8F, 10~15F, 39~47F, 60~75F) is one of the most dangerous monsters inside this dungeon if you keep
the Escape Scroll you were given at the start on hand, as its hypnosis could cause Shiren to read it against his will.
The best way to prevent that is to throw the Escape Scroll away and pick up the one on the ground on 99F,
but that means you'll need to plan for the worst case scenario of a Vexing Kappa throwing the Escape Scroll at you.

Nigiri Morph (9~10F, 17~19F, 30~36F, 50~55F, 60~75F) is also an extremely dangerous monster in this dungeon,
as it can transform your Monster Pots into Large Riceballs. Seal it or have an ally monster be the one to fight it.
For hallways, have one of your ally monsters lead with Shiren following behind them to avoid potential surprise attacks.

11~21F

Your equipment should be pretty strong by now, so you shouldn't have trouble. Continue to strengthen your equipment.

Dangerous monsters: Nigiri Morph (17~19F), Patient Bag (19~21F), Tiger Hurler (19~21F), and Green Zalokleft (19~21F).
Ally monsters will disintegrate if they get caught in a Patient Bag's explosion, so always have Shiren be the one to fight them.
Tiger Hurler can toss ally monsters onto Landmines to one-shot them, so be sure to seal or confuse them before fighting.
Green Zaloklefts can steal your Monster Pots, so again, seal or confuse them to prevent them from using their ability.

This is a good floor range to level Shiren up. Stand between two monsters to have them defeat each other.
Horse Emperor (3100), Gigahead (3150), Cranky Tank (2300), Bear Borg (2500) are all good options.
They're powerful, but your equipment should be pretty strong by now, so they should be manageable.
You can also use ally monsters to weaken them beforehand. Just be sure to only take on one of them at a time.
Ideal to get Shiren up to level 40 with 400 or more HP here, or by 30F at the latest due to Archdragon making an appearance.

22~60F

Shiren's equipment, level, and max HP should be pretty solid at this point if everything has gone well.
You can resupply items with Armordillo Pop as needed instead of relying on items on the ground, so
if feel you have enough Revival Grass and other items, start rushing to the stairs on each floor now.

However, if you haven't been able to prepare as planned, you'll want to take things a little slower.
Monsters will only get stronger as you descend deeper into the dungeon, but you can use that to your advantage.
Capture monsters and send them out right away, using them to distact enemy monsters while you head for the stairs.
You can also have them lead the way in hallways with Shiren following behind them to use them as a disposable shield.
Doing so will protect your important monsters from surprise attacks by Gazers, Nigiri Morphs, and Gyandoras.

Archdragon (30~59F) makes an appearance.
You shouldn't have too much to worry about if you have a shield with 5 弟 (Otogiriso) seals, and have
increased your max HP as recommended. If you're still worried, Tiny Phoenix can be used as a healer.
If you're aiming for 99F, be sure to capture an Archdragon to help ensure you're able to pick up the Escape Scroll on 99F.
Also make sure you have a Posession Staff on hand, and if you want to be safe, capture a second Archdragon as backup.

Other troublesome monsters include Nigiri Morph and Nigiri Manager (30~36F, 45~57F, 60~75F),
Gazer, Super Gazer, and Hyper Gazer (25~31F, 39~47F, 60~90F), Rancor Slug and Intolerable Slug (32~40F, 39~47F).
It's best to act with the assumption that you'll encounter one of them when walking through hallways.

Gyandora (50~79F) also makes an appearance.
If it plucks a Monster Pot, the pot's size will be reduced from 1 to 0, instantly one-shotting whatever monster was inside.
Again, have an ally monster lead the way in hallways with Shiren following behind them to avoid surprise attacks.

Iron Zalokleft (51~53F) can steal your shield as well as Monster Pots, so don't let them use their special attack.

60F~

Queen Spider (60~99F) makes an appearance.
This is the only dungeon where she can be captured, so be sure to do so if you still need one for Monster Kingdom.
If your goal was just to capture her instead of descending to 99F, go ahead and read an Escape Scroll after doing so.
However, if you threw away your Escape Scroll to prevent Gazer from making Shiren read it, you'll need to descend to 99F.
If a Vexing Kappa throws the scroll at you, or you fall in a Pitfall on 99F, you won't be able to escape from the dungeon alive.

Eat Sight Grass or perform empty attacks to check for traps one step at a time inside rooms.
Immediately read a Decoy Scroll if you see both red and blue dots on the map in the starting room.
Send out an Archdragon and take control of it using a Posession Staff to wipe out all monsters on the floor,
and then search for the scroll while using Archdragon to wipe out more monsters every now and then to be safe.
Lastly, Archdragons in Shiren 2 aren't very strong compared to other games, so be careful of monsters like Typhoon.
