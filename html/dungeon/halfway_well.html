Halfway Well

Dungeon you can enter after registering 30 or more monsters to Monster Kingdom.

Shiren won't trigger traps by stepping on them in this dungeon,
but he can instead pick them up and set them on the ground to trap monsters.
Once a trap is set, it cannot be picked up again.
Traps have a chance to break after being triggered by monsters.
Lastly, note that Shiren can set traps down without ending his turn.

Dungeon Overview

The unlock event occurs the next time you exit Mumyu after registering 30 or more monsters to Monster Kingdom.
The carpenter will offer to take you to Shuten Halfway or Shuten Summit after the first attempt.
Note that Halfway Well doesn't allow carry-in items, so you'll have to have an empty inventory.

Trap Dungeon Basics:

  • You can pick up traps and carry them around.
  • Setting a trap down doesn't end your turn.
  • You can only set traps down on the tile you're standing on.
  • Shiren's attack is drastically reduced when he has the Trapper status.
  • Defeating enemies without using traps only provides 20% of the normal amount of experience points.
  • You gain experience points when monsters step on traps, even if the trap doesn't defeat them.
  • Throwing a trap at a monster deals a small amount of damage to them.
  • Traps have a chance to break when monsters step on them, unless a Sturdy Trap Statue is nearby.
  • Clearing the dungeon rewards you with a Trapper Bracelet, which grants the Trapper status while equipped.
  • Pickaxe is the only weapon found on the ground in this dungeon.
  • Shields cannot be found, except for a Traproid Shield from a Golden Interval treasure chest.
  • Storage Pots can be found normally, and grasses can be purchased from dungeon shops.
  • Chain and Flood traps can be found normally. (Both are exclusive to Shiren 2)

Expanding on some of the important points mentioned above:

Shiren's attack is greatly reduced when he has the Trapper status.
His attack strength nearly stops growing after level 3, and stops entirely after level 20.
To illustrate, Shiren's attack at level 99 in Halfway Well is lower than level 6 inside other dungeons.
So you won't be able to reliably defeat monsters using regular attacks beyond the first few floors.
That said, it's still worth leveling up in this dungeon to increase max HP.

Defeating enemies without using traps only provides 20% of the normal amount of experience points.
However, defeating monsters using traps provides 95% experience, or 75% if defeated using a Spoil Trap.
A monster stepping on a trap also provides 75% experience, so if you find a Sturdy Trap Statue early on,
you can easily reach level 99 near the beginning of the dungeon by chaining traps together.

Other notes:

The amount of damage dealt for damage type traps like Rockfall Trap increases by 2% per level up.
Despite that, most traps except for Landslide Trap still don't deal enough damage to quickly defeat enemies.

Shiren is immune to Statues effects inside this dungeon, as they affect enemy monsters instead.
For example, you can circle around an Ouch Statue to have it defeat a monster without Shiren taking damage.

Monster Houses can be generated, but Special Monster Houses do not appear in this dungeon.

You'll automatically clear the dungeon the first time you pick up a Trapper Bracelet found starting on 10F.
After the first clear, the game will prompt you whether you'd like to exit or not when you either
pick up a Trapper Bracelet, or insert or take out a Trapper Bracelet from a Storage Pot.
However, you won't be able to exit while inside a Golden Interval.

Golden Stairs have a 100% chance to generate inside a wall between 50~55F.
The Golden Interval of Halfway Well is the only place where you can obtain a Traproid Shield.
The shield prevents traps from activating when Shiren steps on them, and is one of the best seals in the game.
You'll likely want to make two trips, one to synthesize into your main shield, and a second one for the gallery.

Basic Strategy

How to Defeat Enemies

Shiren's attack is greatly reduced inside this dungeon, so you won't be
able to reliably defeat enemies using regular attacks after the first few floors.
You could slow them or use a poison arrow to lower their strength, but it would still
take a while since you'll only be able to deal 1 damage per hit against later enemies.

So you'll want to make use of traps, statues, scrolls, and Gitan to defeat enemies.
You can obtain a large amount of Gitan to use as a projectile by letting a Gamarra
steal from you, and then inserting the dropped Gitan into a Storage Pot.

Monsters with Floating or Flying elements that step on a tile with a trap won't trigger the trap.
These monsters include Tiny Phoenix, and Yamikichi, Gale, Pumphantasm, Weather Doll families.
Piko Hammer family monsters will destroy traps instead of stepping on them as well.
So you'll have to get a little more creative in how you deal with these enemies.

For example, you could throw Gitan or step on an arrow trap to hit a flying monster,
or step on a landmine or use an Explosion Scroll to defeat everything within a 1 tile radius.
You could also read a Trap Activate Scroll while a flying monster is above a trap.
For Piko Hammer family monsters, you could swing a Swap Staff while standing on a trap,
and have the target trigger the trap you were standing on after swapping places.

Pickaxe Technique

You can dig out one wall tile in a room to mislead the monster AI into thinking there's a hallway,
allowing you to either buy some time or trigger traps along the wall.
