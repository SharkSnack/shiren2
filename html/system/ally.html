Shiren's Allies

Allies are NPC characters who fight monsters alongside Shiren.
Shiren 2 features five ally characters - Himakichi, Riku, Asuka, Kirara, and Mamo.
See the Story Progression page to check requirements for each character becoming your ally.

Ally characters follow behind Shiren, and automatically move toward enemies to attack them.
Allies run from enemies if their HP falls below 25%, and enemies level up if they defeat an ally.
Shopkeepers, Sheriffs, and Guard Dogs won't attack allies, so they can be used as a shield when stealing.

Allies gain experience when Shiren defeats an enemy,
and Shiren and other allies also gain experience if an ally defeats an enemy.
Generally stats like level, HP, strength, Landmine damage, etc. are the same as Shiren for allies,
but unlike Shiren, their defense increases with level ups, they don't have hunger,
and the amount of experience needed to level up differs per character.
See the Exp & Stats page for details about ally stats.

Characters

Himakichi

A Bored Kappa monster who always has more time than he knows what to do with.
He takes an interest in fighting Oni after some convincing by Koppa, and joins Shiren as an ally.

He can catch any thrown item (ally or enemy), and will then toss it at an enemy within throwing range.
However, he can't catch items that have the piercing property, such as Silver Arrows.
Catching and throwing items doesn't use up Himakichi's turn, so he can still move or attack afterward.
The amount of damage the thrown item deals is based on Himakichi's attack strength.
Note that he can catch and throw Shiren's equipment that has been disarmed by a Kengo monster.

Himakichi will drop the item on the ground if an enemy isn't within his throwing range or view.
However, he will throw stone items at an adjacent tile instead of dropping them on the ground.
If Shiren takes control of a Bow Boy family monster, he can collect Wooden Arrows, Iron Arrows,
or Cannonballs simply by shooting them at Himakichi.

Since he's a Kappa family monster, Himakichi can go underwater, and can attack underwater monsters.
He also throws underwater items toward the ground beneath Shiren so that Shiren can pick them up.

Himakichi will eat grass or seed items that have a positive effect,
and will also eat Dragon Grass or Flame Grass when he can hit an enemy with the fire breath.
Unlike other allies, he eats Herbs and Otogiriso, so those items can increase his max HP if at full HP.
If you want to hit Himakichi with a grass or seed item that he won't eat such as Antidote Grass,
you'll need to either seal him or equip a Pierce Bracelet to ensure he doesn't catch it.

Due to his ability to catch thrown items, he's effectively invincible against Bow Boy and Boy Cart.
He can also throw Pop Tank Cannonballs and Wolf Droid bombs back at monsters,
but will get hit by the explosions if the monsters are standing next to Himakichi.
(If Himakichi collapses due to that, the message "Himakichi self-destructed!" will be displayed).

As shown in one of the demos, you can help Himakichi defeat enemies faster by shooting arrows at him.
However, ideally you'll want to avoid having him fight at close range, since he has decent defense
but only average attack, where even Riku surpasses his attack strength starting at Lv18.
He's nice to have around for the Shuten Trails and Onigashima, but the lack of power and utility
becomes apparant in Sealed Room when compared to allies like Asuka and Mamo.
That said, he is able to collect underwater items, so it can still be nice to bring him along.

Riku

A boy who lives in Napus Village.
He was a delicate child, but was inspired by Shiren's efforts to build a castle and becomes an ally.

He can shoot a slingshot at an enemy within a 2 tile radius, dealing fixed damage based on level.
It's similar to Porky Stones, but it's treated as a regular attack rather than a ranged attack.
The slingshot cannot be reflected by Tonosamadon, and doesn't get nullified by a Hoarder Statue.
It is affected by Knockback Statue, and Riku will receive counter damage from a Samuraidon.

He tends to run toward monsters on his own despite lacking in both HP and strength.
He often collapses early on before you get a chance to rescue him, resulting in a leveled up monster.
Oddly enough, he seems to be totally fine as if nothing happened when you return to the village.
This tendency to collapse easily can be used to level up a monster for more experience in Sealed Room
(example: Stun Scorpion on 1F = 4,900 exp), or to capture a higher level monster for Monster Kingdom.

Due to this tendency, the nickname "Blue Snacky" became popular over time.
A proper blue colored Snacky family monster was then introduced in Shiren 4,
and one of the night-time monsters in Shiren 5 includes a reference to Riku as the Blue Snacky.

Some players have fun doing a Restricted Play where Riku must be kept alive until 99F in Sealed Room.
