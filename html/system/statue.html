What are Statues?

Statues are one of the new gimmicks introduced in Shiren 2, and are modeled after various monsters.
They can be found in all dungeons starting from Onigashima except for Shrine's Hidden Hole.
They're said to be made by Shuten Summit residents, but also appear in Sealed Room, so they're a bit of a mystery.
A continuous mechanical sound can be heard when Shiren is within a 11 tile radius, so they're easy to identify.

Statues provide various effects to nearby characters.
Some are positive and can be of help to Shiren, but others can have a negative effect.
Select negative effect statues will switch to affecting only monsters when Shiren has the Trapper status.

Generally, the range of a statue inside a room is either room-wide, or a 1 tile radius around the statue.
However, the tiles along to walls are exempt from a room-wide effect.
If the floor is one giant room with limited view, a room-wide statue will only affect characters in a 11 tile radius.
If a Wall Collapse Scroll is read, the a room-wide statue will affect the entire floor.
That said, there are some exceptions - Ouch Statue always only affects characters within a 1 tile radius,
and Scout Statue always affects the entire floor, no matter where it's located.

Statues will crumble after being attacked a number of times, with the durability varying based on individual statues.
It's best to destroy negative effect statues as soon as you come across them to lower the risk of accidents.
The number of hits needed to destroy statues is as follows:
Explosion Statue (1~2) ≦ Decoy Statue (2~5) ≦ Base Statue Durability (3~5) ≦ Ouch Statue (8~12).
If fire is generated on a tile where a statue is located (Blaze Scroll or Fire Staff), the statue will vanish on that turn.
It's good to remember that for statues that are difficult to approach, such as Ouch, Bomber, and Sleep statues.
Landmine and Big Landmine type explosions will make statues vanish in a single turn as well.

Statues can be moved using a Knockback Staff or Pull Staff, but Swap Staff doesn't have an effect on them.
Dosukoi family monsters can sometimes be seen pushing statues around as well.

Enemies generally avoid attacking statues, but will attack them if the statue is blocking their path.
Piko Hammer family monsters will attempt to break statues in addition to visible traps.
Allies sometimes target statues if they were attacked by them, such as Ouch Statue or Stone Toss Statue.

Statues are generally treated as items when being generated, so they can be commonly found on islands.
They can also be generated blocking room entrances, but monsters usually destroy those right away.
However, statues will never be generated inside a Monster House.

Lastly, an invisible and invincible statue can be generated under special conditions. (glitches section).

Statue Details

Scout Statue

Displays locations of monsters and items on the map as if you have a Scout Bracelet equipped.
This effect will be granted anywhere on the current floor, no matter where the statue is located.

Great statue effect to have around, since it lets you detect shops and Monster Houses.
Use a Knockback Staff or Pull Staff to relocated it to a safe spot where monsters won't destroy it.

Identify Statue

Items are identified when they're picked up, similar to a Specialty Riceball's identification effect.
Only the pot will be identified if a pot with unidentified items inside is picked up.
A Wet Scroll will remain as Wet Scroll when picked up.

One of the best statue effects with zero downsides.
If you're already carrying some unidentified items, you can simply
place them on the ground and then pick them up again to identify them.
Particularly great if you come across one early on inside the Road to the End dungeon.

10 Damage Statue

All damage received by Shiren and monsters will be a fixed 10 points.
This includes even sources such as Quarter Staff, Counter Shield, and Landmine explosions.
However, Poison Grass remains at 5 points of damage,
and Landmine type explosions will still one-shot monsters.

Useful when Shiren has low attack early game, or when inside the Halfway Well dungeon.
Can be used to one-shot a Cave Mamel at the beginning of the Road to the End dungeon.
That said, this statue becomes a liability the deeper you progress, since it increases
the number of hits needed to defeat monsters with troublesome special attacks.
In particular, it greatly increases the risk of Rancor and Intolerable slugs dividing.
It's best to destroy it right away when you don't need its effect.

Knockback Statue

Characters hit by regular attacks will receive 5 tiles of knockback when this statue is present.
Characters that bump into another character due to the knockback will receive 5 damage.
If the character is defeated as a result, the one who dealt the knockback
will be treated as the one that defeated that character.
Characters with fast movement or multiple attacks will stop after dealing knockback.
Counter Shield and Samuraidon's damage reflection doesn't take effect when knocked away.

Generally a positive statue, since it lets you defeat monsters without receiving counter attacks.
It can also be used to escape situations by having a monster intentionally knock you away.
The knockback takes effect when receving damage, so it can be used to reduce the damage
from multi-attack monsters like Lashaaga as well by canceling subsequent hits.

Decoy Statue

Monsters ignore Shiren and prioritize attacking this statue when present.
Archdragon and Porky family monsters will use special attacks at this statue if Shiren is not in view.
Unlike defeating a Snacky, the monster that destroys this statue will not level up.

It has lower durability than most statues, so it's not as useful as it may first appear.
Often destroyed by monsters before you get the chance to come across it.

Ouch Statue

Attacks all characters standing next to the statue each turn, dealing a fixed 18 damage.
It ignores Sanctuary Scrolls, but otherwise it's treated as a regular attack, meaning
it won't attack around corners, and will occasionally miss.
Bracelets will take damage, and it will send you flying if a Knockback Statue is present.
Equipment effects take effect from its attacks. (Counter Shield, Parry Bracelet, 弟 seal, etc.)
The statue will not attack Shiren if he has the Trapper status.

Has the highest durability among statues, requiring 8~12 hits to destroy it.
Your adventure might come to an end if you happen to step on a Sleep Trap in range of it,
so it's best to move it out of the way using a Knockback Staff if it's in the way.
Dosukoi family monsters will still try to push it while being sliced up.

The statue won't attack Shiren inside the Halfway Well dungeon,
so you can walk around the statue in circles to have it defeat monsters that are chasing you.
However, note that you won't gain much experience points if you do so.

Stone Toss Statue

Throws a stone with 30 attack power at a random character in the room.
It's not guaranteed to throw one every turn, but occasionally throws multiple turns in a row.
30 attack power means it can deal significant damage when your defense is still low early on,
but thankfully it doesn't seem to spawn on lower floors. (Only appears 30F+ in Road to the End).
Since it's not fixed damage, you can reduce the damage you take by increasing your defense.
It won't throw stones if a Hoarder Statue is present, and will not throw stones
at Shiren if he has the Trapper status.

Can be particularly troublesome if one gets generated on an island.
It can throw stones at monsters too, but the stones won't deal much damage against late game
monsters with only 30 attack, and you can't count on it to throw them at monsters consistently.
This statue can be destroyed in 3~5 hits unlike Ouch Statue, so it's best to just destroy it.
Equipping a Reflect Shield will make this statue a complete non-issue.

Explosion Statue

Explodes with the power of a Big Landmine when destroyed.
Has the lowest durability among all statues, breaking and exploding in only 1~2 hits.
Can be used to one-shot troublesome monsters, but can be unreliable due to its durability value.
Piko Hammer family monsters can sometimes be seen self-destructing to it.

Hoarder Statue

Prevents items from being placed or thrown.
Shiren and monsters won't drop items if they trip when this statue is present.
Monsters that throw or shoot items will effectively be sealed,
including Kengo family monsters' equipment disarming special attack.
However, monsters can still drop items when defeated.

It can be beneficial in the sense of sealing monsters with ranged attacks or Kengo monsters
from knocking your equipment away, but there aren't many opportunities to make use of it.
Unlike No Magic or Pierce statues, it doesn't really cause costly accidents for Shiren.

Sleep Statue

Spreads sleeping gas in intervals, putting all characters in the room to sleep for 5 turns.
The statue only spreads gas after 6 or more turns have elapsed since the previous activation.
Characters inside walls won't fall asleep, and Shiren will be exempt if he has the Trapper status.

You'll be able to move a minimum of 1 turn after waking up before it spreads gas again,
but it can be annoying since it slows down the pace of the game.
A monster entering the room while you're asleep can be quite dangerous, so it's best to
destroy the statue as soon as possible, ideally using a Fire Staff to do so in a single turn.

No Magic Statue

Nullifies staff and scroll effects, including thrown staves and magic bolts from outside the room.
Sanctuary and Explosion scrolls placed on the ground will be nullified as well.
However, Balance Staff still prevents tripping, and Squid Sushi Scroll can still restore fullness.

It can effectively seal King Tusker family monsters, but the negatives outweigh the positives.
In particular, it greatly limits your options if you're surrounded by multiple enemies,
so it's best to keep an escape item like Warp Grass on hand in case you run into this statue.

Since it nullifies Knowledge Staff, this in-game description cannot be seen during normal play:

[No Magic Statue]
Scroll and staff effects in
the room will be nullified.
Applies to both you
and monsters.

Pierce Statue

Thrown items will pierce through objects and walls, like having a Pierce Bracelet equipped.
Items also pierce through Reflect Shield and Tonosamadon's ability to reflect items.
Applies to enemies as well, but no effect on items that are thrown in an arc,
such as Kappa family monsters using their special attack to throw items.

This statue likely has the highest potential for causing accidents.
From losing items by throwing them at Mixers to losing equipment from Kengo disarming you,
there's a lot to watch out for when a Pierce Statue is present.
Shiren 2 doesn't have a command to take a look around the room,
so be cautious of unknown statues in the room that are outside of your view.
It may be best to choose actions based on the assumption that the statue is a Pierce Statue.

Bomber Statue

Statue with the appearance of a Droplet Ninja, though the effect is closer to Big Landmine Ninja.
Monsters defeated in a room with this statue will explode with the power of a Big Landmine.
Any monsters next to the defeated monster will also explode in a chain-reaction.
Monsters can still drop items, but the item will vanish if it's hit by a subsequent explosion.
Unlike Explosion Statue, this statue won't explode when it's destroyed.

This statue makes it extremely difficult to fight monsters inside the room it's located in,
since Shiren will take heavy damage as a result of defeating monsters at close range,
especially if a chain-reaction occurs when there are multiple monsters next to Shiren.
Ally monsters will also explode, so caution is needed when carrying Monster Pots.
It may be best to assume an unknown statue in the room is this statue,
since it rivals No Magic and Pierce statues in terms of dangerousness.

Trip Statue

Occasionally makes all characters in the room trip. Shiren won't trip if he has the Trapper status.

Has the potential to cause costly accidents by shattering pots or dropping items into water.
Traders and Water Striders might pick up your items as well, potentially leading to item loss.
However, monsters also drop items when tripped, so this statue can be used to collect items.
Traders drop merchandise instead of Vouchers when tripped, allowing you to steal items.
Recommended to collect items until the wind blows if you find this statue in Road to the End.

Gitan Statue

Defeated enemies are guaranteed to be turned into Gitan.
Riceball Statue will take priority if both Gitan and Riceball statues are present.

The amount of Gitan per drop isn't very large, but it can be a nice way to make money early on.
Can also be used to stock up on Gitan inside Storage Pots to be used as projectiles.

Riceball Statue

Defeated enemies are guaranteed to be turned into a Riceball.
The Riceball drop is separate from regular item drops, so monsters can still drop a second item.

Excellent statue that can be used to increase max fullness.
However, it unforunately doesn't appear in Road to the End. (Only Onigashima and Halfway Well).

Sturdy Trap Statue

Modeled after a Traproid monster, which don't make an appearance in Shiren 2.
Exclusively found in Halfway Well, and prevents all traps in the room from breaking.
Mallets and Piko Hammer monsters won't be able to destroy traps while inside the room either.
Monsters can be trapped without concern of traps breaking, so it's an excellent chance to level up.

This statue always faces downward, even when moved by items such as a Knockback Staff.
This is likely because Traproid monsters don't appear, and other angles aren't in the game.

Knowledge Staff cannot be found inside Halfway Well,
so this in-game description cannot be seen during normal play:

[Sturdy Trap Statue]
Most traps in the room
will be indestructible
when this statue is present.

Gravity Statue

Floating monsters can be trapped when this statue is present. Exclusive to Halfway Well.
The statue has a nice effect, but it's often not around when you need it, so don't count on it.
It also only affects monsters inside the same room, so it's somewhat lackluster overall.
That said, you could read a Wall Collapse Scroll to make it apply to the entire floor.

Knowledge Staff cannot be found inside Halfway Well,
so this in-game description cannot be seen during normal play:

[Gravity Statue]
Floating monsters will
also activate traps
while inside a room
with this statue.
