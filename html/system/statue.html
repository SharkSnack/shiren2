What are Statues?

Statues are one of the new gimmicks introduced in Shiren 2, and are modeled after various monsters.
They can be found in all dungeons starting from Onigashima except for Shrine's Hidden Hole.
They're said to be made by Shuten Summit residents, but also appear in Sealed Room, so they're a bit of a mystery.
A continuous mechanical sound can be heard when Shiren is within a 11 tile radius, so they're easy to identify.

Statues provide various effects to nearby characters.
Some are positive and can be of help to Shiren, but others can have a negative effect.
Select negative effect statues will switch to affecting only monsters when Shiren has the Trapper status.

Generally, the range of a statue inside a room is either room-wide, or a 1 tile radius around the statue.
However, the tiles along to walls are exempt from a room-wide effect.
If the floor is one giant room with limited view, a room-wide statue will only affect characters in a 11 tile radius.
If a Wall Collapse Scroll is read, the a room-wide statue will affect the entire floor.
That said, there are some exceptions - Ouch Statue always only affects characters within a 1 tile radius,
and Scout Statue always affects the entire floor, no matter where it's located.

Statues will crumble after being attacked a number of times, with the durability varying based on individual statues.
It's best to destroy negative effect statues as soon as you come across them to lower the risk of accidents.
The number of hits needed to destroy statues is as follows:
Explosion Statue (1~2) ≦ Decoy Statue (2~5) ≦ Base Statue Durability (3~5) ≦ Ouch Statue (8~12).
If fire is generated on a tile where a statue is located (Blaze Scroll or Fire Staff), the statue will vanish on that turn.
It's good to remember that for statues that are difficult to approach, such as Ouch, Bomber, and Sleep statues.
Landmine and Big Landmine type explosions will make statues vanish in a single turn as well.

Statues can be moved using a Knockback Staff or Pull Staff, but Swap Staff doesn't have an effect on them.
Dosukoi family monsters can sometimes be seen pushing statues around as well.

Enemies generally avoid attacking statues, but will attack them if the statue is blocking their path.
Piko Hammer family monsters will attempt to break statues in addition to visible traps.
Allies sometimes target statues if they were attacked by them, such as Ouch Statue or Stone Toss Statue.

Statues are generally treated as items when being generated, so they can be commonly found on islands.
They can also be generated blocking room entrances, but monsters usually destroy those right away.
However, statues will never be generated inside a Monster House.

Lastly, an invisible and invincible statue can be generated under special conditions. (glitches section).

Statue Details

Scout Statue

Displays locations of monsters and items on the map as if you have a Scout Bracelet equipped.
This effect will be granted anywhere on the current floor, no matter where the statue is located.

One of the best statue effects to have around, since it lets you detect shops and Monster Houses.
Use a Knockback Staff or Pull Staff to relocated it to a safe spot where monsters won't destroy it.
