Monster House Characteristics

Monster House is a location in a dungeon where a room is packed with many monsters and items.
They are generated at random, and can be generated in dungeons starting with Onigashima.
The ground is also covered in hidden traps, so care is needed when moving around the room.

It's best to eat Sight Grass if you have it on hand, but otherwise check for traps one tile at a time.
If you successfully clear out a Monster House, you can claim the items as a reward.
Whether Monster Houses are positive or negative generally comes down to a player's skill.

The message "It's a (Monster Type) House!" is displayed when you first step foot into a Monster House,
and the music changes to one of two Monster House themes, depending on the type.

Every monster in the room will wake up the moment you enter the room,
and the location of all monsters on the current floor becomes visible on the map. (Like Scout Bracelet)

Special Monster Houses can include high level monsters outside of the dungeon's normal monster table,
making them extremely dangerous. Though that also works in reverse, where Special Monster Houses
in deeper floors can contain low level monsters, possibly making them easier than regular Monster Houses.

Note that Statues will never be generated inside of a Monster House.
The developers likely realized how chaotic it would be if a Bomber Statue or Trip Statue were present.

Monster House Strategy

Detecting Beforehand

Multiple monsters or items visible near room entrance
There's a chance the room is a Monster House.
The likeliness increases further if you shoot an arrow into the room and hit an unseen monster.
Avoid entering the room if you don't have any items needed to take on a Monster House.
Room with many monsters visible using a Scout Bracelet
Guaranteed to be a Monster House.
Skip entering the room if you don't have any items needed to tackle a Monster House.
Check the sleeping monsters with the bracelet equipped to determine the type of Monster House.
The "It's a Monster House!" message randomly appeared
On rare occasions, a Dosukoi family monster can use its special attack to shift one of the sleeping monsters
outside of the Monster House, which results in the Monster House activating even if you're nowhere near it.
It's particularly annoying when there's a Landmine in the room, since a shifted item or monster could end up
landing on top of the Landmine, triggering an explosion and wiping out some of the items.

Good Items for Monster Houses

If you detect a Monster House beforehand
Thin out the number of monsters in the room before entering, using items such as:
Bracelets: Pierce, Bend, Phantasm
Projectiles: Wooden Arrow, Iron Arrow, Razor Arrow, Porky Stone, Cannonball

Picking off monsters one at a time from outside of the room is an excellent way to give yourself an advantage.
Monsters that receive any damage will instantly wake up, so be mindful of that when using items that pierce.
If you unknowingly step into a Monster House
Take one step back so you end up in a hallway, only having to fight one enemy at a time.
You can hit three monsters per attack at the entrance if you have a Razor Wind or Morning Star.
If there are multiple entrances, or monsters with ranged attacks are present, take another step back.
You'll be safe from ranged attacks, and monsters that lost sight of Shiren will wander somewhere else.
Clearing out the Monster House
Use the items mentioned below to give yourself an advantage!

Weapons

三 Seal (Razor Wind)
Swing your weapon at a room's entrance to deal damage to three enemies at once.
Simple yet powerful seal that should be synthesized onto any main weapon whenever possible.
Morning Star
Two-handed weapon that can be used like Razor Wind.
You might be tempted to remain in the Monster House to deal 5 hits, but it's far too dangerous with 0 defense.
Spear
Regular attacks can pierce through enemies 2 tiles forward.
As long as you don't miss, you can defeat enemies without damage by taking 2 steps back at the beginning.
回 Seal (Healing Sword)
Excellent for restoring HP during extended fights.
The standard must-have seal for any main weapon, along with the 三 seal (Razor Wind).
Type Effective Weapons (Drain Buster, Ghost Sickle, etc.)
These seals shine when you're up against Special Monster Houses.
Pickaxe, Wonder Pickaxe
Dig around the edge of the room before entering a Monster House to thin out monsters along the walls.
Be careful not to dig too much, resulting in an expanded room with nowhere to run.

Shields

見 Seal (Evasive Shield)
Increased evasion means you take less damage during extended fights.
バ Seal (Counter Shield)
You can get by without it, but it helps increase damage output during extended fights.
弟 Seal (Otogiriso)
Effectively reduces the amount of damage you take.
眠 Seal (Slumber Scroll)
Helps if you don't have enough attack power to defeat enemies in a single hit.
Can also help to shut down monsters that move or attack through walls.

Bracelets

Pierce Bracelet
Can be used to thin out the number of monsters before entering the room,
or to clear out the line of monsters that chase after you as a result of waking the room up.
Watch out for Shihan when relying on bracelets such as this one, since it has the ability to disarm bracelets.
Check out the How to Use Pierce Bracelet section for more tips.
Bend Bracelet
Can be used to attack monsters along the walls before entering the room.
Strength Bracelet
Higher strength means you're more likely to defeat enemies in a single hit.
Phantasm Bracelet
Can be used to defeat monsters along the walls before entering the room.
Hide inside a wall after waking the room up to clear the room without taking damage.
Trapper Bracelet
Monster Houses are full of traps, so enemies will trigger traps left and right.
However, note that dangerous traps like Landmine or Flood Trap could be present,
and Shiren's attack is drastically lowered while he has Trapper Bracelet equipped.

Scrolls

Trap Activate Scroll
Similar to Trapper Bracelet, but guaranteed to activate all traps in the room when read.
Always keep in mind that you could end up triggering a Landmine or Flood Trap.
Lightning Scroll
Deals roughly 30 damage to all enemies in the room.
One scroll is enough to clear out an entire Monster House early on, but it isn't as effective on deeper floors.
That said, it can still be a nice way to soften enemies up so that you can defeat them in a single attack.
Blaze Scroll
Deals 20 damage per turn to any character standing in the fire that generates from reading the scroll.
Items on the ground will burn up, and tiles with traps will not be set on fire.
It's best to read this in a hallway to minimize item loss.
Slumber Scroll
Puts all monsters in the room to sleep for 10 turns.
Note that mosnters will move at double speed after waking up from this effect.
It's best to read this when the exit is in the middle of a Monster House, rather than rely on it to clear one out.
Decoy Scroll
Provides a big advantage while inside the room, but only lasts for 10 turns.
It's best to either defeat monsters quickly, or retreat before it wears off and put yourself in a better position.
Avoid reading it when you don't have enough space to summon shadows.
Trap Remove Scroll
Nice to read after clearing out the Monster House.
Fear Scroll
Plays well with the 三 seal (Razor Wind).
Can be used to have a monster defeat another monster, but you'd need a way to defeat the leveled up monster.
Blank Scroll
Can be used as any scroll that you've read in the past.
Choose an option that fits your current situation and needs.
Sanctuary Scroll
Prevents monsters from using regular attacks when they are next to you.
Note that ranged attacks and special attacks from a distance can still hit you.
Take a step back and place it in a hallway if there was a trap at the entrace of the room.

Staves

Swap Staff
Useful when you want to quickly move toward the room's entrace, or if the stairs is in the middle of the room.
Note that the trap is guaranteed to activate if one is present on the destination tile.
Slow Staff
Swing it at the first monster following you into a hallway to have an easier time escaping the situation.
Fire Staff
Retreat into a hallway and swing it to burn the line of monsters that followed you.
Use alongside Silver Arrows or Pierce Bracelet to quickly clear out enemies.
Tunnel Staff
Create new hallways, allowing you to thin out more monsters using projectiles before entering the room.
Nice when Bored Kappa family mosnters are waiting on top of items, or if you don't have a Pierce Bracelet.
Be careful not to dig in a way that expands the room with you standing inside of it.
Miss Staff
Effectively shuts down a monster's regular attacks.
Plays well with Razor Wind, since you can use a monster as a barricade while attacking the sides.
Invisible Staff
Invisible monsters move in a similar fashion to confusion, so this staff makes it easier to escape.
Possession Staff
Nice to have for Special Monster Houses, where you can take control of a high level monster.
Be careful of ranged attacks while you're controlling a monster.

Pots

Grass Effect Pot
Monster Houses are one of the few situations where Grass Effect Pots have a notable use.
Check out the How to Use Grass Effect Pot section for tips.
Sticky Pot
You might be able to slow down the train of monsters by throwing it at the first monster following you.
Throwing it with a Pierce Bracelet equipped lets you trap multiple monsters,
and throwing a sturdy Sticky Pot will trap a monster inside the pot until it's defeated by an attack.

Grass

Twisty Grass
Use with a Pierce Bracelet or Grass Effect Pot to lower the level of multiple monsters at once.
Swift Grass
Eat to move faster, allowing you to act twice per turn and either run away or fight at an advantage.
Warp Grass
Eat it when you're cornered and need to escape the situation.
Talk Seed
Inflicts a random effect to all monsters in the room, possibly slowing or paralyzing enemies.
Sight Grass
Lets you skip having to manually check for traps.
Can be used to locate a Pitfall to advance to the next floor.
Revival Grass
Great to have as backup in case something goes wrong.

Projectiles

Silver Arrow
Retreat into a hallway and shoot to deal damage to a line of monsters.
Razor Arrow
Lets you perform an attack in a pattern similar to a Silver Arrow + Razor Wind.
Has somewhat low attack power, and you'll want to be careful not to hit Shopkeepers or Traders.
Cannonball
Be careful not to self-destruct by getting caught in the explosion.

Dangerous Monsters

Snacky
Often found inside Monster Houses early on.
A monster defeating a Snacky can quickly turn the tide of the situation, depending on what monster levels up.
Dosukoi Family
Shifts items and monsters around, activating traps if anything moves onto a tile that has a hidden trap.
You might suddenly find yourself inside the Monster House, even if you were at the room's entrance.
Flood Traps and Landmines can cause problems, and landing on a Sleep Trap likely guarantees a collapse.
Kengo Family
Having your equipment disarmed can quickly turn a good situation into a bad one.
However, the scarier situation is item loss due to equipment hitting a monster after being knocked away.
Losing your equipment (especially your shield) can outright end an adventure on deeper floors.
Dragon Family
Sky Dragon's flame can reach you if you're at the room's entrance.
Dragons can level up by defeating other monsters, and Archdragon can breathe fire at you from anywhere.
It's best to avoid Monster Houses on floors where this monster family is present.
Pumphantasm Family
Can move and attack through walls.
Place a Sanctuary Scroll on the ground, or move into a different room to deal with them.
Typhoon
Can appear in Ghost Houses.
Moves at triple speed and has the ability to attack through walls.
Deal with it in a similar fashion to Pumphantasm family monsters.
Scorpion Family
Scorpion isn't much of an issue, but Demon Scorpion and Stun Scorpion have terrifying special attacks.
Gazer Family
Its hypnosis not only wastes a turn, but has the potential to unequip your shield or consume Revival Grass.
If you don't have the 山 seal (Echo Shield), use items to avoid giving it a chance to use its special attack.
Porky Family
Its ranged attacks can quickly wear down your HP.
Often leads to a collapse if you run into a Power House early on.
Tiger Tosser Family
Has the potential to block exits with the monsters it throws.
Horse Ronin Family
Shoots Silver Arrows from a distance.
Can level up as a result of its arrows piercing through other monsters.
Water Strider
Can potentially throw Gitan or other high damage items that are on the ground.
Bored Kappa Family
Moves toward items and throws them at you.
Bow Boy Family
Its special attacks can potentially defeat other monsters, resulting in level ups.
Cranky Tank quickly destroys walls and puts you in an unsafe situation.
Recommended to use a Water Pot to seal Cranky Tank if you have one on hand.
Wolf Droid Family
High stats, and has the potential to level up as a result of its bomb explosions.
Sheep Priest Family
Getting hit by a stat lowering special attack makes it difficult to defeat enemies.
King Tusker Family
Can seal, slow, or quarter your current HP depending on the monster's level.
Bat Kangaroo Family
Provides support for other monsters in the room.
Swift Staff and Heal Staff are particularly troublesome,
but Devil Kangaroo's Invisible Staff might actually give you some time to escape.
Gyadon Family
Can pluck items, weakning them and dropping the item on the ground below.
It's particularly bad if it plucks an item you were planning on using, since it'll fall to the enemy side.
If you don't have the ト seal (Walrus Shield), don't hesitate to use items like Seal Staff.

Monster House Types

Regular Monster Houses include monsters only from the current floor's monster table.
Such Monster Houses are called "Normal (Regular) Monster House", or simply "Monster House".

All other Monster Houses are called "Special Monster Houses".
These can only be found in Sealed Room, Road to the End, and Shrine's Hidden Hole between 1~99F.

Special Monster Houses can include monsters outside of the current floor's monster table,
including high level monsters. (ex: Gigahead, Porkton, Typhoon on 12F of Road to the End).
They're typically extremely dangerous, especially if you begin the floor in the middle of one.
However, a single Sanctuary Scroll is usually enough to deal with Cyclops, Drain, and Ghost houses.

Lastly, the music differs between Monster Houses and Special Monster Houses.

Monster House (Regular)

Monsters from the current floor's monster table make an appearance.
Has a much higher chance of being generated compared to Special Monster Houses.

It's best to thin out monsters from outside the room before stepping inside.
Use items like arrows to wake up and defeat enemies one at a time.

Ghost House

  • Bone Dragon Family
  • Pumphantasm Family
  • Gale Family
  • Yamikichi Family

Typhoons detect Shiren from any location, and chase him at triple speed while passing through walls.
Pumphantasm can also attack through walls, so it really helps to have the 仏 seal (Ghost Sickle).
Ghost Houses are one of the few situations where Grass Effect Pot can truly shine,
since you can insert an Otogiriso to deal 100 damage to all monsters in the room while healing yourself.

Drain House

  • Twisty Hani
  • Scorpion Family
  • Slug Family
  • Hunger Puppet Family

These enemies don't have faster movement or ranged attacks, but instead have troublesome special attacks.
Items like Salty Bracelet, Song Bracelet, Level Lock Bracelet, and Scale Shield can block the effects.
Weapons with the ド seal (Drain Buster) will give you an advantage, and a Sanctuary Scroll
can be used to prevent Drain type monsters from attacking or using their special attacks.

Cyclops House

  • Ironhead Family
  • Golem Family
  • Gazer Family

Two high powered Ironhead and Golem families alongside the Gazer family could very well cause trouble.
Weapons with the 目 seal (Cyclops Killer) will give you an advantage here.
The biggest threat is Gazer's hypnosis, so it's nice to have the 山 seal (Echo Shield) as well.
Ironhead family mosnters can level up if they defeat monsters standing between Shiren and themselves,
so be careful where you stand to avoid ending up with an extra Gigahead in the room.

Power House

  • Ironhead Family
  • Golem Family
  • Porky Family
  • Tiger Tosser Family
  • Bone Dragon Family
  • Shaaga Family

Many of these monsters not only have high attack, but also have high HP.
One of the most dangerous types due to the presence of both Porky and Tiger Tosser families.
Porkton can throw stones from up to 10 tiles away, even if you're standing on a Sanctuary Scroll.
竜 (Dragon Killer) and 龍 (Ryujin Sword) seals will help against Shaaga and Bone Dragon families,
while the 目 seal (Cyclops Killer) will help against Ironhead and Golem families.
It's a great opportunity to earn lots of experience, but it might be best to ignore this house due to the risk.

Throwing House

  • Porky Family
  • Tiger Tosser Family
  • Horse Ronin Family
  • Bored Kappa Family
  • Water Strider

Sanctuary Scrolls won't help against projectiles, and Porky monsters toss stones even if you enter hallways.
The 身 (Dodge Shield) or 返 (Reflect Shield) seals offer protection against projectiles, but said shields
can only be obtained in Sealed Room, meaning you have to tackle this type of Special Monster House
without them in Road to the End and Shrine's Hidden Hole.

Monsters like Horse Ronin and Tiger Tosser can level up by dealing damage to other monsters.
Bored Kappa monsters and Water Striders will throw items found on the ground as well,
potentially leaving you with nothing to collect as a reward for tackling the Monster House.
Generally, it's best to ignore this type if you discover it while wearing a Scout Bracelet.

Magic House

  • Bat Kangaroo Family
  • King Tusker Family
  • Sheep Priest Family
  • Gyadon Family

These monsters can be quite dangerous if you don't have the 山 seal (Echo Shield).
Oddly, Gyadon family monsters are included in this type. (Maybe it was supposed to be Gazers?)

There's a lot that could go wrong between Bat Kangaroos speeding monsters up, King Tusker and
Sheep Priest monsters weakening you, and Gyadon monsters plucking your equipment and items.
The ト seal (Walrus Shield) will prevent Gyadon monsters from being able to pluck your items.
Don't underestimate the attack strength of invisible monsters when a Devil Kangaroo is present.
You don't want to take a hit from a level 3 monster such as Emperor Tusker early on.
