Monster Kingdom

Monster Kingdom is a zoo type area for monsters that unlocks after clearing Onigashima.
Monsters captured using Monster Pots can be registered and made to live here.
You can take or deposit monsters freely, and monsters registered to Monster Kingdom
will return here in the event that they are defeated in a dungeon.

Monster Kingdom Zones

Zones are areas within the Kingdom where you can enjoy viewing monsters that have been registered.
Monsters are organized by family, with the exclusion of N'dubbas which have individual enclosures.

The sign next to an enclosure provides descriptions for each monster within the enclosure.
The same descriptions are displayed if you swing or throw a Knowledge Staff at a monster.
Monster attack animations can also be enjoyed by idling after selecting a monster name,
though some monsters won't use their special attack animations while inside Monster Kingdom.

Overall, one could say it's Shiren 2's most popular relaxation spot.
It might be fun to visit these zones every now and then, and recall where you met each monster.

Aiming for Completion

Useful Monsters

Armordillo Family
Possibly the most useful monster in the game.
Use its special attack to quickly obtain Monster Pots and level up items from enemies.
Armordillos can be captured early on in any dungeon besides Shuten Trail Mid.
Mixer Family
Living Synthesis Pot that can be used to quickly strengthen equipment.
Registering a Mixer to Monster Kingdom allows you to synthesize from inside Napus Storage.
Send out → throw items → defeat using 火 seal → collect equipment → take out Mixer Pot again.
Be careful not to return it to its pot or exit the storage before defeating it.
Kengo Family
Disliked as an enemy, but treasured as an ally.
Its special attack lets you collect Shields (Kengo, Iai, Shihan), Weapons (Iai, Shihan), and Bracelets (Shihan).
Collect equipment with positive upgrade values in Shuten Trail Easy or Shrine's Hidden Hole,
and look for equipment with useful seals in Onigashima.
Snacky
Capture a higher level monster by letting an enemy monster defeat an ally Snacky.
Gazer Family
Can be used to level up an enemy monster when 2 monsters are present.
Simply confuse one or both monsters, and then wait until one defeats the other.
Monsoon Doll
Nice to have when capturing Tonosamadon and Mixer Family Monsters.
It has a growth limit of 50, so it's a solid choice for general use as well.
Gazelle Pope
Can be used to perform the Gazelle Pope glitch to quickly register monsters to Monster Kingdom.
However, do keep in mind that using the glitch is cheating, so it's not recommended.

Useful Items

Always ensure safety before leveling up enemy monsters.
It's best to keep Revival Grass and Escape Scroll on hand to avoid losing items.

Monster Pot
Required to capture monsters.
Obtained from Shrine's Hidden Hole, and can be used in any dungeon that allows carry-in items.
Happy Grass
Throw at an enemy monster to level it up.
Happy Staff
Swing at an enemy monster to level it up.
Obtained from Sealed Room (Presto Pot), Onigashima (Specialty Shop), or Road to the End.
Grass Gainer, Grass Tosser
Insert Happy Grass to basically turn it into a Happy Staff.
Soil (Castle Material)
Similar to defeating Snacky, enemy monsters that defeat Figures made from Soil will level up.
Can be helpful to weaken the Figure beforehand to ensure it loses against the desired monster.
Fear Scroll
Read when 2 enemies are lined up in a hallway to have one defeat the other and level up.
Parry Bracelet
Found in Sealed Room (1st Golden Interval).
Equip it when you're surrounded by multiple enemies to redirect damage
and have a monster level up by defeating another monster.
Possession Staff
Take control of a monster and use it to block a room entrance or hallway to have
another monster defeat the monster you took control of, resulting in leveling up.
Unlucky Staff
Occasionally found on the floor in Onigashima. Use it to when you need to catch a lower level monster.
Seal Staff
Tonosamadon must be sealed in order to be captured, so you'll need this, Monsoon Doll, or King Tusker.
Evaporate, Freeze, Wall Collapse Scrolls
Read these scrolls to capture Water element monsters, including Shotfish and Aqua Dragon families.
Note that these scrolls only take effect in Random Dungeons, and should be read outside of Shuten Trails.

Tricky or Tedious to Obtain Monsters

Most monsters can be captured between the Shuten Trails and Onigashima, or Shrine's Hidden Hole.
If you plan on capturing many monsters outside of Shrine's Hidden Hole, you can use an ally Armorillo
inside Shrine's Hidden Hole to obtain lots of Monster Pots, and then store them in Mamo's drawers.

The trickiest monsters to capture are likely Queen Spider and each of the N'dubba colors.

Monsters Requiring Extra Steps
Mixer Family
It swallows Monster Pots like any other item, so you need to either seal it or throw
unneeded items until it reaches the eating limit before throwing the Monster Pot.
The number of items each level can swallow are: Mixer (2), Mixermon (3), and Mixergon (4).
If you'd rather seal it, you can use a Seal Staff, Monsoon Doll, or King Tusker.
Tonosamadon
It reflects thrown items including Monster Pots, so it needs to be sealed first.
Use a Seal Staff, Monsoon Doll, or King Tusker to seal it.
Copycat Mask Family
It's always disguised as another monster when it's generated, so you need to reveal its disguise first.
You can do so by getting hit by a regular attack while equipping a shield with the 識 seal (Identify Scroll),
or by sealing using a Seal Staff, Monsoon Doll, or King Tusker.
This monster family cannot be found in Sealed Room, so search between 22~23F of Onigashima instead.
It has somewhat low attack, so it's easiest to level it up using Happy Grass
or Happy Staff instead of trying to have it defeat another monster.
Zalokleft, Gamarra Families
These two monster families basically have 0 attack power,
so use items like Happy Grass or Happy Staff to level them up instead.
Mr. Drone Family
Capturing it while it's hovering over water will result in a submerged Monster Pot,
so be extra careful when capturing it in Shuten Trail Mid (6~7F),
since Freeze, Evaporate, and Wall Collapse scrolls don't take effect in Shuffle Dungeon maps.
Use items like Happy Grass or Happy Staff to level it up.
Aqua Dragon Family
Only generated inside water, so capturing it by throwing a Monster Pot will result in a submerged pot.
Either use a Swap Staff to force it onto land, or read an Evaporate, Freeze, or Wall Collapse scroll.
Shotfish Family
Always generated underwater, so it cannot be hit by thrown items.
Read an Evaporate or Wall Collapse Scroll to force it onto land in order to capture it.
Hakkeyoi and Gottsuan can potentially shift the Shotfish onto land with their special attack as well.
Can be leveled up while it's underwater by having it defeat a Figure or Snacky.
Monsters In Limited Locations
Kengo, Mixer Families
Cannot be found in Shrine's Hidden Hole, so you'll need to visit a different dungeon like Shuten Trail Hard.
Gyadon, Dosukoi, Orcan, Schubell Families
Cannot be found in the Shuten Trails or Onigashima.
Look for them in Sealed Room or Shrine's Hidden Hole instead.
Easiest to capture the entire group in Sealed Room using items like Happy Grass or Happy Staff.
Poko Hammer, Pako Hammer
These two can only be found naturally in Halfway Well.
Recommended to use Happy Grass or Happy Staff in Shuten Trail Easy.
Tiny Phoenix
Only found on floors 50F+ of Shrine's Hidden Hole, or 20F+ of Sealed Room.
Faster to capture it in Sealed Room, provided you've upgraded your equipment.
Escargon
Rarely found on floors 50F+ inside Sealed Room.
Also appears between floors 40~80F of Road to the End, but cannot be found in Shrine's Hidden Hole.
Taurus Family
Only found in Sealed Room or Road to the End.
In both dungeons, Minotaurs appear on floors 78F+, and Megataurs appear on floors 86F+.
Taurus cannot be found anywhere, so you'll need to use Twisty Grass, Unlucky Seed, or Unlucky Staff.
Queen Spider
Only found on floors 60F+ inside Shrine's Hidden Hole.
Note that Queen Spider no longer spawns in Shuten Trail Hard after clearing Onigashima.
If you want to complete Monster Kingdom, there's no way around having to capture it in Shrine's Hidden Hole.
Practice the techniques and use the information found on this site to reach 60F. You can do it!
N'dubbas
The age of a N'dubba is equivalent to its level.
For example, if you swing a Happy Staff at a N'dubba 1yr (green), it becomes N'dubba 2yr (green).
And if you swing the staff at a N'dubba 1yr (green) 19 times, it becomes N'dubba 20yr (pink).
So it's much faster to search for specific ages in Sealed Room, rather than relying on level up items.

The floor number in Sealed Room directly correlates to N'dubba's age.
So you can search in the following floor ranges for specific colors:
Green: 1~19F, Pink: 20~29F, Blue: 30~59F, Red: 60~69F, Light Blue: 70~76F,
Yellow: 77~87F, Gray: 88~89F, Light Green: 90~98F, White: 99F

N'dubbas can be picked up by swapping places with an ally that is standing on top of a disguised N'dubba.
This allows you to store N'dubbas in Storage Pots, removing the need to bring Monster Pots into Sealed Room.
You can't register disguised N'dubbas to Monster Kingdom, but the disguise will be canceled
if you throw the N'dubba while inside a dungeon, allowing you to capture it.
Note that throwing a disguised N'dubba inside Napus Storage won't cancel the disguise.

Gazelle Pope Glitch

If you defeat an ally monster that had their level halved by a Gazelle Pope, the lower level monster
will get added to Monster Kingdom, and the previous monster returns to Monster Kingdom if registered.
If you do decide to use this glitch, be sure not to perform it on unregistered monsters.

Growth will be halved for both the newly registered monster and the monster used for the glitch.

Necessary Items
Gazelle Pope Pot
Monster Pot (containing a monster of the same family + higher level than the lower level monster you want).
Possession Staff

Send out both monsters inside an area like Napus Storage, take control of Gazelle Pope using
the Possession Staff, lower the level of the target monster, and then simply defeat the target monster.
Be careful not to hit Shiren if you use Gazelle Pope to defeat the target monster.
Also note that you can send out multiple targets to lower the number of Possession Staff uses.

※ Lv4 monsters result in Lv2 monsters, so a Lv3 monster is needed separately for families with 4 levels.

The Monster Kingdom completion events can progress out of order if this glitch is used
to register the last monster needed for completion, so it's best to avoid doing so.

Lastly, Twisty Hani cannot lower ally monster levels, so it cannot be used in place of Gazelle Pope.

Growth Limit Breaking

Requirements
Gazelle Pope (ally or enemy)
Level 2 or higher monster (ally)
※ ally monster must have a higher growth limit after having their level halved.

Ally monsters can still grow stronger while their level is halved by a Gazelle Pope, so the idea is to
halve the level of a monster with low max growth → max out growth before the level down effect
wears off, at which point max growth returns to the lower value of the higher level monster.

Example: N'dubba 99yr has a max growth of 0, and N'dubba 50yr (halved) has a max growth of 52.

However, note that:

  • Any growth over the limit won't be reflected in the monster's stats.
  • Defeating an enemy in a growth limit broken state resets growth to the regular max value.
  • Sending out the monster also resets growth to the regular max value.

So there's no benefit to stats, it can't be used in combat, and it can't be sent outside of its pot.
That said, limit broken monsters can be registered to Monster Kingdom while retaining their growth,
so it could be fun to try for a "perfect" Monster Kingdom with limit broken monsters as trophies.

If you complete Monster Kingdom...

The following rewards are granted when you complete Monster Kingdom:

  • A Mamel icon gets added to your save file.
  • One-time exclusive Carp Streamer Castle Decoration is given to you. (Don't lose it!)
  • A new dungeon, Shrine's Hidden Hole (B), is unlocked.
  • Muchigoro gives you a Fast Clear Campaign Password.
  • More visitors arrive, including NPCs from the nearby Nabus Village.
