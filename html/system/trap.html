Traps

Traps are found on the ground, and activate when stepped on, or if an object is thrown or dropped on top of them.

Allies who are thrown onto traps receive the same effect as Shiren stepping on them,
but ally monsters receive the effect of a monster stepping on them.
For example, if Asuka is thrown onto a Landmine, she will receive damage equal to 50% of her current HP,
but if an ally Mamel is thrown onto a Landmine, it will be instantly defeated, no matter its current HP.

The "Break" column shows the chance of a trap breaking when Shiren has the trapper status
from either being inside the Halfway Well dungeon, or when he has a Trapper Bracelet equipped.

Traps can be destroyed by splashing water on them from a Water Pot.
However, traps cannot be destroyed by explosions or the effect of a Flood Trap.
Traps thrown into water will vanish with the message "[Trap name] disappeared..."
Piko Hammer family monsters can destroy visible traps, and swinging a Knockback Staff
at a trap so that it hits a monster will deal a small amount of damage and make the trap vanish.

Hidden traps cannot be detected by the [Ground] menu option,
but can still be destroyed by splashing water on them from a Water Pot.
Swinging a Mallet over a tile with a hidden trap will reveal the trap without destroying it,
and swinging the Mallet again over the visible trap will destroy the trap.
Hidden traps can be destroyed on the same turn using a Mallet if Shiren is under the effect of Sight Grass.

Damage dealt by traps increases as Shiren levels up when he has the Trapper status
from either being inside the Halfway Well dungeon, or by having a Trapper Bracelet equipped. (Trap Damage)
The "Dmg" column shows the amount of damage the trap deals when Shiren doesn't have the Trapper status.
Shiren's level is not a factor for the amount of damage a trap deals when Shiren doesn't have the Trapper status.

Experience Points

Shiren with the Trapper status gains experience points equal to 95% of the normal amount when he defeats
an enemy using a trap, or 75% of the normal amount if the trap doesn't defeat the enemy.
However, enemies defeated by a Spoil Trap will be the same amount as not defeating an enemy using other traps.
Lastly, Shiren only gains 20% of the normal amount if he defeats enemies using regular attacks.

Chain Trap Experience Points

Chain Traps activate all other traps in a 1-tile radius, potentially granting a large amount of experience points.
The chain can also be continued by placing traps at the end points of One-Way and Log traps,
or by placing Landmines in close proximity so that they explode one after the other.
The formula used in calculating experience points in a trap chain is shown below.

n = Number of traps that successfully trapped an enemy prior to the Chain Trap activating.
m = Numer of Chain Traps that triggered due to a trap that moves an enemy (max: 1),
       or the number of Landmines that exploded away from a Chain Trap.
N = Number of traps that successfully trapped an enemy due to a Chain Trap activating.
M = Number of traps activated by the Chain Trap that didn't trap an enemy.
L = Number of Landmines that exploded within a 1-tile radius of a Chain Trap.
(L isn't included in M, and the number of Chain Traps isn't counted for n or N) 


  Exp = Normal amount * (3/4) * {n^2 + n * m * (2 * N + 1) + (1/2) * N * (N + 3) + M * (n + 1) + L * (n + N / 2)}
  However, a Chain Trap on its own: Exp = Normal amount * (3 / 4)

If the trap defeated an enemy, the following is added to the amount of experience points:


  Normal amount * (1 / 5) * (n + N)
