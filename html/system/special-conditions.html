Special Conditions

Special conditions fall into two categories - status conditions (ailments) and Shiren-exclusive effects.
Status conditions and Shiren-exclusive effects both include negative and positive effects.
This page also includes information on how status conditions affect monsters.

Status Conditions (Ailments)

Shiren and monsters can be inflicted by effects called status conditions.
Conditions with negative effects are called ailments or "bad status", and restrict the character's actions.
Ailments can have a drastic impact toward combat or even your adventure as a whole.
It's good to have some basic knowledge for each one so that they can be utilized for benefit.
Since there are few conditions with positive effects, they're grouped with ailments.

Status Condition Basics

Most effects only last a certain number of turns, and are cured after the duration expires.
The status conditions that last for the entire time on the floor only apply to monsters.

Movement speed cannot be both slowed and hastened at the same time.
Think of it as the character's movement speed stage increasing or decreasing by 1.
There is one exception, however, where a Talk Seed effect can slow a character down
to 1 action every 2 turns even if they were moving at double or triple speed.

Paralysis is unrelated to movement speed, and can occur even when a character is hastened.
If a Slow Staff is swung at a character that already has slowed movement,
they will become paralyzed and their movement speed stage will reset.

Drinking Good Water or Perfect Water will cure all status conditions. (excluding hastened movement),
and being revived by Revival Grass will cure all status conditions.

Causes that have a monster name in brackets represent a special attack used by that monster.

Duration (turns) begins when Shiren first takes action after the character is inflicted by the condition.
For example, if a Devil Kangaroo swings its Invisible Staff at a monster,
in either case of the monster becoming invisible after acting or before acting,
the invisibility only ends after Shiren performs 20 turns worth of actions.
So the monster would have invisibility for 20 turns in the first case, and 21 turns in the second case.

Shiren Exclusive Conditions

Some special conditions are exclusive to Shiren.
Of these effects, ones that are caused by items can be particularly helpful during adventures.

Shiren Exclusive Condition Basics

Whereas status conditions include many negative effects, most Shiren-exclusive effects are positive,
and none of them wear off based on the number of turns that have passed.

Shiren Exclusive Condition List

The "Turn" column is used to show effect duration.
"Floor" in the Turn column means the effect lasts the entire time spent on the current floor.
