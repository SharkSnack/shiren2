Synthesis Seals

Seals are special abilities that can be synthesized onto weapons or shields.

Each seal is represented as single Japanese character, and the color depends on the source item.
The three colors are: equipment (blue), food/grasses (green), and scrolls (red).
Some seals like 金 can be found in two colors, and have a slightly different effect based on color.

Generally seals can make your adventure smoother, but some of them are a double-edged sword,
have a pointless effect, or have an outright negative effect, so some care is needed.

The Blacksmith at Shuten Halfway can erase unwanted seals for a fee.
Gyandora and Monsoon Doll's special attacks can also remove seals at random.

Stacked Effects

～Common Questions～
・Does having multiple 回 seals restore more HP? (Does it stack?) → No, only use 1 回 seal.
・Does having multiple 弟 seals restore more HP? (Does it stack?) → Yes, multiple 弟 seals restore more HP.
・Do seals have to be next to each other to stack the effects? → No, the order of seals doesn't matter.

～Stacking Seals With Similar Effects～

It's sometimes more cost efficient to mix different seals with similar effects, rather than 16 of the same seal.
(Similar effect seal examples: 天 and 幸, 竜 and 火, に and 飯, 龍 and 竜).
This is because multipliers are added together, reaching equal or greater numbers while using less seal slots.
Fire damage reduction seals on a shield, for example: 竜 + 火 = 30% + 20% = 50% total, whereas 竜 x 2 = 35%.

Minotaur's Axe vs. Type Effective Weapons

The quick answer is that it comes down to play style and opinion, rather than one being strictly better.
Type effective seals are guaranteed to deal more damage against a selection of monsters,
while the 会 seal has the potential to deal more damage against all monsters.

Minotaur's Axe
  • (+) Increase damage against all monsters.
  • (+) With enough luck, you can land critical hits without many 会 seals.
  • (-) Chance is based on the number of 会 seals. 14 seals = 100%, but it's likely not worth that amount of investment.
  • (-) Minotaur and Megataur only appear toward the end of post-game dungeons, so it's difficult to obtain.
Type Effective (Ghost Sickle, Drain Buster, Cyclops Killer, Crescent Arm, Dragon Killer, Ryujin Sword)
  • (+) Guaranteed to increase damage against a selection of monsters.
  • (+) One seal is enough to start seeing effective results.
  • (-) Some monsters like Kengo cannot be targeted by these seals, giving an advantage to the 会 seal.
  • (-) Each selection of monsters takes up its own seal slot that could be used for other seals.
  • (-) The seal isn't useful on floors where strong monsters of the applicable type don't appear.

Reflect Shield vs. Dodge Shield

These two shields appear to be similar at first glance, but have a number of differences.
Let's take a look at the pros and cons.

Dodge Shield
  • (+) Dodge all projectiles, and allows you to collect items after dodging them.
  • (+) Can dodge piercing projectiles such as Silver Arrows.
  • (-) Cannot dodge Cannonballs.
  • (-) Cannot dodge thrown monsters, possibly leading to blocked exits in situations such as a Power House.
  • (-) Traps activate if you're standing on one when the item falls on the ground after dodging.
         Particularly dangerous if there's a Strip or Sleep Trap on the ground beneath you.
Reflect Shield
  • (+) Reflect most projectiles, including items and monsters.
         Monsters thrown by Tiger Tosser monsters get reflected, meaning exits can't get blocked.
  • (+) Can reflect Cannonballs, though the explosion can still damage you.
  • (-) Reflected items vanish when they hit enemies, so it's not possible to collect items.
         It's fine if you don't need the item, but can be a problem if a Kappa monster throws your equipment
         after getting disarmed by a Kengo family monster, especially inside Sealed Room.
  • (-) Cannot dodge piercing projectiles, including Silver Arrows and items under the effect of a Pierce Statue.
  • (-) Shopkeepers and Traders will attack you if a reflected Cannonball's explosion damages them.
  • (-) Cannot reflect items that have already been reflected once in the same turn.
  • (-) Slows down the pace of the game.

Neither shield can block objects falling from above.
Sources of objects falling from above include Droplet, Burrs, Rockfall, and Rockslide traps.
Though none of those deal much damage except for Rockslide Trap, and it's possible to prevent
traps from activating by using the カ seal from a Traproid Shield, so it's not really a big deal.

If a shield has both 身 and 返 seals, you'll be able to dodge piercing items while reflecting everything else.

All that said, the question remains, if you only synthesize one, which is better?

Reflect Shield allows you to evade a greater number of things, since it can reflect both items and monsters.
Not being able to collect dodged items isn't that important for post-game dungeons, since by the time you
obtain a Reflect Shield, you'll likely only be interested in things that cannot be found on the ground.
You can also read a Slumber Scroll to prevent a Kappa monster from throwing items,
and you always need to act carefully around Kengo monsters to avoid having your shield disarmed.
Lastly, Silver Arrows from Horse Ronin monsters don't deal much damage if your shield is strong,
so you only really need to watch out for Pierce Statues.

Dodge Shield allows you to collect items thrown by Kappa and Water Strider monsters,
and also lets you collect Wooden and Iron arrows from Bow Boy family monsters.
Dodged items can trigger traps if you're standing on one, but that situation is rare, so it's not a big concern.
The biggest drawback is that you can't dodge Wolf Droid bombs or Pop Tank cannonballs,
meaning there's a significant risk for bracelets cracking compared to Reflect Shield.

In the end, the answer is that Reflect Shield is used more often, but it comes down to what you value more.
