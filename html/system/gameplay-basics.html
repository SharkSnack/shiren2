Dungeon Related

Turns

Turns and Turn-Ending Actions

Being a turn-based roguelike, Shiren and monsters take turns when performing actions in this game.
Generally, turn-ending actions include movement, attacks, using an item, and stepping in place.
Your turn does not end from idling, checking item details, or otherwise navigating menus.
The following actions are easy to forget / mistake:

  • Equipping a weapon, shield, or bracelet does end the turn, but equipping a projectile does not.
  • Talking to an ally character does not end the turn.
  • Talking to a Shopkeeper or Trader does end the turn.
  • Placing a trap on the ground does not end the turn.
  • Failing to place an item on the ground does not end the turn.
  • Successfully taking control of a monster using a Possession Staff does not end the turn.
  • Cancelling a possession and returning to your body does not end the turn.
Turns Elapsing

Various effects or events occur based on the number of turns that have elapsed.
Try your best to not waste turns with meaningless actions.

  • HP regenerates every turn at a rate of 0.5% of your max HP. (decimals accumulate)
  • Fullness decreases by 1% every 10 turns.
  • A new monster is generated every 50 turns.
  • A Sheriff or Guard Dog is generated once per turn during Thief Mode.
  • Wind sweeps you out of the dungeon when 2100 turns have elapsed.
Turn Processing Order
Shiren→Fire weakening→Enemy movement→Traps→Fire damage (move)→Enemy attack→Fire damage→Turn End

Enemies standing on a tile that is on fire who received fire damage due to an action by Shiren
will not take additional fire damage on the same turn unless they move to a different tile that is on fire.

Characters other than Shiren with double or triple movement speed who receive fire damage
by moving to a tile that is on fire will end their turn when they receive the fire damage.

Wind

If you linger on the same floor inside of a dungeon for a long time, a strong wind begins to blow.
(Also occurs inside the Training Hall and Napus Storage)

The gusts happen 3 times, and Shiren is swept back to Napus Village on the third gust of wind.
Being swept away by wind is equivalent to collapsing, so you'll lose all items and Gitan.
Asuka and Mamo's items will not be lost, and items on the floor inside Napus Storage will be safe.

  • First gust of wind occurs on turn 1500
  • Second gust of wind occurs on turn 1800
  • Third gust of wind occurs on turn 2100

Recollections that play after resuming a suspended game will include wind in the playback,
so you can use that to check how many gusts of wind occurred.

Dungeon Types

There are 2 types of dungeons, which are called "Shuffle Dungeon" and "Random Dungeon".

Shuffle Dungeon
  • The map for the floor is randomly selected from a set of predefined patterns.
    • The same pattern will never be repeated during an adventure.
    • Boss floors such as Shuten Trail Hard 15F only have one pattern.
  • The maps are generally very pretty, and have many fine details depicting nature.
    • It's thought that Shiren 2 is among the most detailed games made for the Nintendo 64.
    • Hallways and rooms vary in shape and feel more natural compared to lines and rectangles seen elsewhere.
  • Monster Houses and dungeon shops will never be generated. (Traders can still appear)
  • Some item and monster special attack effects are nullified in this type of map.
    • Any effect that alters the shape of the map is nullified.
      (Pickaxe, Wonder Pickaxe, Tunnel Staff, Wall Collapse Scroll, Rock Beast, Boulder Beast, explosions)
    • Nothing happens to the water on the current floor when Evaporate Scroll or Freeze Scroll is read.
    • Passing through walls is not allowed. (Pumphantasm family, Typhoon, Phantasm Bracelet)
    • Note that items with piercing properties still pierce through objects and walls. (Pierce Bracelet, etc.)
  • Nothing happens when a Flood Trap activates, and Pitfalls deal damage without changing floors.
    • These traps are never generated in this map type, but can be brought in and set using a Trapper Bracelet.
    • Shuten Trail Mid 12F is the only exception, where falling in a Pitfall returns you to 11F.
  • There's a glitch that hard-locks the game in Shuffle Dungeon maps. →Glitches
  • This dungeon type occurs only on select floors of Shuten Trails, boss floors of Onigashima, and Golden Intervals.
    • In other words, Shuffle Dungeons are rarely seen in post-game dungeons.

Dungeons: Small Valley, Shuten Easy (1~4F, 7F, 9~10F), Shuten Mid (3~4F, 6~7F, 9~13F),
Shuten Hard (1~2F, 10~15F), Golden Interval, Onigashima (16F, 25F)

Random Dungeon
  • There are no predefined patterns, and each floor is generated completely randomly.
  • Every tile is a ground, water, ice, wall, or unbreakable wall tile. There are no hole / bottomless pit tiles.
    • Rooms can have features such as water or ice, single column wall tiles spaced throughout the room, etc.
    • Other features include small islands within a room, maze-like rooms with only a 1 tile radius of view, etc.
  • Waterways can be generated, which sometimes have a similar appearance to hallways.
  • Monster Houses, dungeon shops, and Traders have a chance of being generated.
  • Golden Stairs that lead to Golden Intervals can rarely be found hidden in a wall tile in this dungeon type.
  • All dungeons starting with Onigashima are Random Dungeons, and have a limited field of view.

Dungeons: Shuten Easy (5~6F, 8F), Shuten Mid (1~2F, 5F, 8F), Shuten Hard (3~9F),
Onigashima, Shrine's Hidden Hole, Halfway Well, Sealed Room, Road to the End

Field of View

  • Shiren has a set distance he can see called field of view.
    • The view radius is 1 tile in hallways, and is the entire room + 1 tile along the edge of the room inside rooms.
    • The location of items, monsters, and statues are displayed on the map if they are within Shiren's view.
    • Single room floors and some Shuffle Dungeon maps don't differentiate between rooms and hallways,
      and instead have a 5 tile field of view (within a 11 tile radius).
    • You can still see past Shiren's field of view in Shuffle Dungeons, but not in darker dungeons such as Onigashima.
  • Monsters also have their own field of view.
    • They'll attack Shiren if he is visible, and will chase after him into the same hallway if they see him enter one.
    • They can seemingly see Shiren from 2 tiles away in hallways, but they won't use their special attack from that distance.
      • Monsters in a room will also move toward Shiren if he is standing on a tile right outside the room.
    • Monsters that can pass through walls can see Shiren from anywhere on the current floor.
    • Some monsters can use special attacks toward Shiren or other monsters even if they aren't in view,
      such as Porky throwing stones at Shiren, or Bat Kangaroo swinging its staff at other monsters.
  • Note that statues and items like scrolls that affect the entire room don't include the tile right outside of the room.
    • Except for Rock (castle material) items, which can hit monsters right outside of the room.

Dungeon Mechanics

Shops

Occasionally generated in Random Dungeons.

  • Shops can be generated in Shuten Trail Mid, Shuten Trail Hard, Onigashima, Halfway Well, and Road to the End.
    • However, shops will never be generated on Shuffle Dungeon type floors.
  • A maximum number of 2 shops can be generated on a floor.
  • Items are placed in the center of the room as merchandise.
    • There are typically 9 items in a shop, but smaller shops can have fewer items.
    • Be warned that items for sale might include a N'dubba disguised as an item.
  • Traps can be generated inside a shop, including a Spring that may lead to accidentally triggering Thief Mode.

Score (Leaderboard)

Score is calculated when you either clear a dungeon, or collapse and return from a dungeon.
The leaderboard keeps track of 30 placements per dungeon.

Score Formula
The dungeon must be cleared for selling price of items and clear bonus to apply.
Score = (Floor - 1) * 5000 + (Gitan) + (selling price of items) + (clear bonus)
Clear Bonus
  • Shuten Trails, Onigashima, Road to the End
5,000
  • Sealed Room
    Note that you won't be able to obtain the clear bonus once all 5 children have been rescued.
    The floor number you rescue a child on does not affect the clear bonus.
450,000
Floor = 20 in the score formula when Sealed Room is cleared.
  • Halfway Well
500,000
Floor = 10 in the score formula when Halfway Well is cleared.
