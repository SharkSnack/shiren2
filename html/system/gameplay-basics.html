Dungeon Related

Turns

Turns and Turn-Ending Actions

Being a turn-based roguelike, Shiren and monsters take turns when performing actions in this game.
Generally, turn-ending actions include movement, attacks, using an item, and stepping in place.
Your turn does not end from idling, checking item details, or otherwise navigating menus.
The following actions are easy to forget / mistake:

  • Equipping a weapon, shield, or bracelet does end the turn, but equipping a projectile does not.
  • Talking to an ally character does not end the turn.
  • Talking to a Shopkeeper or Trader does end the turn.
  • Placing a trap on the ground does not end the turn.
  • Failing to place an item on the ground does not end the turn.
  • Successfully taking control of a monster using a Possession Staff does not end the turn.
  • Cancelling a possession and returning to your body does not end the turn.
Turns Elapsing

Various effects or events occur based on the number of turns that have elapsed.
Try your best to not waste turns with meaningless actions.

  • HP regenerates every turn at a rate of 0.5% of your max HP. (decimals accumulate)
  • Fullness decreases by 1% every 10 turns.
  • A new monster is generated every 50 turns.
  • A Sheriff or Guard Dog is generated once per turn during Thief Mode.
  • Wind sweeps you out of the dungeon when 2100 turns have elapsed.
Turn Processing Order
Shiren→Fire weakening→Enemy movement→Traps→Fire damage (move)→Enemy attack→Fire damage→Turn End

Enemies standing on a tile that is on fire who received fire damage due to an action by Shiren
will not take additional fire damage on the same turn unless they move to a different tile that is on fire.

Characters other than Shiren with double or triple movement speed who receive fire damage
by moving to a tile that is on fire will end their turn when they receive the fire damage.

Wind

If you linger on the same floor inside of a dungeon for a long time, a strong wind begins to blow.
(Also occurs inside the Training Hall and Napus Storage)

The gusts happen 3 times, and Shiren is swept back to Napus Village on the third gust of wind.
Being swept away by wind is equivalent to collapsing, so you'll lose all items and Gitan.
Asuka and Mamo's items will not be lost, and items on the floor inside Napus Storage will be safe.

  • First gust of wind occurs on turn 1500
  • Second gust of wind occurs on turn 1800
  • Third gust of wind occurs on turn 2100

Recollections that play after resuming a suspended game will include wind in the playback,
so you can use that to check how many gusts of wind occurred.

Dungeon Types

There are 2 types of dungeons, which are called "Shuffle Dungeon" and "Random Dungeon".

Shuffle Dungeon
  • The map for the floor is randomly selected from a set of predefined patterns.
    • The same pattern will never be repeated during an adventure.
    • Boss floors such as Shuten Trail Hard 15F only have one pattern.
  • The maps are generally very pretty, and have many fine details depicting nature.
    • It's thought that Shiren 2 is among the most detailed games made for the Nintendo 64.
    • Hallways and rooms vary in shape and feel more natural compared to lines and rectangles seen elsewhere.
  • Monster Houses and dungeon shops will never be generated. (Traders can still appear)
  • Some item and monster special attack effects are nullified in this type of map.
    • Any effect that alters the shape of the map is nullified.
      (Pickaxe, Wonder Pickaxe, Tunnel Staff, Wall Collapse Scroll, Rock Beast, Boulder Beast, explosions)
    • Nothing happens to the water on the current floor when Evaporate Scroll or Freeze Scroll is read.
    • Passing through walls is not allowed. (Pumphantasm family, Typhoon, Phantasm Bracelet)
    • Note that items with piercing properties still pierce through objects and walls. (Pierce Bracelet, etc.)
  • Nothing happens when a Flood Trap activates, and Pitfalls deal damage without changing floors.
    • These traps are never generated in this map type, but can be brought in and set using a Trapper Bracelet.
    • Shuten Trail Mid 12F is the only exception, where falling in a Pitfall returns you to 11F.
  • There's a glitch that hard-locks the game in Shuffle Dungeon maps. →Glitches
  • This dungeon type occurs only on select floors of Shuten Trails, boss floors of Onigashima, and Golden Intervals.
    • In other words, Shuffle Dungeons are rarely seen in post-game dungeons.

Dungeons: Small Valley, Shuten Easy (1~4F, 7F, 9~10F), Shuten Mid (3~4F, 6~7F, 9~13F),
Shuten Hard (1~2F, 10~15F), Golden Interval, Onigashima (16F, 25F)

Random Dungeon
  • There are no predefined patterns, and each floor is generated completely randomly.
  • Every tile is a ground, water, ice, wall, or unbreakable wall tile. There are no hole / bottomless pit tiles.
    • Rooms can have features such as water or ice, single column wall tiles spaced throughout the room, etc.
    • Other features include small islands within a room, maze-like rooms with only a 1 tile radius of view, etc.
  • Waterways can be generated, which sometimes have a similar appearance to hallways.
  • Monster Houses, dungeon shops, and Traders have a chance of being generated.
  • Golden Stairs that lead to Golden Intervals can rarely be found hidden in a wall tile in this dungeon type.
  • All dungeons starting with Onigashima are Random Dungeons, and have a limited field of view.

Dungeons: Shuten Easy (5~6F, 8F), Shuten Mid (1~2F, 5F, 8F), Shuten Hard (3~9F),
Onigashima, Shrine's Hidden Hole, Halfway Well, Sealed Room, Road to the End

Field of View

  • Shiren has a set distance he can see called field of view.
    • The view radius is 1 tile in hallways, and is the entire room + 1 tile along the edge of the room inside rooms.
    • The location of items, monsters, and statues are displayed on the map if they are within Shiren's view.
    • Single room floors and some Shuffle Dungeon maps don't differentiate between rooms and hallways,
      and instead have a 5 tile field of view (within a 11 tile radius).
    • You can still see past Shiren's field of view in Shuffle Dungeons, but not in darker dungeons such as Onigashima.
  • Monsters also have their own field of view.
    • They'll attack Shiren if he is visible, and will chase after him into the same hallway if they see him enter one.
    • They can seemingly see Shiren from 2 tiles away in hallways, but they won't use their special attack from that distance.
      • Monsters in a room will also move toward Shiren if he is standing on a tile right outside the room.
    • Monsters that can pass through walls can see Shiren from anywhere on the current floor.
    • Some monsters can use special attacks toward Shiren or other monsters even if they aren't in view,
      such as Porky throwing stones at Shiren, or Bat Kangaroo swinging its staff at other monsters.
  • Note that statues and items like scrolls that affect the entire room don't include the tile right outside of the room.
    • Except for Rock (castle material) items, which can hit monsters right outside of the room.

Dungeon Mechanics

Shops

Shops are occasionally generated in Random Dungeons.

  • Shops can be generated in Shuten Trail Mid, Shuten Trail Hard, Onigashima, Halfway Well, and Road to the End.
    • However, shops will never be generated on Shuffle Dungeon type floors.
  • A maximum number of 2 shops can be generated on a floor.
  • Items are placed in the center of the room as merchandise.
    • There are typically 9 items in a shop, but smaller shops can have fewer items.
    • Be warned that items for sale might include a N'dubba disguised as an item.
  • Traps can be generated inside shops, so be careful of Springs in particular.
  • There are either 1 or 2 entrances to a shop, and Shopkeepers always stands at the entrance.
  • Items cannot be placed on the ground where Shopkeepers stand, including the entrance.
    • Droplet Ninja family monsters will never place traps on a shop's entrance tile when defeated.
  • The individual items for sale vary, and are based on each dungeon's item table.
  • Two types of shops can be generated - regular shops, and specialty shops.
    • Specialty shops only sell one category of item, such as only grass items, or only bracelets.
  • Shopkeepers block the exit when Shiren picks up merchandise, and ask for Gitan when you talk to them.
    • Pay the amount of Gitan they ask for, and the item will become yours.
  • Exiting the shop without paying for the item will trigger Thief Mode.
    • For details about stealing, check out the Stealing Techniques page.
  • If you want to sell items, simply place them on the ground.
    • Shopkeepers will ask if you want to sell the items you placed when you talk to them, so select [Yes] to sell the items.
  • Shopkeepers will fight back if attacked by Shiren, but defeating a Shopkeeper will not trigger Thief Mode.
    • They will also attack monsters that deal damage to them, and will level up when they defeat monsters.
  • Merchandise can be refreshed by reading a Restock Scroll.
    • All merchandise on the ground vanishes, and new merchandise is generated in the center of the room.
    • Refreshing always generates 9 items (or fewer based on room size), no matter how many items were on the ground.
    • Merchandise in specialty shops will still only sell the same category of items.

Walls

Walls can be found in Random Dungeons.
They can be broken using items like Pickaxe and Tunnel Staff.
Other things like monster special attacks and explosions can also break walls,
but explosions that occur above water will not break walls.

Characters that can pass through walls
Cannot dig using a Pickaxe while inside a wall, but a Tunnel Staff can be used while inside a wall.
Cannot throw items or roll Cannonballs at a wall while inside a wall.
However, you can throw items or roll Cannonballs if you have a Pierce Bracelet equipped.
Characters that don't have the ability to wall-clip will warp elsewhere if they enter a wall.
You still receive damage from explosions and Archdragon's fire breath inside walls.
Items that arc will still be able to hit you inside walls.

Unbreakable Walls

Unbreakable walls can be found in select Random Dungeons.
They cannot be broken by items like Pickaxe and Tunnel Staff,
and they cannot be broken by monster special attacks or explosions.
The only way to break unbreakable walls is to read a Wall Collapse Scroll.

Characters that can pass through walls
Cannot enter unbreakable walls, even with the wall-clip property. (ex: Phantasm Bracelet)
Cannot move through corners of unbreakable walls.
However, you can attack around corners of unbreakable walls.
But Typhoon and Asuka with a Phantasm Bracelet won't attack around corners.

Waterways

Waterways can be found in select Random Dungeons and Shuffle Dungeons.
Unlike walls, you can move and attack through corners.
Items can fall in water, with a common cause being stepping on a Trip Stone.
Submerged items cannot be picked up even with a Phantasm Bracelet equipped,
and magic from staves will simply travel above the item.
Waterways can be destroyed by reading an Evaporate Scroll or Wall Collapse Scroll.

Frozen Floors

Frozen floors can be found in select Random Dungeons.
They can be generated naturally, or by reading a Freeze Scroll on a floor with waterways.
Characters will slide up to 4 tiles when they move across frozen floors.
The sliding will stop if the character moves on top of an item or trap.
Disguised N'dubbas can be picked up as an item if Shiren was 2~4 tiles away before sliding to it.
Monsters with the Floating element can move across frozen floors without sliding.

Stairs cannot be moved onto frozen floors.

Frozen floor tiles will turn into regular ground tiles when a Wall Collapse Scroll is read.

Fire can be generated on top of frozen floors, and the frozen floor does not melt. (ex: Fire Staff)
Lastly, monsters that are frozen in ice cannot be damaged by fire.

Maze Rooms

Maze rooms can be found in select Random Dungeons.
The room resembles a maze and consists of branching hallways.
Shiren 2 doesn't generate floor-wide mazes, so maze rooms are usually rather small.
Items, traps, and stairs can be generated within the hallways of a maze room, unlike regular hallways.
Field of view remains the same as hallways, and Monsters can only detect Shiren if they are next to him.

Treasure Rooms

Treasure rooms can be found in select Random Dungeons.
These 4x4 rooms do not have an entrance or exit, and include 15 bags of 500 Gitan + 1 Spring.
You'll likely never enter one during regular play, and they can be tricky to find to begin with.
They can be found by using a Scout Bracelet, or by the Scout Bracelet-like effect of Monster Houses.
You'll need items like Wall Collapse Scroll, Tunnel Staff, Phantasm Bracelet, etc. to enter the room.

They are extremely rare, only having a 1% chance of being generated on certain Random Dungeon map types.

Random Dungeon Map Types

1 Large Room

The entire floor is one large room. Shops are never generated in this map type.
Rectangular walls or waterways are generated in the room.
Both Shiren and monsters have a field of view of 5 tiles (11 tile radius).
Scrolls with effects that normally affect the entire room will only affect the field of view.
Statues will only affect tiles in a 11 tile radius, centered around the statue.
It can take a little while to find the stairs, thanks to the limited field of view.

iDash can be used to navigate to items or stairs without regard to the field of view,
but it's not recommended due to the risk of running into or getting surrounded by monsters.
Unlike other games in the series, this map type is not guaranteed to be a Monster House.
Underwater Houses will never be generated when walls are present.
Shiren 2 doesn't have regular Monster Houses in this map type.

1 Medium Room

The entire floor is one medium sized room.
Walls and waterways are never generated, but statues can be present.
Like single large rooms, the field of view is limited to 5 tiles (11 tile radius).
Due to the smaller size, this map type often ends up being more dangerous than
single large rooms, since there's a greater chance of encountering multiple monsters.

The outer wall can be broken using items like Tunnel Staff and Pickaxe,
so one strategy would be to create a tunnel and then swing a Fire Staff
or shoot Silver Arrows to clear out lines of enemies that follow you.
Unlike other games in the series, this map type is not guaranteed to be a Monster House.

1 Large Frozen Room

The entire floor is one large room with frozen floors.
Monster Houses and shops will never be generated in this map type.
Walls and waterways are never generated, but statues can be present.
Like single large rooms, the field of view is limited to 5 tiles (11 tile radius).
It's easy to get caught in traps on these floors due to all of the sliding on ice.

2 Rooms

The entire floor is two large rectangular rooms connected by a single hallway.
It's common for the hallway to have turns, rather than being a straight horizontal line.
Monster Houses are not guaranteed, and shops will never be generated.
The field of view is not limited, so scrolls and statues affect everything in the room.

4 Rooms

Four rectangular rooms connected by hallways in a 2x2 layout.
Monster Houses are not guaranteed, and shops will never be generated.

2 Rooms (Dumbbell Shape)

The entire floor is two circular rooms connected by a single horizontal hallway.
Monster Houses and shops will never be generated.
Items and monsters are generated in the opposite room from where Shiren starts.
The start of the floor is safe, but monsters quickly flow in from the opposite room.
Items like Fire Staff and Silver Arrows can make quick work of monsters on the floor.

4 Rooms (2 Hallways)

The entire floor is four rooms placed in cardinal directions connected by 2 hallways.
Shops will never be generated in this map type.
Each room has four entrances, and each set of two hallways leads to the next room.
It's more efficient in terms of number of turns and fullness depletion
to explore the floor using the inner hallways rather than the outer hallways.

8 Rooms

The entire floor is 8 rooms in a 4x2 layout, with one hallway going around the outside.
It's best to explore the floor using the inner hallways when possible,
since monsters tend to end up circling the floor in the outer hallway.
Unlike previous games, this map type doesn't have a greater chance of generating shops.

12 Rooms

The entire floor is a hallway grid layout with a total of 12 rooms placed on the end points.
It can take quite a few turns to explore the floor due to the number of rooms,
combined with having to return to the hallways after checking each end point.
Consider using a Pickaxe or Tunnel Staff to create shortcuts between rooms.
Unlike previous games, this map type doesn't have a greater chance of generating shops.

1 Medium Room + Small Rooms (Octopus Shape)

The entire floor is one medium sized room with individual hallways leading to single rooms.
Also called "Crab Shape", "Sun Shape", "Radial Shape", and "Octopus Shape".
Expect to encounter many monsters due to having to return to the center after checking each room.
Be careful not to get surrounded at the start, since monsters will quickly flow into the center room.
Particularly tricky when monsters with ranged attacks are present, such as Sky Dragon.

Single Branched Rooms

The entire floor consists of rooms connected by one hallway between each room.
(The number of hallways = number of rooms - 1).
Each branch eventually leads to a room as a dead end, so it's common to encounter monsters.
However, on rare occasions hallways can end up looping or having branches (such as a cross-shape).
One of the most common map types, with Road to the End having 30 floors of either this or single path layouts.

Single Path

The entire floor consists of a single path between rooms without any branching hallways.
(The number of hallways = number of rooms - 1).
Expect to encounter every monster on the floor, often having to fight multiple in a row.

Item and Character Limits

There is a maximum number of items and characters that can be present on a floor.

Item limit
The max number of items (including both inventory and the ground) is 160, including traps.
Monsters will no longer drop new items or traps when the number of items is at the limit.
Actions that increase the number of items are nullified, such as shooting an arrow from a stack [99 arrows → 98 + 1].
Some monsters like Bow Boy won't be able to use their special attack.
A monster inside of a Monster Pot is counted as an item, so Monster Pots cannot be thrown at monsters.
Captured monsters won't be able to return to their pot if the item limit is reached while they're out.
Items on the ground inside Napus Storage will begin to vanish if there are 161 or more items
between your inventory and the number of items on the ground.

Character limit
The max number of characters is 30, and includes Shiren, monsters, allies, NPCs, statues, decoys, and figures.
Monsters will no longer be generated, and items like Decoy Scroll and Soil will not generate shadows or figures.
Ally monsters also cannot be sent out of their pots when the character limit is reached.

Score (Leaderboard)

Score is calculated when you either clear a dungeon, or collapse and return from a dungeon.
The leaderboard keeps track of 30 placements per dungeon.

Score Formula
The dungeon must be cleared for selling price of items and clear bonus to apply.
Score = (Floor - 1) * 5000 + (Gitan) + (selling price of items) + (clear bonus)
Clear Bonus
  • Shuten Trails, Onigashima, Road to the End
5,000
  • Sealed Room
    Note that you won't be able to obtain the clear bonus once all 5 children have been rescued.
    The floor number you rescue a child on does not affect the clear bonus.
450,000
Floor = 20 in the score formula when Sealed Room is cleared.
  • Halfway Well
500,000
Floor = 10 in the score formula when Halfway Well is cleared.

Item Related

Synthesis

Synthesis is a mechanic that allows you to modify one item using another.

  • The item to be modified is called the base, and the secondary item to be consumed is called the ingredient.
  • In a broad sense, the base can also be considered an ingredient.
  • The first item inserted into a Synthesis Pot or thrown at a Mixer becomes the base,
    and items thrown afterward are the ingredients.
  • Generally, the item used as a base will not be lost or transformed.
    • However, items used as ingredients will be consumed and lost.
  • Weapons, shields, and staves are the three item categories that can act as a base.
    • Ingredients must be the same category as the base item when using a Synthesis Pot.
Staff Synthesis
Only staves with identical names can be synthesized together.
Synthesizing staves will add the number of uses together, and will also identify the staff.
Weapon, Shield Synthesis
  • Synthesize weapons with weapons, or shields with shields.
  • Upgrade value (ex: +1) and special abilities (seals) will be added/transferred to the base item.
    • Seals are depicted as a single Japanese character.
  • The max upgrade value for all weapons and shields is +99.
  • Weapons and shields have a limited number of seal slots.
    • Unlike other games in the series, you cannot fit every seal in the game into one piece of equipment.
  • Each piece of equipment comes with a set number of slots, but the number of slots can be increased.
    • The max number of slots is 16.
  • Plating (金) and curse (呪) are counted as seals in Shiren 2.
  • Some two-handed weapons and shields can only be used as a base in synthesis.
  • Things that affect seals:
    Synthesis Pot, Plating Scroll, Purify Scroll, Mixer family, Straw Voodoll family, Monsoon Doll,
    Gyandora, the Blacksmith at Shuten Halfway, and the 帰 seal.
  • Things that affect seal slots:
    Blank Scroll, Mixer family, Gyandora.
Mixer Synthesis
  • Synthesize using different category items as ingredients (ex: grasses, scrolls) when equipment is used as a base.
  • Different category items can only be synthesized by throwing items at a Mixer family monster.
    • To be clear, you cannot synthesize different category items together using a Synthesis Pot.
  • List of ingredients and resulting seals:
    Dragon Grass(火), Flame Grass(火), Otogiriso(弟), Herb(薬), Life Grass(命), Antidote Grass(消 - shield only),
    Strength Grass(ち - weapon only), Happy Grass(幸), Angel Seed(天), Unlucky Seed(不, 呪), Disaster Seed(超, 呪),
    Slumber Scroll(眠), Purify Scroll(祓), Identify Scroll(識), Retreat Scroll(帰), Blank Scroll(seal slot +1),
    Riceball(飯), Large Riceball(飯), Giant Riceball(飯), Specialty Riceball(飯), Silver Arrow(銀), Dragon Tile Bracelet(中).
Seal Order
Seals will be added in the same order as the order of ingredients.
If an ingredient has an innate special ability, that ability will be added before the other seals.
  • Example 1: Katana (三回○○○) followed by Drain Buster+2 (目○○○○) results in a Katana+2 (三回ド目○).
  • Example 2: Katana (三回○○○) followed by Drain Buster+2 (目竜月火○) results in a Katana+2 (三回ド目竜).
    • The seals toward the end (月 and 火) are lost because the number of seals exceeds the number of slots.
    • So remember, seals are added starting from the left, and innate seals are added before other seals.
  • Example 3: Katana+2 (三回ド目○) followed by Homing Blade-1 (呪○○) results in a Katana+1 (三回ド目必).
    • Homing Blade's innate ability (必) was added first, so the curse (呪) didn't transfer.
    • Note that the + and - values were added together, resulting in -1 upgrade value.
    • Remember this in case you come across cursed equipment and want to transfer the innate ability but not the curse.
  • Example 4: Katana+7 (三回ド目必) followed by Disposable Sword+3 (○○○) results in a Katana+10 (三回ド目必).
    • Disposable Sword's innate ability (捨) was not transferred, because the Katana's slots were already filled.
    • So as long as the base equipment's slots are filled, you can add upgrade values without adding undesirable seals.
Special Synthesis
If you synthesize specific seals onto certain pieces of equipment, the item will be transformed into a new item.
Think of these as secret recipes to create powerful equipment.
  • Weapons:
    Kaburasutegi, Daisangen Sword, Fiery Fuuma, Healing Sword, Wonder Pickaxe, Ryujin Sword.
  • Shields:
    Helix Shield, Daisangen Shield, Nirvana Shield.

　※ Check the the item and seal pages for details regarding the items and seals mentioned in this section.
　　 Special synthesis recipes are also listed in the item pages.

Durability

Weapon Durability

All weapons have are assigned a random durability value of 15~60.
Digging using a Pickaxe's ability or destroying traps using a Mallet's ability lowers durability by 1,
and the weapon breaks once durability reaches 0.
When synthesizing weapons, the durability of the base weapon is unchanged.

Bracelet Durability

Bracelets can sometimes crack when you receive damage while they're equipped.
The purchasing and selling price of a cracked bracelet remains the same.

The chance for a bracelet to crack is ~3%, except for the Trapper Bracelet which has a ~10% chance.
Bracelets other than Trapper Bracelet also have a ~9% chance to crack from explosions.

Once a bracelet cracks, a durability value of 5~15 is set, and the bracelet shatters once it reaches 0.
Durability decreases by 1 when you're hit by an attack in the "Actions that can crack or shatter" list below.

So basically, cracks are based on luck, and can occur as early as the first hit after equipping a bracelet.
And a cracked bracelet will never immediately shatter, but will after a little while of receiving attacks.
Note that this applies to bracelets equipped by Asuka as well.

Actions that can crack or shatter
  • Monster regular attacks.
  • Brutal hits from Taurus monsters.
  • Ouch Statue's attacks.
Actions that can crack
  • Body slams by Stone Beast monsters (?)
  • Tripping (Trip Stone, Armordillo monsters, Balance Staff)
  • Explosions (Cannonballs, landmines, Patient Bag explosions, Bomber and Explosion statues)
  • Log, Burr, Rockfall, and Rockslide traps.
Actions that never crack or shatter
  • Getting hit by arrows, stones, or other thrown items.
  • Special attacks or traps that don't deal damage. (ex: ones that inflict status conditions).
  • Getting sprayed by water from a Shotfish monster.
  • Fire or ice breath attacks.
  • Non-physical ranged attacks. (ex: staff magic).
  • Droplet Trap, Flood Trap

So, there's no need to unequip bracelets when you're fighting Bow Boy or Boy Cart.
They also don't need to be unequipped when you're up against Archdragon or Porkton's special attacks.
And if your bracelets are already cracked, they don't need to be unequipped when fighting Cranky Tank.

Ally Items (Asuka, Mamo)

Asuka's equipment and items stored inside Mamo's drawers generally carry forward between adventures.
They will both drop their items if they collapse inside a dungeon.
However, there are a few cases where Asuka and Mamo's items will vanish:

  • Selecting [Return to Napus Village] from the main menu.
  • Copying a journal. (Asuka and Mamo's items will vanish in the copy).
  • Certain in-game events.

It's best to store important items in Napus Storage, rather than having Asuka or Mamo carry them.

Status

Level, Growth

Shiren, Ally Level

Shiren and his allies start at level 1, and will level up after gaining a certain amount of experience points.
They can reach a max level of 99, but their level resets each time they start a new adventure.
Some items and monsters have the ability to decrease level, and the minimum level is 1.
Leveling up increases max HP and base attack, and HP is restored equal to the amount of max HP you gained.
Allies will also gain an increase in defense when they level up.

Shiren and his allies gain experience points at the same rate.
However, some items that modify level have different effects between Shiren and ally characters.
For example, swinging a Happy Staff at Shiren provides 500 experience points,
and swinging it at an ally will simply level them up.

See the Exp & Stats page for details on level up stats and required amount of experience points.

Enemy Monster Level

Monsters level up and turn into a higher rank monster if they defeat other monsters or Shiren's allies.
Monsters can also level up from items such as Happy Staff, Happy Grass, Angel Seed.

Ally Monster Growth

Ally monsters don't have experience points or level ups, but instead have what's called growth.
Growth occurs when an ally monster defeats an enemy monster, increasing both max HP and strength.
Every monster has a set growth limit, and the stat gains per growth differs between monsters.
Ally monsters will keep their growth between adventures, but growth will be halved if they are defeated.
(Growth is only halved for monsters registered to Monster Kingdom, since unregistered monsters are lost).

HP

Shiren, Ally HP

Shiren always starts with 15 HP at the beginning of an adventure, and max HP never falls below 15.
Max HP increases with each level up, and items can be used to increase max HP as well. (max: 999).
Max HP decreases if Shiren loses levels due to monster special attacks or item effects.
Note that Mamo starts with 100 HP, since it doesn't have the ability to gain levels.

Remaining HP regenerates at a rate equal to 0.5% of max HP per turn.
Decimals accumulate, and will be reflected in the remaining HP once the value reaches 1.
Some HP is restored after receiving an attack if you have the 薬 or 弟 seal synthesized onto your shield,
but note that the HP restoration occurs after the HP loss, so if the amount of damage surpasses
the amount of remaining HP, you'll collapse before the HP restoration occurs.

Monster HP

Max HP increases for ally monsters when they grow stronger, and items like Life Grass
can be used to increase max HP for both ally and enemy monsters. (max: 9999).
However, max HP increases from sources other than growth are temporary,
and will be reset when the ally monster returns to its pot.
Monsters do not regenerate HP, but ally monsters will be fully healed when they return to their pot.

Accuracy Modifiers

Some equipment abilities and special conditions can modify accuracy.

100% Accuracy
  • Regular attacks using a Homing Blade (必 seal).
  • Regular attacks against a paralyzed or sleeping target.
  • Shooting a projectile or throwing an item with a Bullseye Bracelet equipped.
0% Accuracy
  • Monster regular attacks while inflicted with the Miss status condition.
  • Regular attacks against a target that has a shield with 16 見 seals + 1 Paper Thin Bracelet equipped.
  • Shooting a projectile or throwing an item with a Dragon Tile Bracelet equipped.
Other
  • Regular attacks against a target that has a shield with 見 seals equipped. (Evasive Shield).
  • Regular attacks against a target with Paper Thin Bracelets equipped. (One = 1/2, Two = 1/4).
  • Regular attacks by Shiren or allies while inflicted with the Miss status condition. (1/2).
    Note that Homing Blade's effect (必 seal) will be nullified while inflicted with the Miss status condition.

Critical Hits・Brutal Hits

Critical hits are determined after accuracy is checked, so they aren't a factor for accuracy.
On the other hand, brutal hits are determined prior to the accuracy check, and are guaranteed to connect.
So Taurus family monsters effectively have higher accuracy than other monsters:

Brutal hit chance = 0.25
Regular attack accuracy = 0.875
Accuracy = 0.25 + 0.75 * 0.875 = 0.90625
